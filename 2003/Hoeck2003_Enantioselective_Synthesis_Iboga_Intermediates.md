# Enantioselective Synthesis of Key Intermediates in a Novel Approach towards the Iboga-Alkaloid Family

**Citation:** Höck, S. & Borschberg, H.-J. (2003). Enantioselective Synthesis of Key Intermediates in a Novel Approach towards the Iboga-Alkaloid Family. *Helvetica Chimica Acta*, 86, 1397–1409.

## Abstract

Significant improvements in a novel synthetic concept towards the iboga alkaloid family are presented. The key step for constructing the bicyclic aliphatic core consists of an intramolecular nitrone–olefin 1,3-dipolar cycloaddition reaction of a 1:1 mixture of nitrones **15**/**16**, yielding two diastereoisomeric tricyclic isoxazolidine derivatives **17** and **18**. The required nitrones were prepared from the readily available (*S*)-hydroxylactone **6** in twelve steps with an overall yield of 15% (average: 83.5% per step). The relative configuration of the minor isomer was deduced unambiguously by single-crystal X-ray analysis of the derived tricyclic carbamate **21**. As four out of five asymmetric centres in the pair **17**/**18** have opposite configuration, destruction of the one possessing the same absolute configuration transforms the original set of diastereoisomers into a pair of enantiomers. This contention was verified by oxidising the two alcohols **20** and **22** to yield the two antipodal forms of ketone **23**. The absence of significant amounts of by-product and the high reproducibility of the crucial cycloaddition reaction represent marked improvements over earlier attempts. The new route, starting from L-glutamate, provides access to both naturally occurring antipodal series of the targeted alkaloid class.

## Key Findings

This is a pure organic synthesis paper containing no biological, pharmacological, or clinical data. Its significance for ibogaine research is foundational — it develops improved methodology for constructing the isoquinuclidine core shared by all iboga alkaloids.

Key achievements:

- **Starting material:** L-Glutamic acid — an inexpensive, readily available chiral-pool building block — serves as the sole source of chirality, replacing the less accessible piperidine derivative (±)-**3** used in earlier work.
- **Key reaction:** Intramolecular nitrone–olefin 1,3-dipolar cycloaddition converts nitrones **15**/**16** into tricyclic isoxazolidines **17** and **18** in 58% combined yield with a 59:41 diastereomeric ratio.
- **Reproducibility:** The cycloaddition proceeds with high reproducibility and negligible by-product formation — a marked improvement over earlier attempts that suffered from poor yields and irreproducibility on scale-up.
- **Stereochemical proof:** X-ray crystallography of carbamate (−)-**21** unambiguously confirmed relative and absolute configuration. Oxidation of alcohols **20** and **22** to antipodal ketones (+)- and (−)-**23** demonstrated that both naturally occurring enantiomeric series of iboga alkaloids are accessible from a single starting material.

## Methodology

### Retrosynthetic Strategy (Scheme 1)

The iboga alkaloid general structure **A** is dissected into a tryptophyl unit **B** and an aliphatic isoquinuclidine core. The isoquinuclidine is accessed *via* isoxazolidine derivatives, which are formed by intramolecular nitrone–olefin 1,3-dipolar cycloaddition. In the improved route, the nitrone is generated by intramolecular condensation between a hydroxylamine and an aldehyde (structure **C**), rather than the earlier, less reliable amine-oxidation approach.

### Forward Synthesis (Schemes 2 and 3)

**Phase 1 — Chiral building block (Scheme 2, steps a–m):**

1. L-Glutamic acid ((*S*)-**4**) → (*S*)-hydroxylactone **6** *via* diazotisation and borane reduction (known methods).
2. **6** → trityl ether **7** → ring-opened, O-benzylated acid → Mitsunobu esterification with racemic but-3-en-2-ol → allylic ester **8** (1:1 diastereomeric mixture, 78.2% over 3 steps).
3. Selective trityl removal (ZnBr₂) → Swern oxidation → dimethyl acetal protection → acetal **9** (83.5% over 3 steps).
4. Ireland–Claisen rearrangement (LDA/TBSCl/HMPT) → *in situ* reduction (LiAlH₄) → primary alcohol **11** (82.4% over 2 steps).
5. Swern oxidation → oxime **13** (2:1 *E*/*Z* mixture, 95.6% over 2 steps) → NaBH₃CN reduction → hydroxylamine **14** (74.8%).

**Phase 2 — Key cycloaddition (Scheme 3, step a):**

Hydrolysis of the dimethyl acetal of **14** with 1.5 M H₂SO₄ in 1,4-dioxane at 60 °C generates the *cis*- and *trans*-nitrones **15** and **16** *in situ*. Over 24 hours, these undergo intramolecular 1,3-dipolar cycloaddition to give isoxazolidines **17** (major, *endo*-product from *cis*-nitrone) and **18** (minor) in 58% combined yield (59:41 ratio). The *cis*-nitrone **15** reacts faster and more efficiently.

**Phase 3 — Elaboration and stereochemical proof (Scheme 3, steps b–d):**

- Reductive N–O cleavage (Zn/AcOH) followed by carbamate formation (1,1′-carbonylbis[1*H*-imidazole]) → separable cyclic carbamates (−)-**19** (10.9%) and (−)-**21** (49.5%).
- Hydrogenolysis (H₂, Pd/C) of the benzyl groups → alcohols (−)-**20** and (−)-**22**.
- Swern oxidation of both alcohols → antipodal ketones (+)-**23** and (−)-**23**, confirming enantiomeric relationship and validating access to both natural antipodal series.
- Separation of **17**/**18** was also achieved by fractional crystallisation: seeding the mixture with crystals of minor isomer **18** enabled isolation of the more-valuable major isomer **17**, which possesses the absolute configuration of most naturally occurring iboga alkaloids.

## X-Ray Crystallographic Data

Single-crystal X-ray analysis of (−)-**21** (orthorhombic, space group *P*2₁2₁2₁, *a* = 10.1906 Å, *b* = 11.9127 Å, *c* = 12.4091 Å, *Z* = 4) established the relative configuration with the benzyloxy group in the *exo* position. Key structural features:

- The nitrogen atom cannot assume standard sp² geometry expected for a urethane derivative due to bridging in the tricyclic system; instead it sits as a flattened pyramid, ~0.4 Å above the plane defined by its three bonding partners C(2), C(10), and C(11).
- The 1,3-oxazin-2-one ring adopts a slightly deformed twist conformation.
- The isoquinuclidine system is deformed, with boat forms whose dihedral angles deviate considerably from idealised values.
- *R*(all) = 0.0872; *R*(gt) = 0.0528; *wR*(ref) = 0.1696. Crystallographic data deposited as CCDC 201931.
- Absolute configuration at C(9) assumed (*S*) based on derivation from L-glutamic acid.

## NMR Data Summary

Tables 1 and 2 provide complete ¹H- and ¹³C-NMR chemical shift values (CDCl₃, δ relative to SiMe₄) for compounds **1**, **17**–**23**. These data are fully consistent with the proposed structures and confirm the stereochemical assignments derived from the X-ray analysis of (−)-**21**. Notable diagnostic features include the methyl doublets of **17** (δ 1.15, *J* = 6.3 Hz) and **18** (δ 1.20, *J* = 6.3 Hz), and the carbonyl carbon of ketone **23** at δ 205.7 confirming successful oxidation.

## Clinical Implications

This paper has no direct clinical implications — it contains no biological, pharmacological, or clinical data. Its relevance to ibogaine research is indirect and foundational: it develops improved synthetic routes to the isoquinuclidine core shared by all iboga alkaloids. Such methodology is a prerequisite for total synthesis of ibogaine and its analogues, and for structure–activity relationship studies that may eventually yield clinically superior compounds with reduced cardiac liability.

The demonstration that both naturally occurring antipodal series are accessible from a single chiral-pool starting material (L-glutamic acid) is of strategic importance for medicinal chemistry programmes targeting iboga-type scaffolds.

## Limitations

- The synthesis terminates at advanced intermediates (tricyclic isoxazolidines/carbamates) — no iboga alkaloid natural product was completed in this work.
- Overall yield of 15% over 12 steps to the nitrone precursors, while efficient per step (83.5% average), limits practical scalability.
- The 59:41 diastereomeric ratio of the cycloaddition means nearly half the material produces the less-desired isomer **18**, though both isomers are synthetically useful.
- The paper explicitly notes this work represents a foundation for "numerous representatives of the iboga alkaloid family" — completion of total syntheses remains future work.
