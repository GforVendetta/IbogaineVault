# Dimethyltryptamine (DMT) and ibogaine elicit membrane effects in HEK cells transiently transfected with the human 5-HT2A receptor

## Citation
Eliasen JN, Kristiansen U, Kohlmeier KA. Dimethyltryptamine (DMT) and ibogaine elicit membrane effects in HEK cells transiently transfected with the human 5-HT2A receptor. *Brain Research*. 2025;1850:149425. doi:10.1016/j.brainres.2024.149425

## Key Findings
Ibogaine (30-100μM) activates membrane currents in 5-HT2AR-transfected HEK cells via NSCC. I197V SNP shows shorter DMT response duration. First demonstration of 5-HT2AR-mediated ionic currents in this model.

## Abstract
This study represents the first demonstration that HEK cells transfected with the 5-HT2A receptor can produce measurable membrane currents following exposure to psychedelic agonists. Using whole-cell patch-clamp electrophysiology, the authors showed that both DMT (500nM) and ibogaine (30-100μM) elicit predominantly inward currents in transfected cells but not in non-transfected controls.

## Methods

### Cell Model
- **Cell line:** HEK293 (Human Embryonic Kidney)
- **Transfection:** Transient transfection with human 5-HT2AR (wildtype or I197V SNP)
- **Reporter:** GFP co-transfection for visual identification

### Electrophysiology
- **Technique:** Whole-cell patch clamp
- **Holding potential:** -70 mV
- **Drug application:** 60 seconds via seven-barrel application system
- **I-V curves:** -110 to +20 mV in 10mV increments

### Drug Concentrations
| Drug | Concentration | Rationale |
|------|---------------|-----------|
| DMT | 500 nM | Approximates human plasma levels (60-500nM) |
| Ibogaine | 30 μM | Lower therapeutic range |
| Ibogaine | 100 μM | Higher therapeutic range |
| 5-HT | 30 μM | Positive control |

## Results

### Response Rates
| Drug | WT Response Rate | I197V Response Rate |
|------|------------------|---------------------|
| DMT 500nM | 75.0% (27/36) | 68.2% (15/22) |
| Ibogaine 30μM | 92.0% (23/25) | 81.8% (9/11) |
| Ibogaine 100μM | 71.9% (23/32) | 54.2% (13/24) |

No responses in non-transfected HEK cells (n=12 DMT, n=13 ibogaine).

### Current Polarity
Majority of responses were inward currents in both genotypes:
- DMT: 74-93% inward
- Ibogaine 30μM: 78-96% inward
- Ibogaine 100μM: 69-78% inward

### Kinetics

#### Amplitude
No significant differences between WT and I197V for any drug condition.

#### Duration
**Significant finding:** DMT-induced inward current duration was significantly shorter in I197V SNP (107.7 ± 19.2 sec) compared to WT (226.7 ± 28.9 sec; p=0.0022).

No duration differences for ibogaine at either concentration.

### I-V Curve Analysis
- **Reversal potential (DMT):** ~-3 to -11 mV (consistent with NSCC)
- **Reversal potential (ibogaine):** ~-8 to -30 mV
- Significant drug × voltage interaction for all conditions
- Main effect of drug for DMT and 100μM ibogaine (not 30μM)

## Interpretation

### Mechanism
Data consistent with activation of non-selective cation channels (NSCCs) downstream of 5-HT2AR → Gq → PLC-β → IP3/DAG pathway. HEK cells endogenously express functional NSCCs including TRPM4.

### SNP Pharmacology
The I197V polymorphism:
- Associated with altered antidepressant/antipsychotic response in humans
- Shows differential kinetics to DMT (shorter duration) but not ibogaine
- No amplitude differences suggest receptor expression levels similar between genotypes
- May reflect differences in desensitisation, internalisation, or downstream signalling efficiency

### Methodological Contribution
First demonstration that 5-HT2AR transfection in HEK cells produces agonist-activated membrane currents. This establishes a potential drug screening platform for novel 5-HT2AR agonists including psychedelic-inspired compounds.

## Limitations
- In-vitro model — HEK cells lack neuronal complexity
- Concentrations difficult to translate to in-vivo brain levels
- Did not definitively identify the conductance (presumed NSCC)
- Outward currents in minority of cells not characterised

## Clinical Relevance
- Supports 5-HT2AR as mediator of ibogaine's psychoactive effects
- Demonstrates ibogaine acts as functional 5-HT2AR agonist at therapeutically relevant concentrations
- SNP data may inform pharmacogenomic approaches to psychedelic therapy
- Platform could screen novel ibogaine analogues for 5-HT2AR activity
