# Treating War Trauma with Ibogaine and 5-MeO-DMT

**Authors:** Martin Polanco, Mia Black  
**Source:** *The Alkaloids* (Brill Academic), 2025, Chapter 20  
**DOI:** [10.1163/9789004729759_022](https://doi.org/10.1163/9789004729759_022)

---

## Key Findings

> Mission Within protocol: sequential ibogaine + 5-MeO-DMT treatment for veterans with PTSD, TBI, and moral injury. 850+ veterans and 250 family members treated 2017–2023 (~48 retreats/year). Comprehensive safety screening (EKG, electrolytes, urinalysis) with ACLS-trained medical staff. Three-phase integration model supports long-term outcomes.

---

## Clinical Significance

This book chapter provides the most comprehensive published description of the **Mission Within protocol** — the combination ibogaine + 5-MeO-DMT treatment model that has treated the largest cohort of veterans with ibogaine globally. Essential reference for any clinic serving veteran populations.

---

## Summary

### Background

The chapter describes the evolution of ibogaine treatment from addiction focus to trauma applications. In 2015, veterans presenting for addiction treatment at Crossroads Ibogaine Clinic in Mexico serendipitously reported resolution of PTSD symptoms, leading to the development of a dedicated veteran-focused protocol.

### Treatment Model

**Programme structure:**
- Cost: $6,000 USD (scholarships available for SOF veterans)
- Duration: 4–5 day retreat
- Setting: Baja California, Mexico
- Staffing: 3 medical professionals, therapeutic facilitators, security

**Treatment sequence:**
1. Pre-treatment preparation (2–4 weeks before)
2. Ibogaine administration (Day 1–2, evening ceremony)
3. Rest day
4. 5-MeO-DMT administration (Day 3–4, morning)
5. Group processing and celebratory dinner
6. Post-treatment integration (3 calls over several weeks)

### Safety Protocols

**Pre-screening requirements:**
- Electrocardiogram (EKG) — rule out arrhythmias and prolonged QT
- Electrolyte levels
- Urinalysis drug screen
- Liver function tests
- Vitals assessment

**Contraindicated medications:**
- Antipsychotics
- Antidepressants
- Sleep aids
- Certain antibiotics and antifungals
- Antiarrhythmics
- Some supplements (e.g., milk thistle)

**On-site monitoring:**
- Continuous cardiac monitoring
- Pulse oximetry
- ACLS-trained personnel
- IV port placed on arrival

### Therapeutic Rationale

The chapter articulates a complementary mechanism for the two substances:

**Ibogaine:** "Shadow work" — excavates unconscious material, trauma, unprocessed grief, and negative self-talk. Induces a therapeutic dream state allowing recontextualisation of traumatic memories from a stepped-back perspective. Strong detoxifying properties.

**5-MeO-DMT:** "Mystical completion" — classic mystical experience of oneness, unity, and infinite love. Reintegrates the psyche after ibogaine's excavation. "Wipes the slate clean" and allows participants to finish on a "high note."

### Integration Framework

The chapter describes a three-phase post-treatment model:

| Phase | Timeframe | Characteristics |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Pink Cloud** | Weeks 2–4 | Afterglow, intense emotional highs/lows, maximum neuroplasticity |
| **Between Worlds** | Months 1–3 | Most challenging — old identity released, new vision unfolding |
| **Clarity Arising** | Months 3–6 | New vision manifesting, evidence of transformation |

Key integration practices emphasised: meditation, exercise, journaling, dietary changes, sleep hygiene, spiritual practice, relationship work.

### Outcomes

**Treatment population (2017–2023):**
- 850+ veterans treated
- 250 veteran family members (mostly spouses)
- ~48 retreats per year
- Clinical studies initiated with Dell Medical School, UT Austin
- Three published papers

### Mechanism Discussion

The chapter discusses ibogaine's complex pharmacology (acting on dopamine, serotonin, acetylcholine, sigma, opioid, and 50+ other receptors) and its neurotrophic effects:

- Stimulates BDNF and GDNF ("fertilisers for the brain")
- Promotes neurogenesis and neuroplasticity
- Effective for mild traumatic brain injury (mTBI) — common in combat veterans
- Breaks cue-desire linkages underlying addiction

### Practitioner Qualifications

The chapter emphasises that therapists working with veterans should:
- Have personal experience with ibogaine and 5-MeO-DMT
- Understand military culture and "Operator Syndrome"
- Hold minimum master's degree in relevant mental health field
- Avoid New Age vocabulary that veterans cannot relate to
- Ideally be veterans themselves or have worked extensively with military populations

## Contextual Notes

### Relationship to MISTIC Protocol

This protocol predates and differs from the Stanford MISTIC protocol ([[2024/Cherian2024_Magnesium_Ibogaine_TBI]]):

| Aspect | Mission Within | MISTIC |
|--------|----------------|--------|
| Setting | Mexico (unregulated) | Clinical trial |
| Combination | Ibogaine + 5-MeO-DMT | Magnesium + ibogaine |
| Cardiac approach | Pre-screening to exclude risk | Mg co-administration to prevent QTc prolongation |
| Sample size | 850+ (observational) | 30 (controlled) |

Both approaches target the veteran/TBI population with complementary safety strategies.

### Historical Context

The chapter provides valuable first-person accounts from both authors:
- **Polanco:** Introduced to peyote as teenager, family member's cocaine addiction led to ibogaine discovery, transitioned from ophthalmology residency to ibogaine practice
- **Black:** Clinical psychology doctoral training, personal psychedelic healing experiences, joined Mission Within in 2018
