# The Use of Ibogaine in the Treatment of Substance Use Disorders: Integrating Psychoactive Experiences as Part of the Recovery Journey

**Author:** Martie Underwood  
**Institution:** Canterbury Christ Church University  
**Degree:** Doctor of Psychotherapy by Professional Studies  
**Year:** 2025  
**Pages:** 193

---

## Overview

This doctoral thesis comprises five interconnected components examining ibogaine's phenomenology and its role in substance use disorder (SUD) recovery. The research integrates quantitative consciousness mapping with qualitative narrative analysis and longitudinal case study methodology, representing the first systematic application of the Phenomenology of Consciousness Inventory (PCI) to ibogaine states and the first investigation of narrative self transformation following ibogaine treatment.

---

## Thesis Structure

The thesis consists of five components:

1. **Literature Review** — Pharmacological, cultural, and psychological aspects of ibogaine
2. **Small-Scale Research Project (SSRP)** — PCI study exploring ibogaine state of consciousness (n=21)
3. **Applied Research Project (ARP)** — Mixed methods investigating narrative self transformation (n=38 questionnaire, n=10 interviews)
4. **Report on Professional Practice (RPP)** — Single case study of 32-month OUD recovery (n=1)
5. **Reflective Account** — Integration of learning journey

---

## Component 1: Literature Review

### Pharmacological Context

The review synthesises current understanding of ibogaine's complex pharmacology:

- **NMDA antagonism** — Primary proposed mechanism (still speculative)
- **Multi-receptor interactions** — Sigma, nicotinic, mu/kappa-opioid, dopaminergic, serotonergic systems
- **Noribogaine** — Primary metabolite with slow clearance (detected for days), acts as antidepressant and alleviates cravings
- **CYP2D6 metabolism** — Genetic polymorphisms affect metabolism
- **Dosing range** — 15–25mg/kg HCl, 3–5g TA/PTA

### Three Phases of Ibogaine Experience

1. **Acute phase** (1–3 hours onset) — Visual, dream-like, oneirophrenic
2. **Reflective phase** (4–20 hours) — Neutral, introspective
3. **Residual stimulation** (12–72+ hours) — Gradual lessening

### Cultural Context

The review documents Bwiti tradition (Gabon, Cameroon, Equatorial Guinea):

- Monotheistic universal religion where iboga = tree of knowledge
- Initiation rites comprising four stages: admission, theory, exercises, practice
- Purification protocols: abstain from sex/food, confession, large dose over 3 days/nights
- Four stages of visionary experience: incoherent images → menacing animals → mythical peaceful images → higher spiritual entities
- *Nlem myore* ("one heart only"): collective flow of emotions during ceremonies
- *Edika*: ceremonial exit and presentation to group as *bandzi* (initiated member)

### Safety Context

All fatalities occur in uncontrolled settings. Co-consumption of other substances represents a major risk factor. Neurotoxicity at high doses (100mg/kg) in rats, but species variation suggests human doses (up to 55mg/kg) are likely safe.

## Component 2: Small-Scale Research Project (SSRP)

### Study Design

**Aim:** Quantitatively map ibogaine's state of consciousness using the Phenomenology of Consciousness Inventory (PCI)

**Method:** Cross-sectional, self-report questionnaire administered retrospectively  
**Sample:** n=21 (treatment-seeking individuals)  
**Instrument:** PCI (12 major dimensions, 14 minor dimensions)

### Key Findings

**Visual imagery as dominant feature:**
- Strong correlation between visual imagery and altered experience (r²=0.624)
- Strong correlation between visual imagery and self-awareness (r²=0.200)
- Visual experience appears integral to therapeutic potential, not merely a byproduct

**PCI psygram revealed interconnectedness:**
- Altered experience, memory, and self-awareness form interconnected cluster
- Self-awareness cultivated through visual experience and altered state, NOT through internal dialogue

**Significant increases vs. baseline:**
- Negative affect (anger, sadness, fear)
- Meaning
- Arousal

**State classification:**
- Ibogaine SoC most closely resembles shamanic journey (not holotropic breathwork as hypothesised)
- Oneirophrenic classification inadequate — only describes acute phase, not reflective/residual phases

**Internal consistency:**
- Cronbach's alpha ≥0.70 for 8/12 major dimensions (reliable)
- Only 4/14 minor dimensions showed adequate reliability

### Theoretical Implications

The study challenges the oneirophrenic classification of ibogaine states. The reflective and residual phases involve distinct consciousness characteristics not captured by dream-like descriptors. The research supports holotropic state characteristics (per Grof 2016): transformation of consciousness, dramatic perceptual changes, intense emotions, altered thought processes, invasion of other dimensions while remaining oriented.

## Component 3: Applied Research Project (ARP)

### Study Design

**Aim:** Investigate narrative self transformation following ibogaine treatment

**Method:** Mixed methods, sequential explanatory design  
**Sample:** n=38 (questionnaire), n=10 (semi-structured interviews)  
**Framework:** Narrative self framework adapted from Amada et al. (2020)

### Eleven Dimensions of Narrative Self

1. Behavioural autonomy
2. Psychological autonomy
3. Purpose
4. Connectedness
5. Self-insight
6. Self-knowledge
7. Self-compassion
8. Decentering/objectivity
9. Self-esteem
10. Self-regulation
11. Resisting external influence

An **emerging 12th dimension** was identified: **self-attitudes** (decreased self-judgement)

### Quantitative Results

| Dimension | % Reporting Transformation |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| Connectedness (to self) | 97% |
| Self-insight | 97% |
| Self-knowledge | 97% |
| Psychological autonomy | 92% |
| Self-compassion | 92% |
| Self-esteem | 92% |
| Relationship with family/friends | 92% |
| Better conflict handling | 89% |
| Connected to people/animals/environment | 89% |
| Healthier boundaries | 86% |
| Decentering from stressful situations | 86% |
| Less influenced by others' opinions | 86% |

**Critical finding:** Adaptive transformation reported across ALL dimensions — no maladaptive changes reported.

### Integration Protocol Roadmap

Based on adaptive narrative self transformations, the thesis proposes targeted integration interventions:

| Dimension | Integration Protocol |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Behavioural autonomy | Assertiveness training |
| Psychological autonomy | Meditation, mindfulness |
| Purpose | Values clarification |
| Connectedness | Emotion regulation |
| Self-insight | Expressive writing |
| Self-knowledge | Psychoeducation |
| Self-compassion | Reframing critical self-talk |
| Self-attitudes | Reframing critical self-talk |
| Decentering | Mindfulness, meditation |
| Self-regulation | DBT skills training |
| Resisting external influence | Boundary setting |

## Component 4: Report on Professional Practice (RPP)

### Case Study Design

**Participant:** 26-year-old male  
**Follow-up:** 32 months post-ibogaine treatment (January 2021)  
**Condition:** Opioid Use Disorder (OUD)  
**Method:** Interpretative Phenomenological Analysis (IPA)

### Aetiology Findings

- Religious trauma and shame identified as contributing factors to OUD development
- Exposure to substances in multiple treatment facilities also contributed
- Religious trauma under-researched in SUD literature

### Ibogaine Experience Themes

- Death themes and transpersonal encounters
- Metacognitive shifts
- Fear during journey provided adaptive motivation for change
- Rites of passage symbolism psychologically significant

### Recovery Strategies

**Physical:** Established exercise routine

**Psychological:**
- Self-compassion development
- Self-insight cultivation
- Mindfulness practice

**Transpersonal:**
- Rites of passage symbolism
- Death and rebirth themes

### Clinical Implications

- Self-insight emerged as potential mechanism of action
- Increased mindfulness and self-compassion = adaptive narrative self transformation
- Metacognition important for recovery journey
- Fear (during journey) can serve adaptive function when properly integrated
- Death themes and rite of passage symbolism = adaptive transpersonal aspects

3. **Integration protocol roadmap** — Targeted interventions based on adaptive transformation patterns
4. **Mixed methods approach** — Sequential explanatory design combining quantitative and qualitative data
5. **IPA case study** — Demonstrating long-term recovery mechanisms

## Clinical Implications Summary

### For Treatment Providers

- Visual imagery may be integral to therapeutic potential — should not be suppressed
- Integration protocols can be targeted to specific narrative self dimensions
- Self-knowledge requires adequate psychotherapy support
- Higher self-compassion linked to increased happiness, optimism, curiosity; decreased anxiety, depression, rumination
- Decentering enables adaptive emotional regulation
- Self-regulation crucial for preventing relapse
- Resisting external influence essential for SUD recovery

### For Assessment and Screening

- Religious trauma should be assessed as potential aetiological factor
- Transpersonal orientation may enhance treatment outcomes
- Awakening experiences (temporary expansion of awareness) are common and clinically meaningful

### For Research

- Ibogaine induces distinct, structured SoC characterised by visual phenomena, heightened self-awareness, perceptual alterations
- Increased connection to self (inner experience) vs. external environment
- Oneirophrenic classification requires expansion to include reflective and residual phases

---

## Limitations

- Small sample sizes (SSRP n=21, ARP n=38/10, RPP n=1)
- PCI subscales showed lower internal consistency for minor dimensions
- Self-selected treatment-seeking populations
- Researcher dual role as treatment provider (reflexivity maintained)
- No demographic data collected (confidentiality protection)
- Findings exploratory, hypothesis-generating
- Legal status and ethical complexities limit large-scale trials
- Variability in dosing protocols and treatment settings

## Theoretical Framework

### Transpersonal Orientation

The thesis advocates for transpersonal framework in ibogaine research/practice:
- Death themes, archetypal imagery, ego dissolution, mystical experiences integral to therapeutic mechanism
- Rites of passage symbolism psychologically significant for recovery
- Religious trauma under-researched in SUD literature

### Key Theoretical Connections

- **Holotropic states** (Grof 2016): Transformation of consciousness, dramatic perceptual changes, intense emotions, altered thought processes
- **Near-death experience parallels**: Panoramic memory, neutral emotional tone, meeting transcendent beings, dissociative state
- **Psychedelic paradigm** (Winkelman 2014): Cathartic/mystical experiences → permanent personality/mood changes
- **Memory retrieval** (Popik 1996): Spatial learning enhancement in animal models
- **Mentalisation** (Choi-Kain & Gunderson): Ability to reflect on mental state of self, enhances secure attachments and emotional self-regulation
- **Narrative identity** (McAdams 2001, 2013, 2022): Self as social actor, motivated agent, autobiographical author
