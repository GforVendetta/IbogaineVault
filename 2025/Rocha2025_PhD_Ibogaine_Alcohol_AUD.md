# Tolerability and Efficacy of Ibogaine in the Treatment of Alcohol Use Disorder: An Open-Label Dose-Escalation Study

**Citation:** Rocha, J. M. (2025). *Tolerabilidade e eficácia da ibogaína no tratamento do transtorno por uso de álcool: um estudo aberto e de escalonamento de doses.* PhD Thesis. University of São Paulo, Ribeirão Preto Medical School. Supervisor: Hallak, J. E. C. Co-supervisor: dos Santos, R. G.

> **Language note:** Original thesis in Portuguese. Content below translated to English.

## Abstract

This PhD thesis reports the first clinical trial worldwide specifically designed to evaluate ibogaine for Alcohol Use Disorder (AUD). An open-label, dose-escalation design tested ibogaine hydrochloride (99.3% purity, Phytostan Enterprises Inc.) at doses from 20 mg to 400 mg in 9 participants with moderate-to-severe AUD (DSM-5) at the Clinical Hospital of Ribeirão Preto Medical School, University of São Paulo, Brazil.

The study was conducted in two phases: a dose-escalation phase (Volunteers 1–3, doses 20–320 mg, 72-hour inter-dose intervals) and a treatment phase (Volunteers 4–9, single 400 mg dose). COVID-19 necessitated major protocol changes mid-study, reducing hospitalisation from 20 days to 3 days, follow-up from 12 months to 3 months, and shifting from multi-dose to single-dose administration.

Primary outcomes were tolerability (adverse events via UKU Side Effect Rating Scale) and efficacy (alcohol use, depression, anxiety). Secondary outcomes included subjective effects (CADSS, HRS), personality changes (NEO-FFI, PID-5), and social cognition (MET, REFE).

## Key Findings

- **First worldwide AUD-specific ibogaine trial** — landmark despite small sample and methodological limitations
- **Dose-dependent QTc prolongation** observed across all doses, with peaks approaching clinically dangerous thresholds (496 ms at 400 mg)
- **Transient ST-elevation and T-wave inversion** in one participant (Vol 7, 400 mg) — spontaneous resolution, follow-up angio-CT normal
- **Hypertensive crisis** in one participant (Vol 3, 240 mg) — BP 180/110 mmHg, required captopril + lorazepam
- **2/9 participants achieved 3-month abstinence** from both alcohol and cocaine (Vol 5 and Vol 8)
- **Marked depression improvement** — BDI-II scores dropped from Severe (baseline) to Minimal (discharge) across the cohort
- **Major confound:** 8/9 participants had comorbid cocaine use disorder, making it impossible to attribute outcomes solely to ibogaine's effect on alcohol

## Methodology

### Study Design
- **Type:** Open-label dose-escalation (Phase I/II characteristics)
- **Setting:** Clinical Hospital, Ribeirão Preto Medical School, University of São Paulo
- **Ethics:** Approved by HC-FMRP-USP Ethics Committee (CAAE: 64182816.5.0000.5440); ANVISA authorised ibogaine importation
- **Registration:** RBR-9h7ckx (ReBEC — Brazilian Clinical Trials Registry)

### Participants
- **Enrolled:** 9 (all male, ages 29–58)
- **Screening funnel:** 388 interested → 9 enrolled
- **Inclusion:** Adults 18–65 with moderate-to-severe AUD (DSM-5), abstinent ≥3 days
- **Key exclusions:** Cardiac disease, psychotic disorders, pregnancy, hepatic impairment, concurrent QT-prolonging medications

### Dosing Protocol

| Phase | Volunteers | Doses (mg HCl) | Design |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| Dose-escalation | Vol 1–3 | 20, 80, 160, 240, 320 | Ascending, 72h intervals |
| Treatment | Vol 4–9 | 400 (single dose) | One session |

- **Formulation:** Ibogaine HCl 99.3% purity (Phytostan Enterprises Inc.)
- **Administration:** Oral capsules, after light breakfast
- **Monitoring:** ECG at 8 timepoints per session (baseline, 1h, 2h, 3h, 4h, 5h, 6h, 12h); vital signs hourly

### COVID-19 Protocol Modifications
The study protocol was substantially altered mid-study due to the pandemic:
- Hospitalisation reduced: 20 days → 3 days
- Follow-up reduced: 12 months → 3 months
- Multi-dose protocol abandoned → single-dose (Vols 4–9)
- Creates a heterogeneous dataset complicating interpretation

### Outcome Measures
- **Tolerability:** UKU Side Effect Rating Scale, ECG monitoring, vital signs, laboratory panels
- **Efficacy:** Timeline Followback (TLFB), BDI-II (depression), BAI (anxiety)
- **Subjective effects:** CADSS (dissociation), HRS (hallucinogen rating), BPRS (psychiatric symptoms)
- **Personality:** NEO-FFI (Big Five), PID-5 (pathological traits)
- **Social cognition:** MET (multifaceted empathy), REFE (facial emotion recognition)

## Cardiac Safety Data

> **⚠️ CLINICAL SAFETY NOTE:** This section contains clinically actionable cardiac data. Dose-dependent QTc prolongation was observed with peaks approaching dangerous thresholds.

### QTc Prolongation by Dose and Volunteer

| Volunteer | Dose (mg) | Baseline QTc (ms) | Peak QTc (ms) | Time to Peak | ΔQTc (ms) |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|---------------|-------------|-----------|
| Vol 3 | 240 | 413 | 492 | 12h | +79 |
| Vol 5 | 400 | 413 | 496 | 12h | +83 |
| Vol 9 | 400 | 409 | 470 | 6h | +61 |

- QTc prolongation was **dose-dependent** and observed across all dose levels
- Multiple subjects approached or reached the 500 ms threshold associated with increased arrhythmia risk
- Prolongation persisted at 12 hours post-dose, consistent with ibogaine's long half-life and noribogaine accumulation
- **No magnesium cardioprotection protocol** was used — contrast with [[2024/Cherian2024_Magnesium_Ibogaine_TBI|MISTIC protocol]] which achieved zero QTc events

### Serious Cardiac Adverse Events

**Volunteer 7 (400 mg):**
- **5h post-dose:** QTc 452 ms with T-wave inversion in V2/V3
- **6h post-dose:** Transient ST-elevation in V2/V3 (ischaemic mimic pattern)
- **Spontaneous resolution** by 12h post-dose
- **Follow-up angio-CT:** Normal coronary anatomy, no evidence of ischaemia
- **Interpretation:** Likely ibogaine-mediated ion channel effects rather than true ischaemia, but clinically significant and warranting close monitoring

**Volunteer 3 (240 mg):**
- **Hypertensive crisis:** BP reached 180/110 mmHg
- **Management:** Captopril 25 mg + lorazepam 4 mg administered
- **Outcome:** Planned 400 mg dose session was cancelled as a safety measure

### Electrolyte Data

Laboratory values (Na, K, Ca, Mg) were measured at admission and discharge for all participants. No clinically significant electrolyte derangements were reported, though notably **no pre-treatment electrolyte optimisation protocol** was employed — a significant gap given current best practice (cf. [[2024/Cherian2024_Magnesium_Ibogaine_TBI|MISTIC protocol]]).

## Adverse Events

### UKU Side Effect Profile (across all sessions)
- **Somnolence:** 53%
- **Nausea:** 36%
- **Anxiety:** 33%
- **Ataxia:** Common (dose-dependent)
- **Tremor, dizziness, dry mouth:** Also reported

Adverse events were dose-dependent and generally transient, resolving within the monitoring period.

### Medications Administered During Study
- Captopril 25 mg (Vol 3 — hypertensive crisis)
- Lorazepam 4 mg (Vol 3 — anxiolysis during hypertensive episode)
- Ondansetron (anti-emetic, multiple volunteers)
- Metoclopramide (anti-emetic)

## Efficacy Results

### Alcohol Use
- **2/9 achieved 3-month abstinence** from alcohol and cocaine (Vol 5, Vol 8)
- Remaining participants showed variable outcomes
- **3/9 lost to follow-up** (33% attrition)

### Depression and Anxiety
- **BDI-II:** Dramatic improvement from Severe (baseline) to Minimal (discharge) across the cohort
- **BAI:** Improvement from Moderate (baseline) to Mild (discharge)
- Depression improvement was consistent even in participants who did not achieve abstinence

### Subjective and Dissociative Effects
- **CADSS and HRS** scores were dose-dependent, peaking 2–3 hours post-administration
- Effects consistent with ibogaine's known psychoactive profile
- Higher doses (400 mg) produced more intense dissociative and visionary experiences

### Personality and Social Cognition
- **NEO-FFI:** Trends toward reduced Neuroticism, though underpowered
- **PID-5:** Reductions in pathological personality traits
- **MET/REFE:** No consistent changes in empathy or emotion recognition

## Limitations

> **⚠️ These limitations are substantial and must be considered when interpreting results.**

1. **Polysubstance confound:** 8/9 participants had comorbid cocaine use disorder. It is impossible to attribute outcomes solely to ibogaine's effect on alcohol — this is the single most significant limitation
2. **No control group:** Vols 4–9 received open-label treatment; Vols 1–3 served as their own dose-escalation controls (methodologically weak)
3. **High attrition:** 3/9 lost to follow-up (33%) — undermines efficacy conclusions
4. **Self-reported outcomes only:** No objective biomarkers (PEth, EtG, CDT) to verify abstinence claims
5. **Positive toxicology at admission:** Vols 2, 3, 5, and 6 tested positive for substances despite 3-day abstinence instruction — raises compliance questions
6. **Protocol heterogeneity (COVID):** Mid-study changes created two distinct sub-groups (multi-dose vs single-dose, long vs short hospitalisation, 12-month vs 3-month follow-up) — complicates any aggregate analysis
7. **Very low initial doses:** First 3 subjects received 20–320 mg (likely sub-therapeutic) yet are included in efficacy analysis
8. **No electrolyte optimisation:** Absence of magnesium cardioprotection protocol — now considered best practice per MISTIC
9. **Small sample (N=9):** Insufficient for meaningful statistical inference on efficacy

## Clinical Implications

Despite its limitations, this study is clinically significant for clinical practice in several ways:

1. **Cardiac monitoring benchmark:** The QTc data provides dose-response evidence for ibogaine cardiotoxicity in a controlled setting, reinforcing the need for continuous cardiac monitoring and the value of magnesium cardioprotection
2. **Dose-response relationship confirmed:** Clear dose-dependent relationship for both therapeutic effects and cardiac risk
3. **ST-elevation/T-wave changes:** The Vol 7 event is an important addition to the literature on ibogaine's cardiac effects beyond QTc — clinicians should be aware of ischaemic mimic patterns
4. **Hypertensive risk:** The Vol 3 hypertensive crisis reinforces the need for blood pressure monitoring and having antihypertensive medications available
5. **Depression improvement:** Consistent BDI-II improvement suggests antidepressant effects even at sub-therapeutic doses for addiction — relevant for mood comorbidity in the patient population
6. **AUD feasibility demonstrated:** Despite limitations, this establishes that ibogaine for AUD is feasible to study and warrants larger, better-controlled trials
