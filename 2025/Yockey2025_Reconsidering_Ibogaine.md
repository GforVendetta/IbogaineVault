# Reconsidering Ibogaine for the treatment of severe mental illness and substance use disorders

**Authors:** R. Andrew Yockey (University of Mississippi, Department of Public Health)

**Citation:** Yockey RA. Reconsidering Ibogaine for the treatment of severe mental illness and substance use disorders. *Journal of Medicine, Surgery, and Public Health*. 2025;7:100213. doi:10.1016/j.glmedi.2025.100213

---

## Key Findings

Editorial/perspective piece arguing for reconsidering ibogaine's therapeutic potential for severe mental illness (SMI) and co-occurring substance use disorders. Synthesises existing evidence and calls for structured research priorities including RCTs with pharmacogenomic considerations, safer analogues, and inpatient cardiac monitoring protocols.

---

## Summary

### Global Burden Context

- Severe mental illness (SMI) affects 4–7% of adults globally
- Associated with 10–20 year reduction in life expectancy
- Mental and substance use disorders accounted for 14.6% of global years lived with disability (YLDs) in 2019
- Cumulative economic impact projected at $16 trillion (2010–2030)
- Up to 85% of individuals in LMICs receive no mental health treatment

### Historical Overview

- Traditional use in Bwiti spiritual practices (Gabon, Cameroon, Republic of Congo)
- Early 20th century: marketed in France as stimulant "Lambarène"
- 1960s: Howard Lotsof's discovery of anti-addictive properties
- As of 2006: >3,400 individuals had received ibogaine therapy (likely far higher today)
- Dozens of clinics worldwide currently offer treatment

### Clinical Evidence Reviewed

- Early observational studies suggest ibogaine and noribogaine may attenuate withdrawal, reduce cravings, and improve mood/trauma symptoms
- Two double-blind placebo-controlled trials: modest reductions in dependence symptoms but underpowered
- Noller (2018): 12-month follow-up showed significant reductions in depressive symptoms and drug use
- Davis (2017): Retrospective survey of 88 individuals in Mexico — 54% remained abstinent from opioids for ≥6 months, 30% reported complete long-term abstinence

### Safety Concerns

- QT interval prolongation and risk of fatal arrhythmias (torsades de pointes)
- Sudden cardiac deaths reported, particularly with underlying cardiovascular disease, electrolyte imbalances, or concurrent substance use
- Neurotoxicity at high doses in preclinical models (cerebellar Purkinje cells)
- Adverse neurological events: ataxia, tremors, psychosis
- Complex pharmacokinetics with long half-life and active metabolites

### Safer Analogues Under Development

- **18-methoxycoronaridine (18-MC)**: Aims to replicate anti-addictive effects without cardiotoxicity
- **Tabernanthalog (TBG)**: Non-hallucinogenic analogue promoting neuroplasticity
- Human trials still in early stages

### 2025 US Policy Developments

| State | Initiative |
|-------|------------|
| **Texas** | $50 million public–private partnership for FDA-regulated clinical trials (OUD, PTSD, TBI). Mandates inpatient treatment with cardiac monitoring. State retains IP rights. |
| **Colorado** | Natural Medicine Health Act — licensed "healing centres" for supervised ibogaine administration |
| **Arizona** | HB2871 — appropriation for ibogaine clinical research |
| **New York** | S1817 — requires OASAS to facilitate ibogaine clinical research |
| **Washington** | SB 5204 — concerning ibogaine-assisted therapy |
| **Kentucky** | SB 240 — ibogaine research in the Commonwealth |

### Research Priorities Identified

1. **RCTs with pharmacogenomics** — particularly CYP2D6 polymorphisms affecting metabolism
2. **Inpatient administration** — continuous ECG monitoring and emergency support
3. **Safer analogues** — continued development of 18-MC and tabernanthalog
4. **Mechanistic studies** — neurotransmitter modulation and neuroplasticity
5. **Longitudinal follow-up** — durability of benefits, delayed adverse events, relapse rates

---

## Clinical Relevance

This perspective piece provides a useful 2025 snapshot of the US policy landscape and synthesises the case for structured ibogaine research. While not presenting original data, it articulates the tension between promising observational evidence and serious cardiac safety concerns that has shaped the field's development.

The Texas initiative represents the largest public investment in ibogaine research to date and may accelerate the evidence base for clinical protocols.
