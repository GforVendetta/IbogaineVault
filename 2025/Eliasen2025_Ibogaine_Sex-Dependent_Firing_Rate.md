# The psychoactive compound ibogaine sex-dependently alters the firing rate and afterhyperpolarization of Ih-negative neurons in the mouse ventral tegmental area

**Citation:** Eliasen JN, Rezagholizadeh A, Jacobsen HP, Kristiansen U, Kohlmeier KA. The psychoactive compound ibogaine sex-dependently alters the firing rate and afterhyperpolarization of Ih-negative neurons in the mouse ventral tegmental area. *Neuroscience*. 2025;589:308–321. doi:10.1016/j.neuroscience.2025.10.001

---

## Key Findings

Sex-dependent effects of 100 μM ibogaine on putative GABAergic (Ih-negative) neurons in the mouse ventral tegmental area (VTA):

**Males:**
- Significant decrease in action potential (AP) firing rate (0.82 → 0.45 Hz)
- Significant increase in inter-AP intervals (1.46 → 2.83 sec)
- No change in afterhyperpolarisation (AHP) duration or amplitude

**Females:**
- No change in AP firing rate or intervals
- Significant decrease in AHP duration (26.2 → 20.0 msec)
- Significant decrease in AHP amplitude (11.9 → 7.1 mV)

**Both sexes:**
- Intracellular calcium rises (~29% ΔF/F₀) in majority of neurons
- No changes in membrane currents, membrane potential, I-V relationship, rheobase, or spontaneous excitatory postsynaptic currents (sEPSCs)

**Baseline sex differences:**
- Male Ih-negative VTA neurons fired at significantly higher frequency than females
- Males showed larger AHP amplitude at baseline

## Abstract

Depression and substance use disorder affect millions world-wide, and risk factors include sex. Conventional pharmacotherapies show at best a 50% success rate. Psychedelics exhibit both anti-addictive and anti-depressive properties. One of these, ibogaine, a root bark sourced alkaloid, interacts with receptors implicated in antidepressive and anti-addictive effects with affinities in the micromolar range. However, cellular actions underlying therapeutic properties are not well understood.

In this study, for the first time, the authors evaluated the cellular effects of 100 μM ibogaine on putative GABAergic neurons of the ventral tegmental area (VTA), as activity of neurons in this nucleus, which includes dopamine neurons, modulates emotion and motivated behaviour. Neurons from male (n = 14) and female mice (n = 16) were putatively identified as GABAergic based on lack of Ih-current.

While no ibogaine-induced effects on membrane currents, membrane potential, I-V relationship, rheobase or spontaneous excitatory postsynaptic currents (sEPSCs) were detected in either sex, rises in intracellular calcium were induced in males and females. Further, ibogaine decreased the action potential firing rate in males, but not in females, whereas altered afterhyperpolarisation kinetics were noted in females, but not males.

Interestingly, at baseline, male Ih-negative VTA neurons fired action potentials at a significantly higher frequency than females, however, membrane currents, I-V relationship, membrane potential, rheobase, and sEPSCs did not differ between sexes.

The data suggest that sex-based firing differences exist in a subpopulation of VTA neurons and further, ibogaine induces changes in neuronal signalling in this population that differ between males and females, which could contribute to therapeutic actions.

## Methods

### Animals
- Naval Medical Research Institute female and male mice (postnatal day 14–25)
- Housing conditions designed to reduce stress and anxiety
- Pre/peri-adolescent mice used to minimise hormonal modulation effects

### Electrophysiology
- Whole-cell patch clamp recordings from VTA brain slices (250 μm coronal)
- Neurons identified as putatively GABAergic based on absence of Ih current
- Voltage-clamp and current-clamp configurations
- 100 μM ibogaine bath application (dissolved in DMSO 1:1000)

### Calcium Imaging
- Multiple cell imaging with Fura-2 AM
- Single cell imaging with Fura-2-penta via patch pipette
- Ratiometric fluorescence (F340/F380) as proxy for intracellular calcium

### Parameters Evaluated
- Membrane holding currents
- Current-voltage (I-V) relationships
- Membrane potential
- Rheobase
- Action potential frequency and interval
- Afterhyperpolarisation (AHP) duration and amplitude
- Spontaneous excitatory postsynaptic currents (sEPSCs)
- Intracellular calcium changes

---

## Results Summary

### No Effect on Membrane Properties
Ibogaine (100 μM) failed to alter:
- Membrane holding currents in either sex
- I-V relationship (-120 to -30 mV)
- Membrane potential
- Rheobase
- sEPSC amplitude or frequency

Similar null results were observed in male medial prefrontal cortex (mPFC) pyramidal neurons.

### Sex-Dependent Effects on Action Potential Firing

**Males:**
- Baseline AP frequency: 0.82 ± 0.12 Hz
- During ibogaine: 0.45 ± 0.09 Hz (p < 0.0001)
- Baseline AP interval: 1.46 ± 0.27 sec
- During ibogaine: 2.83 ± 0.68 sec (p = 0.0025)

**Females:**
- Baseline AP frequency: 0.38 ± 0.07 Hz
- During ibogaine: 0.49 ± 0.11 Hz (ns)
- No significant change in AP intervals

Control experiments (ACSF without ibogaine) confirmed the effect was drug-specific.

### Sex-Dependent Effects on Afterhyperpolarisation

**Females:**
- AHP duration decreased: 26.2 → 20.0 msec (p = 0.0013)
- AHP amplitude decreased: 11.9 → 7.1 mV (p = 0.0007)

**Males:**
- No significant change in AHP duration or amplitude

### Calcium Imaging
- Multiple cell imaging: 80% of female cells responded (75% increases); 50% of male cells responded (86% increases)
- Single cell imaging: 53% of female Ih-negative neurons showed calcium increases; 71% of male neurons showed increases
- No significant sex difference in amplitude of calcium rises (~29% ΔF/F₀)

### Baseline Sex Differences
- Males fired APs at significantly higher frequency than females (0.82 vs 0.38 Hz, p = 0.0037)
- Males had significantly greater AHP amplitude at baseline (15.9 vs 11.9 mV, p = 0.033)

## Discussion

This represents the first ex vivo electrophysiological study of ibogaine's effects on Ih-negative VTA neurons.

### Mechanistic Implications
- Ibogaine's reduction of GABAergic firing in males could result in disinhibition of dopamine VTA cells
- This could contribute to increased dopamine signalling observed in vivo after ibogaine
- Sex-dependent effects suggest different mechanistic pathways may underlie therapeutic actions in males vs females

### Calcium and Plasticity
- Calcium rises in both sexes could promote transcription of plasticity-related genes (BDNF, GDNF)
- 5-HT2A receptor agonism may trigger IP3/DAG pathway leading to calcium release
- Plasticity-promoting effects could facilitate network reorganisation relevant to anti-depression and anti-addiction effects

### Limitations
- Ih absence as GABAergic marker is not definitive
- Single concentration tested (100 μM)
- Pre/peri-adolescent mice only
- Mechanisms underlying sex differences not fully elucidated

---

## Clinical Relevance

While this is a preclinical study, the findings have potential clinical implications:

1. **Sex-based treatment responses:** The sex-dependent cellular effects suggest ibogaine may work through different mechanisms in males vs females, potentially relevant to personalised treatment approaches

2. **Dopamine modulation:** Disinhibition of dopamine neurons via reduced GABAergic firing could contribute to anti-depressive and anti-addictive effects

3. **Plasticity:** Calcium-mediated signalling may underlie long-term therapeutic effects through BDNF/TrkB pathways
