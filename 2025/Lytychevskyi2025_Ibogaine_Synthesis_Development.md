## Abstract

This MSc thesis addresses two independent synthetic chemistry projects. Only Chapter 1 concerns ibogaine and is relevant to the IbogaineVault. That chapter reviews existing total syntheses of ibogaine and develops novel retrosynthetic strategies targeting both racemic and enantioselective routes, with the goal of providing a feasible, scalable synthesis. Chapters 2–3 concern acetal-free analogues of the glycolipid immunostimulant KRN7000 and are unrelated to ibogaine research.

## Key Findings

### Historical Total Synthesis Review

Chapter 1 provides a detailed review of all accomplished total syntheses of ibogaine, contextualising the structural challenge posed by the molecule's three key fragments: the indole moiety, a tetrahydroazepine ring, and an isoquinuclidine cycle.

**Büchi (1966)** — The first total synthesis of (±)-ibogaine. Employed an acid-catalysed cyclisation and skeletal rearrangement strategy, followed by zinc-mediated reduction and fragmentation with subsequent Michael addition.

**Sinha (2012)** — Developed separate approaches to the quinuclidine and indole portions, employing a reductive Heck strategy. Final coupling yielded ibogaine through a convergent route.

**She (2016)** — A biomimetic approach via condensation of 5-methoxytryptamine with dimethylglutarate, followed by an intramolecular aldol reaction. The endgame involved formation of 12-methoxy-tabertinggine as an intermediate en route to ibogaine.

**Trost** — Demonstrated an enantioselective synthesis of (+)-ibogamine using asymmetric allylic alkylation, providing the foundation for chiral approaches to the iboga skeleton.

### First-Generation Strategy: Asymmetric Diels-Alder

The author's primary approach fragmented ibogaine retrosynthetically into indole and isoquinuclidine components. The key bond-forming step was an asymmetric Diels-Alder reaction employing MacMillan's imidazolidinone organocatalyst.

- Synthesised the required diene starting material (compound **33**)
- Explored both chiral (MacMillan catalyst) and racemic Diels-Alder conditions
- Conducted extensive optimisation of catalyst loading, temperature, solvent, and acid co-catalyst (Table 1.1 — the only data table in the ibogaine chapter)
- Racemic Diels-Alder succeeded; enantioselective variant proved challenging under tested conditions

### Second-Generation Strategy: Robinson Annulation

An alternative approach via asymmetric Michael addition followed by intramolecular aldol condensation. This Robinson annulation strategy encountered competing reaction pathways — the intramolecular aldol could form either the desired or undesired ring closure product. A formal aza-Diels-Alder reaction was proposed as a subsequent step.

### 4-Ethylcyclohexenone Construction

A critical intermediate for both strategies. Multiple synthetic routes were explored:

- **Asymmetric deprotonation with Saegusa-Ito oxidation** — chiral route via LDA-mediated deprotonation
- **Three-step approach from ethyl vinyl ketone** — racemic route through 4-ethylcyclohexanone
- **IBX-mediated dehydrogenation** — oxidation of saturated ketone to enone
- **Stork-Danheiser transposition** — both racemic and chiral variants investigated
- **3-Ethoxycyclohexenone alkylation** — two-step approach via ethylation

The experimental section (Chapter 3, sections 3.2.15–3.2.19) documents synthesis and characterisation of 3-ethoxycyclohexenone, 3-ethoxy-6-ethylcyclohexenone, 4-ethylcyclohexenone, and MacMillan catalyst variants.

## Methodology

Synthetic organic chemistry thesis employing standard techniques: NMR spectroscopy (¹H, ¹³C, COSY, DEPTQ, HSQC, HMBC), high-resolution mass spectrometry, and extensive reaction optimisation. No biological assays, no animal studies, no clinical data. All experimental work conducted at the University of Windsor with HRMS support from Brock University.

## Scope Note

This is a dual-project thesis. Only Chapter 1 (ibogaine synthesis, ~34 pages including figures) is relevant to the IbogaineVault. Chapters 2–3 concern KRN7000 glycolipid immunology — an entirely separate field. The spectral data section (pages 106–219) contains NMR characterisation primarily for KRN7000 intermediates, with only the final three spectra (compounds 3-ethoxycyclohexenone, 3-ethoxy-6-ethylcyclohexenone, 4-ethylcyclohexenone) relating to ibogaine synthesis intermediates.

## Clinical Implications

Limited direct clinical relevance. This is foundational synthetic chemistry work aimed at developing scalable access to ibogaine. If successful, such routes could address supply constraints that currently limit clinical availability. The thesis does not complete a full synthetic route — it advances methodology toward that goal.

The broader context is significant: ibogaine's controlled substance status in many jurisdictions means that reliable, scalable synthesis is a prerequisite for expanded clinical access. Work like this, alongside Šijić's concurrent synthesis review (2025), represents the current frontier of making ibogaine more accessible through chemistry rather than solely through plant extraction from *Tabernanthe iboga*.

## Limitations

- Synthesis route not completed — neither generation achieved full total synthesis of ibogaine
- Dual-focus thesis dilutes ibogaine content (roughly half the thesis concerns KRN7000)
- Enantioselective Diels-Alder variant unsuccessful under tested conditions
- No biological evaluation of any intermediates or products
- Pure methodology — no pharmacological, toxicological, or clinical data
