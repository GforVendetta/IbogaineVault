# A Qualitative Inquiry into Practitioners' Experiences of Using Ibogaine and Other Psychoplastogens in the Treatment of Substance Misuse

**Author:** Ahmet Enginsoy, MSc
**Institution:** Lancaster University, Faculty of Health and Medicine
**Degree:** Doctor of Philosophy in Mental Health
**Date:** April 2025

---

## Key Findings

PhD thesis exploring ibogaine treatment from the practitioner perspective. Combines a scoping review of psychedelic-assisted therapy trials with qualitative interviews of experienced ibogaine providers. Emphasises the importance of holistic, patient-centric approaches and the interplay between set, setting, and therapeutic outcomes.

---

## Thesis Structure

### Component 1: Scoping Review (Chapter 3)
**"The Research Design and Intervention Characteristics of Trials Using Psychedelics and Other Psychoplastogens for Addiction Treatment"**

- Maps the broader field of psychedelic research for addiction
- Examines design characteristics across LSD, psilocybin, ketamine, ibogaine trials
- Identifies methodological evolution, bias concerns, and outcome measures used
- Highlights need for standardisation and larger sample sizes

### Component 2: Qualitative Study (Chapter 4)
**"Exploring Current Practices in Substance Misuse Treatment with Iboga and Ibogaine: Insights from Facilitators"**

Semi-structured interviews with experienced ibogaine treatment providers using reflexive thematic analysis.

**Research Questions:**
1. What is the role of ibogaine and treatment providers in substance misuse recovery?
2. How is ibogaine used to treat substance misuse?
3. What therapies are used to support ibogaine treatment?

---

## Theoretical Framework

### Biopsychosocial Model
The thesis adopts a biopsychosocial lens, arguing that ibogaine's therapeutic potential cannot be understood through pharmacology alone but requires integration of:
- **Biological:** Neuroplasticity, GDNF upregulation, multi-receptor modulation
- **Psychological:** Transformative experiences, altered consciousness, insight
- **Social:** Set and setting, therapeutic relationship, integration support

### Recovery Models Referenced
- **CHIME Framework:** Connectedness, Hope, Identity, Meaningfulness, Empowerment
- **Self-Medication Hypothesis (Khantzian):** Substance choice influenced by specific psychopharmacological effects addressing psychological struggles
- **Personal Recovery Model:** Emphasis on meaningful life despite ongoing challenges, rather than purely symptom-based "clinical recovery"

---

## Key Themes from Practitioner Interviews

### Varied Dosing Strategies
Practitioners employ diverse dosing approaches reflecting different treatment philosophies and patient needs.

### Pre-Treatment Preparation
- Screening and assessment
- Psychological preparation
- Ritual elements in some settings
- Set and setting optimisation

### Post-Treatment Integration
- Integration sessions where insights are discussed
- Ongoing therapeutic support
- Connection to community resources

### Holistic Patient-Centric Approach
Emphasis on treating the whole person within their social context, not merely administering a pharmacological intervention.

## Ibogaine Pharmacology Summary

The thesis provides a concise overview of ibogaine's mechanisms:

| Target | Action |
|--------|--------|
| 5-HT2A receptors | Agonism |
| Opioid receptors (kappa, mu) | Modulation |
| Sigma-1, Sigma-2 receptors | Agonism |
| NMDA receptors | Antagonism |
| Nicotinic receptors | Antagonism |
| Dopamine/5-HT transporters | Antagonism |
| MAO | Inhibition |
| GDNF | Upregulation (neuroplasticity) |

**Distinction from classical psychedelics:** Ibogaine produces a "dream-like state" rather than the typical "trip" associated with LSD/psilocybin, possibly due to its broader receptor profile beyond 5-HT2A.

## Critique of Current Evidence

The thesis acknowledges limitations in ibogaine research:

- Small sample sizes (e.g., 14 participants in Alper studies)
- Lack of control groups in many studies
- Only two RCTs identified by Brown & Molinari (2021) systematic review
- Safety concerns including cardiac complications
- Need for rigorous, large-scale trials with comprehensive safety monitoring

---

## Recommendations

### For Academics
- More robust research methodologies needed
- Larger participant groups and extended follow-up
- Address inherent biases in psychedelic research

### For Policy Makers
- Adaptive regulatory frameworks to support safe clinical use
- Balance innovation with safety requirements

### For Treatment Providers
- Patient-centric, holistic approaches
- Emphasis on integration and aftercare
- Recognition of set and setting as central to outcomes

### For Those Seeking Treatment
- Importance of choosing qualified providers
- Understanding the role of preparation and integration

---

## Methodological Approach

- **Design:** Qualitative inquiry using reflexive thematic analysis (Braun & Clarke)
- **Epistemology:** Critical realist position
- **Data Collection:** Semi-structured interviews with ibogaine practitioners
- **Quality Criteria:** Trustworthiness, credibility, transferability, dependability, confirmability
- **PPI:** Patient and Public Involvement incorporated

---

## Clinical Relevance

This thesis offers valuable insight into the "how" of ibogaine treatment from those delivering it. While not providing efficacy data, it illuminates:

- The diversity of treatment approaches across providers
- The importance of non-pharmacological elements (preparation, setting, integration)
- Practitioner perspectives on what makes treatment effective
- The gap between research evidence and clinical practice

Particularly relevant for protocol development — understanding how experienced providers conceptualise and deliver treatment.
