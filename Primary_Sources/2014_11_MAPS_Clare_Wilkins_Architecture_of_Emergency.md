# Clare Wilkins at MAPS 2014: Architecture of Emergency Using Psychedelics During the Anthropocene Era

> **Source:** MAPS Psychedelic Science Conference, November 2014
> **Speaker:** Clare Wilkins, Director, Pangea Biomedics; GITA Board Member
> **Context:** 9 years post-treatment; presenting evolved treatment philosophy and protocols
> **Video:** [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y_ttCNEKf8E)

---

## Key Themes

1. **Anthropocene Framing** — Mass extinction era parallels emergency approach to psychedelic treatment
2. **Evolution from Flood to Incremental** — Shifted from single large dose to gentle increase while decreasing opiates
3. **Demythologising Ibogaine** — "You're not going to get 10–20 years of therapy in one night"
4. **Total Alkaloid vs Hydrochloride** — Whole plant has modulating factors, like coca vs cocaine
5. **Family-Centred Treatment** — Treating the organism and family system, not just the individual
6. **Integration Crisis** — Most emergency calls relate to self-administration and post-treatment crisis
7. **MAPS Collaboration** — From meeting Rick Doblin at Burning Man to Phase 1/2 trials in Spain
8. **Patient-Centred Philosophy** — "The body's behaviour is the teacher and the true guide"

---

## Historical Context: GITA and Howard Lotsof

Clare references her role as GITA board member and the organisation's Patient Advocacy Program, based on Howard Lotsof's Patient Bill of Rights. She notes the evolution from early 1990s–2000s protocols (rapid detox, large single dose) to extended integrative treatment models.

Key collaborations mentioned:
- **Rick Doblin / MAPS** — Observational study collaboration
- **ICEERS** — Phase 1/2 efficacy and safety trials for methadone dependence (Spain)
- **Ken Alper** — 18-MC study with MindMed (ibogaine analogue research)
- **GITA** — Clinical guidelines co-authorship
