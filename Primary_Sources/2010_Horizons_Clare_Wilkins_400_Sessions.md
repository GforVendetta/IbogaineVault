# Clare Wilkins at Horizons 2010: Over 400 Ibogaine Sessions

> **Format note:** This paper retains its original academic structure. All YAML metadata and cross-references are complete. A full analytical conversion to vault format is planned for v1.1.


> **Source:** Horizons: Perspectives on Psychedelics conference, New York (2010)
> **Speaker:** Clare Wilkins, Founder, Pangea Biomedics
> **Context:** Clare's 40th birthday; 5 years post-ibogaine treatment for 9-year methadone dependency

---

## Key Themes

1. **Outcome Data from 400+ Treatments** — 92% leave detoxed; 18% maintain 6mo-4yr abstinence
2. **Engagement Over Abstinence** — Reframing success as saying "yes" to life, not just "no" to drugs
3. **Family Systems Approach** — The family is the organism; the addict is one digit of the whole hand
4. **MAPS Longitudinal Study Launch** — Protocol development with Beck inventories, addiction severity measures
5. **Nutrition as Medicine** — Orthomolecular approach; "profound root nutrition"
6. **GDNF and Brain Repair** — Neuroplasticity mechanism; receptor repair vs. agonist action
7. **Howard Lotsof Tribute** — "My spiritual father"; acknowledging his legacy

## Transcript

### Personal Introduction

**00:00:20 Clare Wilkins:** Can everybody hear me? My name is Claire Wilkins and I work with ibogaine. And I just first of all want to share that yesterday was my 40th birthday. To be here and presenting on ibogaine, on the work that we've done and to be around my family, my new found family is such an incredible experience, especially considering that 10 years ago when I turned 30, I was living with my mother on something called long acting methadone, which they don't serve at the finer establishments anymore. A fifth of vodka and a pint of Häagen-Dazs ice cream pretty much every day. And so it's a little different this birthday.

### Honouring Howard Lotsof

Its anti-addictive properties were not really discovered until Howard Lotsoff took the medicine, whom I also want to take a moment to honour who is not here with us. And if he were alive, he certainly would be here. Right up until the end he was at conferences, on the phone, doing work. Howard is my spiritual father. Howard came along in my life and scooped me under his wing and I wouldn't be here speaking. I can't stand speaking in public. And for him it was very important that for the work for ibogaine that we do share this information.


### Pangea Overview

Pangea is an inpatient detox service for men and women aged 18 to 72. Our oldest client was my father, who was 72 years old when he came to take the medicine. And a lot of these clients have a history of homelessness, domestic violence, mental disorders, physical disease, substance misuse and social exclusion. Of course, they are incredibly highly functioning as well, which is a testament to their endurance and strength of character to be able to deal with these kinds of situations and also hold a regular full time job, not to mention the full time, no benefits, double time overtime, job of managing dependency.

We use three different versions of the medicine: root bark, total alkaloid extract, and ibogaine hydrochloride. It's used in Gabon for initiation and rites of passage and also for physical and emotional healing.

### Questioning Abstinence

So we started looking at abstinence and there's no clear consensus really on what abstinence is. In the National Treatment Outcome Research Project in England and the Drug Outcome Research Project in Scotland, they had conflicting ideas. The definition of drug free was users who confined their drug use to substitute medication. And another definition was an individual being totally drug free other than alcohol and tobacco use for a period of 90 days prior to their research interview.

For me, who drank in the morning before breakfast, I wouldn't have considered myself clean after getting out of treatment if I was still drinking alcohol. More and more people are on substitute prescribing and less completely abstinent. We're seeing more of this.

14,656 substance misusers treated over a period of six months and not a single person is reported as leaving the program drug free. That was from *Addiction Today* in 2009.

### Harm Reduction Philosophy

With harm reduction, it dilutes the focus on abstinence and encourages more of us to realise that we're all drug users in a sense. We really need to open up and discuss the medicines that we are all taking and not focus so much on the addict. It's usually the illicit chemically dependent one that we focus on, asking them to be "clean"—which is also I think a derogatory term, because what is clean and the opposite of clean is dirty.

There's an incredible amount of guilt just to get their medicine or their drug. And if you're not a legal drug user, that shame—usually the best thing or the only thing that can repair that shame is more of the drug. That's what it was like for me each day.

### Underlying Issues: Depression and Trauma

Depression is huge. Depression is something that we really want to look at with addiction. There's a huge correlation. I want to find out if they have a history of chronic depression or PTSD or chronic anxiety, because these are things we know are going to emerge during the treatment.

We deal with very, very malnourished population. We get people who come in and we say, what is your diet? And it's like coffee and gummy worms. It's 3pm, more coffee, more gummy worms. So at our clinic, we focus a lot on nutrition. We use almost all organic food and we do a lot of nutritional support with IVs and supplements and with food as medicine.

### Ibogaine as Addiction Interrupter

Ibogaine is an addiction interrupter. Dimitri also calls it the relationship interrupter. You take ibogaine, you look at yourself, you take a deep inquiry into your life, and you start looking at not just your addictions but anything that's not serving you.

Ibogaine helps us fall back in love with ourselves and fall back in love with each other. Psychedelics and entheogens do that too. It brings love back into the equation.

### MAPS Study Launch

We have been working with MAPS for a while, and we've finally got a protocol that has put together all of the measures that we believe will be tracking people over a long period of time, where they're assessing their quality of life, addiction severity, depression, and what is contributing to the increase or decrease in those factors. That will convince even the most dubious of skeptics. Our first three clients that we're starting up again with the study is this coming Monday.

The University of Kent has also approached us about research on ibogaine and cognition, as well as nutrition as an adjunct—which I consider ibogaine as nutrition, profound root nutrition.

### Outcome is Engagement, Not Abstinence

Outcome is engagement, not abstinence. You're engaging more in life. What are you saying yes to? If all your life is spent saying no to something, you're still saying no to that giant thing. What are you going to start saying yes to? What are your passions?

If you're going to give up drugs that really made you feel good, it better be worth it.

### Success Data

- **92%** of clients leave our clinic free from the dependencies of their choice
- **18%** of clients are free from their primary substance of abuse from six months after treatment or more (up to four years)
- **42%** of clients use the primary substance within three months

Wes Bennett is here tonight. He did a treatment for crystal methamphetamine four years ago and is still free. What a great birthday present.

### Ibogaine Equals...

- Brain repair
- Insight
- Shame washing
- Empowerment
- Reawakening
- Body intelligence

Without even talking and without even a therapeutic process, there's a cleansing of shame that people feel post-ibogaine. I had done a lot of things in my life that I was not happy about. With ibogaine, it essentially opens up that part of you where you accept yourself. There's a giant acceptance of you completely as a whole being.

All my life, I defined myself as an addict and broken and needing to be fixed. "Hi, my name is Claire. I'm an addict." And it was so much greater than that. The story was so much larger.

### Family Systems

We need to look at the family. Unless we start dealing with the whole family... the more that we can find that place in ourselves, the more that we can connect with drug users and understand them better.

### Mechanism of Action (Q&A with Ken Alper present)

Ken Alper, who knows best as far as the people in the audience—the last slide in his presentation was "we don't know the mechanism of action." But what we do know is that ibogaine doesn't work like an opiate agonist. It has qualities that make you feel like it does, so you go through the process without feeling withdrawal.

What they're saying is that ibogaine comes in and works on the receptors and actually starts repairing the receptors. It increases the GDNF, which is the glial derived nerve growth factor, which is what is produced when you're exercising really hard. It increases dopamine, it increases serotonin.

**I want to really be clear about that though. With chronic depression and any chronic issues, ibogaine does not solve it. These are lifelong issues that need a whole range of care.**

### Closing Quote

> "Misplaced attachment to what cannot satiate the soul is not an error exclusive to addicts, but is the common condition of mankind." — Gabor Maté
