# Clare Wilkins at Breaking Convention 2017: Novel Approach to Methadone Detox

> **Source:** Breaking Convention 2017
> **Speaker:** Clare Wilkins, Director, Pangea Biomedics
> **Context:** Presenting ICEERS pilot study results; promoting EntheoScience Vienna conference

---

## Key Themes

1. **Critique of Flood Dosing** — "Flood is a natural disaster"—the architecture of emergency
2. **ICEERS Pilot Study** — 47-year-old woman, 17 years on methadone, 6-week cumulative protocol
3. **Remote Treatment via Skype** — Clare in Mexico, team in Spain
4. **Cannabis Oil as Adjunct** — Replaced benzodiazepines for sleep
5. **Ibogaine as Adaptogen** — Lower doses create homeostasis rather than pushing hard
6. **Bwiti Traditional Precedent** — Women's incremental dosing practice

---

## ICEERS Pilot Study Summary

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Patient age | 47 years |
| Methadone duration | 17 years |
| Treatment duration | 6 weeks |
| Starting dose | 1.5 mg/kg |
| Peak dose | 10 mg/kg (600 mg) |
| Maximum QTc | 440 ms (at hour 3, 500 mg dose) |
| Outcome at presentation | ~20 months methadone-free |

### Dosing Progression
- Week 1: 1.5 mg/kg
- Week 2: 3 mg/kg
- Week 3-6: Progressive increase to 10 mg/kg

## Transcript

### The Architecture of Emergency

**00:00:15 Clare Wilkins:** My name is Clare Wilkins and I'm the founder and director of Pangea Biomedics, which is an ibogaine clinic in Mexico. I've been working with ibogaine for over a decade, for 12 years in Mexico. I was also treated with ibogaine in 2005 for a nine year methadone dependency.

Ibogaine is pro-arrhythmic, it's bradycardic—which means it lowers your heart rate—it prolongs the QT, it's a hERG channel blocker, it's a cardio stimulant. And if you have any kind of drug in you, it's going to potentiate that drug. I've seen a guy who had a cappuccino, just a little bit of ibogaine in the morning, and he was shaking just from having a bit of coffee.

There are all these contraindications, and I call it **the architecture of emergency** of how ibogaine is approached. We had a team of 12 and we would bring in three to four people. We had hep lock ports, constant heart monitoring, pulse oximetry, the whole thing. And people would arrive and it was like being in triage, like being in emergency.

### Critique of Flood Dosing

There was a lack of fortification and stabilisation. That was how treatment was approached—just come on in, few days, this is a thing, it'll work. And we're withdrawal free. You have to be in withdrawal to get ibogaine. So this whole "withdrawal free" thing is bullshit.

There was little time to develop a therapeutic alliance. People who usually have severe dependencies are treating trauma. Gabor Maté's main theory—that addiction is highly correlated with trauma. And then, "Oh, 10 years of therapy in one night. I'm going to take ibogaine and then I won't be an addict anymore. I'll be reset to a pre-addictive state."

**This whole concept of a flood dose—a flood is a natural disaster.** Tsunami, flood, earthquake. So there's this notion in language on how we work with ibogaine of treating the addict, shock the junkie.

### Ibogaine as Adaptogen

Roman Paskulin did a lot of studying of metabolic stress and the resilience reflected through all body systems. When you're taking ibogaine, it's a huge stress on the body. Then when you're going through detox, it's another stress. And people die.

He also came up with the notion that **ibogaine is an adaptogen**. We work with ibogaine and orthomolecular medicine. If people needed to be lifted up or to go down, it would act like an adaptogen—responding to the cell in an adaptogenic way, resulting in homeostasis.

It also slowed things down so people could actually remember. The way we dosed was longer time, accumulation over time, adding in what was lacking. We did neurotransmitter assessments.

### Traditional Context

There's history in terms of traditional context of the Bwiti and the Mbiri using incremental cumulative dosing. With Bwiti, they do it with women—it's daily and you get to familiarise yourself. It's like going to a country—you learn to say hello, where's the bathroom?

When people start taking ibogaine, they go, "Oh, this isn't so bad." And then they build up. I just worked with someone who was so scared he would see his dead adopted dad who died on his birthday. In this slow dosing, he was just able to really soften up and trust.

### The ICEERS Pilot Study

We did a pilot study with ICEERS in Spain. She was 47. She was dependent upon methadone for 17 years. And she tried to stop on her own numerous times. In Spain and in Europe there are no clinics.

I was in Mexico via Skype and the team was in Spain. I would get up at 3 in the morning as soon as she'd go into withdrawal. What we did was a six-week cumulative approach wherein she used methadone, and then when she would go into withdrawal, we would give ibogaine.

**It's considered unorthodox to administer ibogaine and opiates at the same time.** Most people just take the cushion right out and then throw the ibogaine. "Oh, you still have withdrawals? Here's another booster." It's exhausting to people. They can't sleep. Our patients sleep okay. And they also are able to integrate.

Every week we started with 1.5 mg/kg, then 3 mg/kg, then up to 5 mg/kg. She went up to 600 milligrams, which was 10 mg/kg. And she wanted to rush right away too. But we encouraged her to take her time to process—she was very grateful.

### Safety Data

The QTC was within normal range. The highest QTc was reached with the 500 mg dose at hour three, being 440 milliseconds. She suffered from sleep deprivation. She was glad to have those days of not taking ibogaine so she could sleep.

She only used benzodiazepines at the beginning, then realised she could use cannabis oil. In Spain it's legal and it goes wonderfully with ibogaine—it helps you sleep. She also switched to some opium tincture rather than methadone in the last couple weeks.

### Emotional Integration

The emotional release is so important. When I did ibogaine, from Monday through Friday and then I went home, I saw this eucalyptus tree in front of my house and I just cried and cried because I had never smelled that eucalyptus tree before. My boyfriend at the time looked at me and was like, "When are you going to stop crying when you look at trees?" And I'm like, "I don't want to stop crying when I look at trees."

With ibogaine there are all these threads and networks that you find and that you make with things and people.

### Call to Action

In August she will be two years off methadone. It's really amazing.

The need for changes: incomplete treatments, persistent withdrawal symptoms and cravings, short-acting opiates controversy. Long term studies are necessary to ensure safety and efficacy in larger samples and design ibogaine protocols that can be integrated into existing medical models.

Contact ICEERS if you're part of any kind of ibogaine work and get your clients to just answer some questions so that we can show in the language of science what we already know about ibogaine.
