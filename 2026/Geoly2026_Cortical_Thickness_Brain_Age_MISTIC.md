# Increased cortical thickness and decreased brain age among special operations veterans with blast TBI after a magnesium-ibogaine protocol

**Citation:** Geoly, A.D., Coetzee, J.P., Buchanan, D.M., Struckmann, W., Kim, B., Sridhar, M., Azeez, A., Lissemore, J.I., Cherian, K., Faerman, A., Keynan, J.N., Singal, P., Shanbour, A., Bandeira, I.D., Kratter, I.H., Adamson, M.M., Saggar, M., Rolle, C., & Williams, N.R. (2026). Increased cortical thickness and decreased brain age among special operations veterans with blast TBI after a magnesium-ibogaine protocol. *iScience*. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.isci.2026.115121

> **Note:** Dr. Nolan Williams passed away on October 8, 2025. All authors affirm his significant contributions to the conception, design, and interpretation of this work. This study is dedicated to his memory.

## Abstract

Background: Ibogaine is a psychoactive alkaloid with therapeutic potential which may promote neuroplasticity. Its effects on human brain morphometry are unknown. Methods: Thirty Special Operations Forces veterans with prior blast-induced TBI participated in an observational study in which they received ibogaine co-administered with magnesium. Structural MRIs were collected at baseline (n=25), initial post-treatment (n=25), and 1-month post (n=22). Longitudinal analyses assessed cortical thickness, subcortical volume, and predicted brain age (pBA); estimated from T1 scans. Results: pBA was significantly reduced at 1 month relative to baseline (−1.3 years). Cortical thickness analysis revealed post-treatment increases in 11 regions. Subcortical analyses revealed significant volumetric expansion in 8 regions. Conclusion: Magnesium-ibogaine therapy was associated with increased cortical thickness, subcortical expansion, and reduced pBA at 1 month. Although T1s are sensitive to nonstructural changes, the overall direction of effect is consistent with neuroplastic change.

## Key Findings

- **Cortical thickness increases:** Significant increases in 13 of 62 cortical ROIs from baseline to 1 month (pFDR < 0.05), primarily right-lateralised — right lateral prefrontal (rpORB, rpTRI), temporal (rMGH, rSTG, rENT), parietal (rIPL, rpostC), bilateral lingual (lLING, rLING), bilateral lateral orbitofrontal (lLOF, rLOF), left medial orbitofrontal (lMOF), and right lateral occipital (rLOG)
- **Subcortical volumetric expansion:** Significant expansion in 8 subcortical regions (pFDR < 0.05), including bilateral cerebellar white matter, bilateral basal forebrain, bilateral ventral diencephalon, right hippocampus (transient), and left caudate contraction
- **Predicted brain age reduction:** Mean pBA reduction of 1.3 years from baseline to 1 month (p_holm = 0.045), from 39.4 years (SD 10.2) to 38.5 years (SD 10.4)
- **Normative modelling:** Targeted normative percentile rank increased by +8.70 relative percentile from baseline to 1 month (p_holm < 0.05), trending toward the 50th (healthy) percentile
- **Right hemisphere lateralisation:** 10 of 13 cortical regions with significant thickening were right-lateralised, consistent with prior functional imaging studies of hallucinogens showing preferential right hemisphere effects
- **No significant morphometry–clinical correlations:** Exploratory correlations between structural changes and WHODAS-2.0 did not survive FDR correction, possibly due to limited sample size and uniformly robust clinical improvement

## Methodology

**Design:** Observational pilot study (no control group), part of the broader MISTIC clinical study (Stanford IRB-54095).

**Participants:** 25 male Special Operations Forces veterans (from original cohort of 30) with blast-induced TBI, mean age 44.5 years (SD 7.0). Comorbidities: PTSD (84%), MDD (56%), AUD (48%), anxiety disorder (44%), SUD (24%). Mean 65.2 lifetime TBIs (SD 98.6). All right-handed. 23 mild TBI, 1 moderate, 1 moderately-severe.

**Treatment:** MISTIC protocol — approximately 12.05 mg/kg (SD 1.44) oral ibogaine after IV magnesium sulfate (1 mg) infusion pre- and post-ibogaine administration, at a clinic in Mexico.

**MRI acquisition:** 3 Tesla GE Discovery MR750 scanner, 32-channel head-neck coil, Stanford University. BRAVO sequence (3D, T1-weighted, FOV=256×256mm, matrix=256×256, TR=6.39ms, TE=2.62ms, slice thickness=0.9mm, flip angle=12°). Three timepoints: baseline, initial post-treatment (~4–5 days after baseline), 1-month post-treatment. 22 participants had 1-month data.

**Cortical thickness analysis:** ANTs longitudinal cortical thickness pipeline with OASIS template and Mindboggle OASIS-TRT-20 DKT 31 atlas labels. 62 cortical ROIs (31 left, 31 right). Validated against FreeSurfer 7.4.0 longitudinal pipeline; ANTs showed superior test-retest reliability.

**Subcortical volume analysis:** Log-jacobian determinant maps from ANTs pipeline. 28 subcortical and cerebellar ROIs from Mindboggle volumetric atlas.

**Predicted brain age:** brainageR algorithm applied to T1w MRI scans at each timepoint.

**Normative modelling:** Z-scores computed against 996 healthy male controls from IXI, Kirby, OASIS, NKI, and SRPB datasets (all processed through ANTs).

**Statistical approach:** Linear mixed-effects models (LME) with Wald Chi-Square tests (Type II), FDR correction across regions, Holm-Bonferroni correction for 3 pairwise contrasts. Model: Rᵢ ~ Visit + Age + CES + nrTBIs + eTIV + (1 | Subject). Covariates for pBA model additionally included WM SNR and GM SNR.

### Subcortical Volume Results

Significant volumetric expansion (pFDR < 0.05) in 8 subcortical ROIs:

- **Bilateral cerebellar white matter** — expanded BL→IP, sustained at 1M (left only)
- **Bilateral basal forebrain** — expanded BL→IP, sustained at 1M (left only)
- **Bilateral ventral diencephalon** — expanded BL→IP, sustained at 1M (bilateral)
- **Right hippocampus** — transient expansion BL→IP, returned to baseline at 1M
- **Left caudate** — significant volumetric *contraction* BL→1M (discussed as potentially adaptive — synaptic pruning/efficiency)

| 1M − BL | −1.256 | 0.496 | −2.531 | 0.045* |

LME ANOVA: significant main effects of Visit (Χ² = 6.967, p = 0.031), chronological age (Χ² = 12.25, p < 0.001), and GM SNR (Χ² = 4.796, p = 0.029) on pBA.

Notably, participant pBAs were already lower than chronological ages at baseline (mean pBA 39.4 years vs. mean chronological age 44.5 years), possibly due to exceptional physical fitness required for SOF selection.

### Normative Modelling Results

- **Targeted normative percentile rank** (13 significant regions): significant main effect of visit (Χ² = 13.302, pFDR = 0.001). BL→IP: +7.49 percentile (p_holm < 0.05). BL→1M: +8.70 percentile (p_holm < 0.05).
- **Whole-brain normative percentile rank**: significant main effect (Χ² = 6.724, p = 0.035). Trending increases: BL→IP: +4.92 percentile (p_holm = 0.077). BL→1M: +5.55 percentile (p_holm = 0.077).

## Neuroplasticity Mechanisms

The authors hypothesise that the structural changes may reflect ibogaine-induced neuroplasticity, supported by:

- **Neurotrophic factor upregulation:** Animal studies demonstrate ibogaine upregulates GDNF, BDNF, and nerve growth factor (NGF), which may promote neural repair at the cellular level
- **NMDA antagonism:** Both ibogaine and magnesium act as NMDA antagonists, with potentially additive effects
- **SERT inhibition:** Ibogaine is a noncompetitive inhibitor of the serotonin transporter, potentially contributing to antidepressant effects
- **Multi-receptor pharmacology:** Ibogaine interacts with glutamatergic, nicotinic, sigma, mu/kappa opioid, serotonergic, and dopaminergic systems
- **Cerebellar white matter expansion:** Bilateral expansion without grey matter contraction may indicate remyelination rather than toxicity, supported by preclinical myelination marker upregulation following ibogaine

The right-hemispheric lateralisation of cortical thickening is consistent with prior functional imaging studies of hallucinogens showing preferential right hemisphere cerebral blood flow and glucose uptake increases. The affected regions (prefrontal, temporal, parietal, orbitofrontal) overlap with areas showing pathological thinning in blast TBI populations, suggesting a putative reversal-like phenomenon.

## Cerebellar Safety Data

All participants experienced transient cerebellar signs (mild ataxia, intention tremor) during MISTIC therapy, resolving within 24 hours. Despite historical concerns from rat studies showing high-dose ibogaine-induced Purkinje cell degeneration, this study found bilateral cerebellar white matter *expansion* with no grey matter contraction. The authors note that T1w MRI resolution (0.9mm) cannot approach the specificity of post-mortem histology (microns), precluding definitive claims about neurotoxicity. At least one prior post-mortem neuropathological examination of a patient who used ibogaine multiple times found no evidence of ibogaine-related neurotoxicity.

## Clinical Implications

This study provides the first group-level assessment of MISTIC's effects on gross brain morphometry in humans. Key clinical implications include:

1. **Structural neuroplasticity evidence:** The cortical thickening and subcortical expansion patterns are consistent with neural repair processes, complementing the clinical and cognitive improvements reported in [[2024/Cherian2024_Magnesium_Ibogaine_TBI]]
2. **Brain age as outcome metric:** The significant pBA reduction demonstrates a potentially useful biomarker for tracking ibogaine treatment response, analogous to how pBA has been used in TBI populations to chart dementia risk
3. **MISTIC safety signal:** Cerebellar white matter expansion without grey matter contraction provides reassurance against concerns from historical rat neurotoxicity studies
4. **Reversal of TBI pathology:** The cortical thickening in regions specifically documented to show thinning in blast TBI (bilateral lateral orbitofrontal, right pars orbitalis, right pars triangularis) is suggestive of targeted repair
5. **Comparability with other interventions:** The magnitude of cortical thickness increases is comparable to those seen with ECT, sertraline, exercise training, and testosterone replacement therapy

## Limitations

As noted by the authors:

- **No control condition** — cannot rule out placebo effects, though unlikely to cause structural brain changes directly; could promote healthier lifestyles indirectly
- **No biological samples** — no serum data on BDNF, GDNF, or other neurotrophic factors to confirm hypothesised mechanisms
- **Limited sample size** (n=25, 22 at 1 month) — underpowered to detect small-to-moderate effects and morphometry–clinical correlations
- **T1 sensitivity to nonstructural changes** — longitudinal T1 scans may detect tissue water content changes rather than true structural neuroplasticity
- **Single-sex sample** — all male; cannot assess sex effects
- **Population specificity** — highly trained SOF veterans with superior physical fitness are not representative of general TBI or general ibogaine treatment populations
- **TBI diagnosis limitation** — based on clinical history/recollection; definitive blast TBI diagnosis requires post-mortem histology
- **Unexpected baseline pBA** — participants had younger-than-chronological pBA at baseline despite multiple TBIs, possibly due to exceptional cardiovascular fitness or scanner properties
- **Normative modelling limitations** — unable to apply sophisticated site-harmonisation due to lack of healthy controls in sample
- **Time frame** — only 1-month follow-up; long-term durability unknown
