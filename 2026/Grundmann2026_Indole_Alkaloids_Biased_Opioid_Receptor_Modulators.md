# Indole Alkaloids as Biased Opioid Receptor Modulators

**Citation:** Grundmann O, Henderson A. Indole Alkaloids as Biased Opioid Receptor Modulators. *Preprints.org* 2026. doi:10.20944/preprints202601.2298.v1

**Status:** Preprint (not peer-reviewed)

---

## Key Findings

Indole alkaloids (ibogaine, noribogaine, mitragynine) function as **biased G-protein coupled receptor agonists** at μ- and κ-opioid receptors that do NOT recruit β-arrestin. This mechanistic distinction is clinically significant because β-arrestin recruitment is associated with respiratory depression, tolerance, constipation, and dependence — the major adverse effects of classical opioids like morphine and fentanyl.

---

## Central Thesis

The indole ring structure allows these alkaloids to interact with different amino acid residues compared to morphinans, resulting in selective activation of the analgesic G-protein pathway without triggering the β-arrestin pathway responsible for adverse effects.

## Molecular Mechanism

The biased activation mechanism involves:

1. **Glutamine-124 reorientation** — The Gln124 residue in transmembrane region 7 reorients to accommodate the indole ring system
2. **Lack of Tyr326 interaction** — In classical opioids (e.g., lofentanil), Gln124–Tyr326 interaction triggers β-arrestin recruitment; indole alkaloids avoid this
3. **Distinct binding pocket** — Interactions with Asn127, Ile144, Trp133 (TM2/TM3) and Met151, Asp147 (indole methoxy group)
4. **Helix 8 intracellular loop** — Different conformational changes in this region distinguish biased vs unbiased activation

## Non-Opioid Targets

The review notes that indole alkaloids also bind other receptors, contributing to complex pharmacology:

**Ibogaine:** 5-HT2A, 5-HT3, D3, NMDA, M1, M2, nicotinic (NN)

**Noribogaine:** NMDA, M1, M2, nicotinic (NN)

**Mitragynine:** 5-HT1A, 5-HT2A, 5-HT2B, α2A, α1A, D2

---

## Clinical Safety Notes

The review acknowledges:
- Ibogaine's QT prolongation concern makes it "unlikely to achieve drug approval"
- CYP2D6 poor metabolisers may need dose reduction
- Kratom (mitragynine source) associated with dependence, seizures, and cardiovascular events at high doses

---

## Implications for Drug Development

The authors propose indole alkaloids as scaffolds for novel biased opioid receptor modulators with improved therapeutic indices. The structural features conferring biased activation could be exploited to develop analgesics with reduced respiratory depression liability.

---

## Limitations

- Preprint, not peer-reviewed
- Review synthesises existing data; no original research
- β-arrestin recruitment at μ-opioid receptors specifically not yet demonstrated for ibogaine/noribogaine (only κ-opioid data available)
