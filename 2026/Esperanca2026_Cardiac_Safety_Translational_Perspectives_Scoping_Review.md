# Ibogaine: Therapeutic Potential, Cardiac Safety, and Translational Perspectives in the Treatment of Substance Use Disorders—A Scoping Review

**Citation:** Esperança, M. P., Gomes, N. G. M., & Campos, M. G. (2026). Ibogaine: Therapeutic Potential, Cardiac Safety, and Translational Perspectives in the Treatment of Substance Use Disorders—A Scoping Review. *Molecules*, 31(3), 545. https://doi.org/10.3390/molecules31030545

## Abstract

This scoping review synthesises preclinical and clinical evidence on ibogaine for substance use disorders (SUDs), mapping therapeutic efficacy, cardiac safety, and translational constraints using PRISMA-ScR methodology across PubMed/MEDLINE, SciELO, and Cochrane databases (1901–2025). Twenty-two studies met inclusion criteria (9 therapeutic efficacy, 10 safety/toxicology, 3 preclinical). Human observational studies consistently report acute withdrawal suppression and craving reduction, but sustained abstinence outcomes remain uncertain due to methodological limitations. Cardiac safety evidence reveals mechanistically coherent, exposure-dependent risk including QTc prolongation (values exceeding 600 ms), malignant ventricular arrhythmias, and at least 19 fatalities temporally associated with ibogaine exposure. The authors position ibogaine as a lead compound informing rational design of next-generation anti-addictive therapeutics rather than a viable therapeutic endpoint.

## Key Findings

### Therapeutic Evidence (9 Human Studies)
- Acute withdrawal suppression is the most reproducible finding across opioid use disorder cohorts
- Craving reduction and mood disturbance attenuation consistently reported
- Sustained abstinence outcomes are heterogeneous, poorly verified, and methodologically constrained
- No randomised controlled trials exist; all efficacy data from open-label observational studies

### Cardiac Safety Evidence (10 Human Studies)
- QTc prolongation exceeding 600 ms documented in multiple case reports (up to 714 ms)
- Malignant ventricular arrhythmias: torsades de pointes, ventricular tachycardia, ventricular fibrillation
- Cardiac arrest requiring defibrillation reported in clinical settings
- 19 fatalities in Alper 2012 forensic review, with additional cases in subsequent reports
- Prolonged risk window: QTc prolongation persisting 7–12 days post-exposure
- Noribogaine metabolite implicated as driver of delayed cardiotoxicity via lipophilic tissue sequestration

### Cardiotoxicity Mechanisms
- hERG/IKr potassium channel blockade → QT/QTc prolongation → torsades de pointes substrate
- CYP2D6-dependent metabolism: poor metabolisers have increased exposure to ibogaine + noribogaine
- Concentration–QTc relationship: noribogaine plasma levels directly correlate with ventricular repolarisation delay
- Lipophilic tissue sequestration: adipose reservoir enables slow redistribution, sustaining low-level exposure beyond the acute phase

### Preclinical Evidence (3 Studies)
- Dose-dependent cerebellar neurotoxicity in rats: Purkinje cell degeneration at ≥50 mg/kg i.p.
- No-observed-adverse-effect level (NOAEL): 25 mg/kg
- Species-specific vulnerability (rats affected, mice not); translational relevance uncertain

### Pharmacodynamics
- Polypharmacological profile engaging dopaminergic, serotonergic, opioid, glutamatergic, and nicotinic systems
- Neurotrophic signalling: GDNF/BDNF induction in VTA/NAc; mTOR-dependent synaptic plasticity
- Habenular–interpeduncular axis: α3β4 nAChR antagonism reduces aversion-driven relapse across substance classes
- Noribogaine: μ/κ-opioid partial agonism, SERT inhibition; prolonged exposure drives sustained effects and cardiac risk

### Analogue Development
- 18-methoxycoronaridine (18-MC): retains anti-addictive efficacy with reduced hallucinogenic and cardiotoxic liability
- Tabernanthalog (TBG): non-hallucinogenic, water-soluble, psychoplastogenic
- Oxa-iboga derivatives (benzofuran scaffold): enhanced KOR engagement with attenuated cardiac risk
- Second-generation analogues demonstrate proof-of-concept for uncoupling efficacy from toxicity

## Methodology

PRISMA-ScR (Scoping Reviews) framework:
- **Databases:** PubMed/MEDLINE (n=152), SciELO (n=31), Cochrane (n=16)
- **Temporal scope:** 1901–2025
- **Final inclusion:** 22 studies (9 therapeutic efficacy, 10 safety/toxicology, 3 preclinical)
- **Exclusion:** 112 records (not ibogaine-focused, no empirical data, outside scope)
- **No meta-analysis** due to heterogeneous study designs and outcome measures

## Chemical Characterisation

- **Ibogaine:** Monoterpenoid indole alkaloid (C₂₀H₂₆N₂O); rigid polycyclic scaffold with indole-tetrahydroazepine-bicyclic cage structure
- **Phytochemistry:** Predominant alkaloid in *Tabernanthe iboga* root bark (1.93% w/w); co-occurs with noribogaine, coronaridine, tabernanthine, voacangine, caffeoylquinic acids
- **Noribogaine:** Primary active metabolite via CYP2D6-mediated O-demethylation; μ/κ-opioid partial agonist, SERT inhibitor; prolonged half-life drives sustained therapeutic and cardiotoxic effects

## Regulatory Context

- No regulatory approval in the EU; no EMA/HMPC monograph for traditional use
- WHO acknowledges therapeutic interest but emphasises lack of robust safety/efficacy data
- Absence of standardised pharmaceutical formulations; variable alkaloid content in plant materials
- Clinical trials required for full medicinal development; simplified registration pathways unavailable
- Phytochemical standardisation needed: marker-based ibogaine quantification + chromatographic fingerprinting

## Clinical Implications

This review positions ibogaine as a pharmacological lead compound rather than a clinically deployable therapeutic — a distinction with direct relevance for clinical practice:

1. **Cardiac risk is mechanistically coherent and clinically significant** — not idiosyncratic or dismissible. The hERG blockade → QTc prolongation → arrhythmia cascade is well-characterised and exposure-dependent.
2. **The prolonged risk window (7–12 days)** demands extended cardiac monitoring beyond the acute dosing period, supporting existing monitoring protocols.
3. **CYP2D6 polymorphism** creates interindividual variability in safety margins — poor metabolisers face disproportionate risk. Pharmacogenomic screening is warranted.
4. **Noribogaine as the principal cardiac risk driver** supports strategies targeting metabolite exposure (dose fractionation, electrolyte optimisation per MISTIC protocol).
5. **Analogue pipeline** (18-MC, TBG, oxa-iboga) offers future therapeutic alternatives with potentially uncoupled efficacy/toxicity profiles.

## Limitations

- Scoping review methodology maps evidence breadth but does not assess quality or synthesise effect sizes
- No randomised controlled trials in the evidence base; all efficacy data from open-label observational studies
- Safety reports confounded by pre-existing cardiac disease, polysubstance use, electrolyte disturbances, non-standardised preparations
- Heterogeneous outcome measures and incomplete follow-up across therapeutic studies
- Attrition and self-report bias in substance use outcome measures
- Preclinical neurotoxicity data (cerebellar Purkinje cell degeneration) of uncertain translational relevance
