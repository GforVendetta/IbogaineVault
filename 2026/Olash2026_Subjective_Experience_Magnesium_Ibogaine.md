# Accelerated Recovery Using Magnesium Ibogaine: Characterizing the Subjective Experience

**Citation:** Olash C, Buchanan DM, Brown R, Faerman A, Cherian K, Lin G, Spiegel D, Gross JJ, Williams N. Accelerated recovery using magnesium ibogaine: characterizing the subjective experience of its rapid healing from neuropsychiatric disorders. *npj Mental Health Research* 2026. doi:10.1038/s44184-026-00185-7

## Key Finding

This is the **qualitative companion study** to [Cherian et al. (2024)](../2024/Cherian2024_Magnesium_Ibogaine_TBI.md), examining the subjective phenomenology of the same 30 US Special Operations veterans who showed massive clinical improvements (Cohen's d > 3.0) following magnesium-ibogaine treatment.

The central thesis: **ibogaine functions as "accelerated auto-psychotherapy"** — a single-session experience that compresses multiple psychotherapeutic processes into hours rather than months.

---

## Study Design

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Cohort | 30 male US Special Operations veterans with TBI |
| Method | Constructivist grounded theory analysis |
| Data | Post-session written narratives (3 open-ended questions) |
| Timing | Average 13.3 days post-treatment (range 1–86 days) |
| Setting | Ambio Life Treatment Center, Tijuana, Mexico |
| Dose | Up to 14 mg/kg ibogaine with pre/post IV magnesium sulfate |

### Baseline Comorbidities
- 23/30 met criteria for PTSD
- 15/30 for Major Depressive Disorder
- 14/30 for anxiety disorder
- 15/30 for Alcohol Use Disorder
- Average 38.6 TBIs (mainly mild, blast-related)
- Average 5.5 combat deployments

## Four Emergent Phenomenological Domains

### 1. Trauma Processing and Dialogic Insight

Re-encountering traumatic memories with radically altered affect — often suffused with love, forgiveness, and acceptance.

> *"I remembered the door gunner from the helicopter shoot-down. I carried his body out. The stains were on my uniform; it haunted me for all these years… When I remembered to look for him, he appeared. He was smiling and happy. It released me, I felt that pain go away, and it was immense."*

Subthemes:
- Re-encounter and re-appraisal of trauma
- Therapeutic dialogue (ibogaine experienced as interactive guide)
- Insight into maladaptive patterns

### 2. Altered Self and Mystical Connection

> *"The concrete borders of my sense of self were obliterated. I became part of everything. Who I was was centered in my heart and was projected into the universe."*

Subthemes:
- Unattached observer / out-of-body experience
- Ego-loss
- Aligning selves (integration of fragmented parts)
- Mystical connection
- Meaning making

### 3. Emotional Resolution and Compassionate Reconnection

> *"All the pain came out. Love for family and friends rushed in. It was overwhelming and lasted several minutes."*

Subthemes:
- Rediscovery of love
- Compassion and forgiveness (toward self and others)

### 4. Embodied Brain Healing and After-Effects

> *"What was happening in my brain was as if all my neurons and synapses were firing and recharging and healing."*

Subthemes:
- Felt sense of neural repair
- Post-treatment after-effects (reduced cravings, improved attention, changed habits)

## Theoretical Framework: "Accelerated Auto-Psychotherapy"

The authors propose ibogaine compresses established psychotherapeutic processes into a single session:

| Psychotherapeutic Parallel | Ibogaine Experience |
|---------------------------|---------------------|
| **CBT** — Cognitive restructuring | Rapid reappraisal of core beliefs through experiential evidence |
| **Exposure therapy** — Fear extinction | Traumatic memories revisited with agency and felt safety |
| **Mentalization-Based Therapy** — Perspective-taking | Enhanced empathy, understanding others' viewpoints |
| **Humanistic therapy** — Unconditional positive regard | Intrinsic empathic climate, felt sense of being supported |
| **Logotherapy** — Meaning-making | Renewed sense of purpose and existential clarity |
| **ACT** — Self-as-context | Observer perspective, decoupling thoughts from identity |

---

## Neurobiological Correlates Proposed

The paper links phenomenology to candidate neural mechanisms:

- **κ-opioid agonism** → Dream-like (oneiric) quality; reduced top-down priors
- **NMDA antagonism** → Dissociative/ketamine-like out-of-body sensations
- **SERT inhibition (noribogaine)** → Anxiolytic, mood-stabilising effects
- **5-HT receptor engagement** → Possible oxytocin release, social bonding
- **DMN modulation** → Ego dissolution, reduced self-referential processing

The phenomenology parallels EEG findings: gamma power with reduced coherence — a signature reminiscent of REM sleep.

---

## Clinical Implications

The qualitative findings help explain the unprecedented effect sizes (d > 3.0) in the Cherian 2024 quantitative study:

1. **Trauma material surfaces and resolves** in a single session
2. **Emotional reconnection** restores social bonds damaged by PTSD
3. **Meaning-making** addresses existential dimension of moral injury
4. **Felt sense of neural repair** may reflect actual neuroplastic changes

---

## Limitations

- Open-label, uncontrolled observational design
- Retrospective narratives (recall bias)
- Self-written responses (no interview probing)
- All-male Special Operations cohort (limits generalisability)
- No direct phenomenology-outcome correlation analysis
