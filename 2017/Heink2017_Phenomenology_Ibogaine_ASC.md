# Examination of the Phenomenology of the Ibogaine Treatment Experience: Role of Altered States of Consciousness and Psychedelic Experiences

**Citation:** Heink, A., Katsikas, S., & Lange-Altman, T. (2017). Examination of the Phenomenology of the Ibogaine Treatment Experience: Role of Altered States of Consciousness and Psychedelic Experiences. *Journal of Psychoactive Drugs*. DOI: 10.1080/02791072.2017.1290855

## Abstract

Psychedelic drugs have historically been used for ritualistic purposes and to help individuals gain insight. Ibogaine, a naturally occurring psychoactive substance, has been reported to have anti-addictive properties that aid in the treatment of substance use disorders. An online survey obtained retrospective data from individuals who used ibogaine in the past. Individuals who used ibogaine tended to describe thematically similar experiences post-treatment. This study adds to the literature by using the 5d-ASC, a psychometrically sound measure of altered states of consciousness (ASCs), to examine the ASCs induced by ibogaine and discusses the demographic characteristics of those who seek ibogaine treatment (N = 27). The study also examined several aspects of ibogaine treatment experience, including reasons for seeking treatment, course of treatment, and treatment outcome. Results indicated a positive correlation between the various dimensions of the ASCs and the outcome (ability to make changes in one's life, cravings, and how changed the person was as a result of ibogaine treatment). While this study is limited in generalizability due to high attrition and low sample size, it deepens the understanding of the phenomenological experience of ibogaine and explores the possible utility of ibogaine in the treatment of substance use disorders.

## Key Findings

- Ibogaine induced significantly more intense ASCs than high-dose psilocybin (315 μg/kg) across all six 5d-ASC dimensions
- Visual Restructuralisation (VRS) positively correlated with how "changed" participants felt (rho = .679, p = .01)
- Global ASC (G-ASC) positively correlated with perceived change (rho = .419, p = .05)
- Auditory Alterations (AUA) positively correlated with importance of visions for making life changes (rho = .433, p = .05)
- 96% reported ibogaine reduced withdrawal symptoms; 68% reported "dramatic" reduction
- 92% reported craving reduction in first 4 weeks; 91% reported continued reduction for months
- No participants reported being worse off after the ibogaine experience
- No significant correlation between ASCs and perceived effectiveness, suggesting ibogaine may be effective regardless of ASC intensity
- Withdrawal symptom reduction correlated with cravings at 4 weeks (rho = .720, p = .01) and months (rho = .675, p = .01)
- Craving reduction at 4 weeks strongly correlated with craving reduction at months (rho = .972, p = .01)

## Methodology

### Study Design
Online retrospective survey using anonymous self-report questionnaire. IRB-approved by Spalding University.

### Participants
- N = 27 completed surveys (100 started; 392 viewed informed consent)
- Mean age: 35.11 (SD = 7.85)
- 96% White/Caucasian/European
- 56% male
- From 10 different countries
- Education: 7% some high school, 35% some college, 11% technical certification, 8% associate degree, 39% bachelor's degree or higher
- 31% reported multiple ibogaine treatments

### Recruitment
Survey link sent to 14 ibogaine treatment centres; posted on 12 online forums and discussion boards including Facebook and an ibogaine-specific threaded bulletin board. Moderator permission obtained as needed.

### Instruments

**Demographics Questionnaire (DQ):** Age, race/ethnicity, country of origin, country of treatment, education, language.

**5-Dimensional Altered States of Consciousness Rating Scale (5d-ASC)** (Dittrich, 1998): Second revision of the English translation of Abnormer Psychischer Zustand (APZ). Sliding scale from 0 ("No, not more than usual") to 100 ("Yes, much more than usual"). Six primary dimensions:
- Oceanic Boundlessness (OBN)
- Dread of Ego-Dissolution (DED)
- Visual Restructuralisation (VRS)
- Auditory Alterations (AUA)
- Vigilance Reduction (VIR)
- Global Altered State of Consciousness (G-ASC)

Reliability: internal consistency α = .88 to .95 for individual dimensions; total scale α = .96 (Dittrich, Lamparter, & Maurer, 2011). Validated to distinguish between amount administered and type of psychedelic substance (Studerus et al., 2010).

**Additional items:** Multiple-choice questions on administration process, reasons for seeking treatment, course of treatment, physical and psychedelic effects. Self-reported treatment outcome questions on Likert scales (see Treatment Outcome Measures below).

## Treatment Outcome Measures

Seven self-reported treatment outcome factors were assessed on Likert scales:

| Factor | Question | Scale |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Withdrawal | Effect of ibogaine on withdrawal symptoms | 1 = dramatically reduced → 5 = dramatically increased |
| Cravings (weeks) | Effect on drug cravings in first 4 weeks | 1 = dramatically reduced → 5 = dramatically increased; 6 = not applicable |
| Cravings (months) | Effect on drug cravings in months following | 1 = dramatically reduced → 5 = dramatically increased; 6 = not applicable |
| Importance | Importance of visions/hallucinations to ability to make life changes | 1 = not at all important → 4 = extremely important; 5 = did not experience |
| Changed | How changed as a result of ibogaine | 1 = much worse → 5 = much better |
| Expectation | Actual experience compared to expectations | 1 = completely different → 4 = very similar |
| Effectiveness | How effective for main treatment issue | 1 = not at all helpful → 5 = extremely helpful |

## Experience Reports

### Administration Context
- Treatment settings: medical treatment facilities (22%), someone else's home (26%), motel rooms (22%), counselling centres (15%), own home (11%), other (4%)
- Administered by: medical professional (33%), non-medical professional with ibogaine experience (33%), counselling professional (15%), self-administered or other (remainder)
- 70% reported questions were answered completely by treatment providers

### Reasons for Seeking Treatment
- Substance abuse (drugs or alcohol): 78%
- Spiritual growth: 8%
- Other (alcohol abuse, chronic pain, anxiety, self-awareness): 14%

### Expectations
- 59% described experience as "completely different" from expected
- 4% described it as "different"
- 92% experienced effects within the first hour after administration

### Physical Effects (percentage endorsing at least "some")
- Buzzing in ears: 89%
- Difficulty trying to move: 81%
- Feeling physically heavy: 77%
- Decreased control of movements: 70%
- Lightheadedness/dizziness: 56%
- Nausea: 52%
- Emotional distress: 46%
- Hesitant to take the next dose: 35%
- Vomiting: 30%
- Diarrhoea: 19%

### Psychedelic Effects
- Heightened sense of perception: 88%
- Any hallucinations: 97%
- 63% indicated "quite a bit" or "almost all" of experience included hallucinations/visions

**Perceptual changes:**
- Change in perception of time: 93%
- Change in perception of light: 89%
- Change in perception of sound: 85%
- Change in perception of body: 82%
- Change in perception of space: 78%

**Hallucinatory content:**
- Hearing a buzzing noise: 96%
- Hallucinations of living people: 77%
- Visions from own childhood: 77%
- Visuals of animals: 62%
- Hearing drums beating: 54%
- Visions of television screens: 46%
- Hallucinations of deceased people: 38%
- Communication with the deceased: 27%

**Meaning and control:**
- Visions contained symbolism: 74%
- Visions had obvious personal meaning: 74%
- Visions told a story: 70%
- Able to exit visions by opening eyes (at least "a little"): 78%
- Returned to same vision upon re-closing eyes: 78%
- 52% felt unable to control visions "at all"

### Insight
- Past-related insight: 100%
- Meaning of life: 92%
- Evolution of the universe: 89%
- Creation: 88%
- Evolution of humanity: 85%
- Death/after-life: 85%
- Relief from guilt: 85%
- Evolution of the animal world: 77%

### Communication
- 66% found it at least "a little" difficult to communicate with counsellors/staff
- 48% stated staff "check-ins" interfered with their experience

## Treatment Outcomes

### Withdrawal
- 96% found ibogaine had a reductive effect on immediate post-treatment withdrawal symptoms
- 68% indicated ibogaine "dramatically reduced" withdrawal symptoms

### Cravings
- 92% reported ibogaine "reduced" or "dramatically reduced" drug cravings in first 4 weeks
- 91% reported craving reduction continued for months after treatment
- 49% indicated "dramatic" reduction in cravings at months follow-up

### Changed
- 67% stated they were "much better" because of the ibogaine experience
- 30% stated they were "better"
- 0% indicated being "worse" or "much worse"

### Importance of Visions
- 41% indicated visions/hallucinations were "extremely important" to ability to make life changes
- 82% stated they were at least "a little" important

### Effectiveness
- 76% rated ibogaine as "extremely helpful" for main treatment issue
- 16% rated it as "very helpful"

## 5d-ASC Data

### Ibogaine vs Psilocybin Comparison

| Dimension | Ibogaine (N = 27) | Psilocybin (N = 42, 315 μg/kg) |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------------------|
| OBN (Oceanic Boundlessness) | 62.45 | 41.72 |
| DED (Dread of Ego-Dissolution) | 41.96 | 22.53 |
| VRS (Visual Restructuralisation) | 66.23 | 44.27 |
| VIR (Vigilance Reduction) | 48.18 | 41.59 |
| AUA (Auditory Alterations) | 54.87 | 11.53 |
| G-ASC (Global ASC) | 168.19 | 108.52 |

*Note: Psilocybin data from Studerus (2012), high-dose condition (315 μg/kg, n = 42). Ibogaine scores exceeded psilocybin across all dimensions, with the largest discrepancy in Auditory Alterations (54.87 vs 11.53).*

### Significant Correlations

| Variable 1 | Variable 2 | rho | p |
|-----------|-----------|-----|---|
| Withdrawal symptoms | Cravings (4 weeks) | .720 | .01 |
| Withdrawal symptoms | Cravings (months) | .675 | .01 |
| Withdrawal symptoms | Effectiveness (main issue) | −.546 | .01 |
| Cravings (4 weeks) | Importance of ASCs for change | −.391 | .05 |
| Cravings (months) | Changed | −.448 | .05 |
| Cravings (4 weeks) | Cravings (months) | .972 | .01 |
| VRS | Changed | .679 | .01 |
| G-ASC | Changed | .419 | .05 |
| AUA | Importance (visions for life changes) | .433 | .05 |

### Non-significant relationships
- No significant correlations between withdrawal symptoms and any 5d-ASC variables
- No significant correlations between cravings (weeks or months) and 5d-ASC variables
- No significant correlation between perceived effectiveness and 5d-ASC variables

## Clinical Implications

This study provides the first standardised psychometric quantification of ibogaine-induced ASCs using the 5d-ASC, establishing that ibogaine produces more intense altered states than high-dose psilocybin across all measured dimensions. The finding that ASC intensity (VRS, G-ASC) correlates with perceived change but not perceived effectiveness suggests a dissociation: ibogaine may reduce withdrawal and cravings through pharmacological mechanisms independent of the psychedelic experience, while the subjective experience serves as a catalyst for broader psychological transformation.

For integration practice, the finding that 48% reported staff check-ins interfered with the experience has direct implications for clinical protocols regarding timing and frequency of monitoring interactions during the acute phase. The correlation between auditory alterations (AUA) and perceived importance of visions for life changes suggests that the auditory dimension of the ibogaine experience may be underappreciated relative to visual phenomena in therapeutic integration work.

The high endorsement of insight across multiple existential domains (meaning of life, creation, death, evolution) alongside 85% reporting relief from guilt underscores the importance of post-treatment integration support to help individuals process these experiences therapeutically.

## Limitations

- Retrospective self-report survey design
- Small sample size (N = 27 completers from 100 starts and 392 consent views — 73% attrition from start, 93% from consent viewing)
- Homogeneous sample (96% White/Caucasian/European)
- No control group or pre-treatment baseline measures
- Variable treatment settings and providers (no standardised administration protocol)
- Potential self-selection bias — completers may differ systematically from non-completers
- No objective outcome measures (e.g., drug screens)
- Psilocybin comparison data drawn from a different study population (Studerus, 2012) rather than direct head-to-head comparison
