# Fatalities after taking ibogaine in addiction treatment could be related to sudden cardiac death caused by autonomic dysfunction

**Citation:** Maas, U. & Strubelt, S. (2006). Fatalities after taking ibogaine in addiction treatment could be related to sudden cardiac death caused by autonomic dysfunction. *Medical Hypotheses*, 67, 960–964. doi:10.1016/j.mehy.2005.12.035

## Abstract

The authors hypothesise that ibogaine-related fatalities are sudden cardiac deaths resulting from dysregulation of the autonomic nervous system in combination with psychological stress. Ibogaine affects the autonomic nervous system through multiple neurotransmitter systems and stimulation of the cerebellar fastigial nucleus, which may have long-lasting effects. The risk of cardiac arrhythmias is increased during sympathetic overstimulation or when sudden left-hemispheric sympathetic activation occurs during a phase of high parasympathetic tone. At least eight fatalities had been recorded, often occurring days after ingestion or following surprisingly small doses. The paper draws extensively on Gabonese Bwiti healing practice, where traditional healers prevent these risks by maintaining patients in a right-hemispheric hypnotic trance state for days, effectively dampening the fright reactions that could trigger lethal arrhythmias.

## Key Findings

- **Autonomic dysregulation hypothesis:** Ibogaine fatalities are proposed as sudden unexplained death syndrome (SUDS) caused by ventricular fibrillation triggered by autonomic nervous system imbalance
- **Fastigial nucleus mechanism:** Ibogaine stimulates cerebellar Purkinje cells and the fastigial nucleus, which modulates autonomic function — effects may persist for weeks after stimulation
- **Dose-dependent risk profile:** High doses (>10 mg/kg) may paradoxically be safer during peak effects due to strong parasympathetic dominance (vagal "feigned death" state), while low doses (<10 mg/kg) carry greater risk from sympathetic stimulation
- **Post-peak vulnerability window:** The period after peak ibogaine effects, when exhaustion combines with high vagal tone, creates vulnerability to sudden left-hemispheric sympathetic stimulation from startle responses
- **Lateralised cardiac innervation:** Left-hemispheric sympathetic stimulation can drive ventricular myocardium independently of sinus node rate, potentially causing ventricular extrasystoles and torsade de pointes
- **At least 8 fatalities documented:** Several occurred days after ingestion or after doses well below the therapeutic 20–30 mg/kg range; at least two had pre-existing cardiac conditions
- **Traditional protective protocol:** Gabonese healers isolate initiates, maintain right-hemispheric trance through ritual music (5–6 Hz theta rhythm), and observe a minimum three-day critical period — practices consistent with preventing autonomic-triggered arrhythmias

## Methodology

Narrative hypothesis paper synthesising evidence from autonomic neuroscience, cardiac electrophysiology, ethnographic fieldwork in Gabon, and ibogaine pharmacology. The authors integrate their own ethnographic research on Bwiti initiation ceremonies with established cardiac arrhythmia mechanisms. No original clinical or experimental data are presented; the paper proposes a testable theoretical framework.

## Autonomic Nervous System Mechanisms

Ibogaine affects the autonomic nervous system through multiple convergent pathways:

**Direct neurotransmitter effects:**
- Inhibits cholinesterase → increases both parasympathetic and sympathetic tone
- Nicotinic receptor antagonist and sigma-2 receptor agonist → weakens autonomic functions
- Blocks calcium channels → reduces synaptic acetylcholine transmission
- Muscarinic receptor agonist → increases parasympathetic function

**Indirect cerebellar pathway:**
- Stimulates Purkinje cells and fastigial nucleus
- Fastigial nucleus modulates autonomic responses in life-threatening situations
- Effects on autonomic nervous system may persist for weeks after cerebellar stimulation
- Context-dependent: can trigger either sympathetic activation (fight/flight) or parasympathetic dominance (feigned death/freeze)

## Cardiac Arrhythmia Mechanisms

The paper identifies several pathways to lethal arrhythmias:

1. **Sympathetic overactivity** → QT prolongation → electrical inhomogeneity → ventricular extrasystoles via early afterpotentials → re-entrant circuits
2. **Increased vascular tone** from sympathetic stimulation → myocardial ischaemia (especially with pre-existing cardiac disease)
3. **Lateralised sympathetic imbalance** → left-sided sympathetic overstimulation drives ventricular myocardium past sinus node frequency → ventricular extrasystoles → torsade de pointes
4. **Post-peak vulnerability** → high vagal tone suppresses right-hemispheric (sinus node) control while sudden stress triggers left-hemispheric sympathetic activation → critical imbalance

## Traditional Bwiti Cardioprotective Practices

The paper's most distinctive contribution is its analysis of how Gabonese Bwiti healers prevent cardiac deaths during initiation:

- **Isolation from normal life** — removes potential startle/stress triggers during vulnerable period
- **Sustained trance induction** — maintains right-hemispheric cortical activation, which supports vagal dominance and prevents sudden sympathetic lateralisation
- **Ritual polyrhythmic music** — induces 5–6 Hz theta rhythm associated with calm and rest states
- **Extended observation period** — minimum three days of continuous monitoring and ritual containment (fastigial nucleus effects may persist for weeks)
- **Trance as arrhythmia prevention** — hypnotic states inhibit amygdala arousal system, blocking the cardiac arrhythmia response to frightening stimuli

The authors note that Gabonese experience encompasses over 100,000 initiated individuals, representing a vast body of empirical safety knowledge.

## Clinical Implications

This paper has significant implications for ibogaine treatment practice:

- **Post-treatment monitoring duration:** The three-day minimum observed in Bwiti practice, combined with evidence that fastigial nucleus effects persist for weeks, argues for extended post-treatment observation well beyond the acute pharmacological window
- **Environmental control:** Reducing startle stimuli and maintaining a calm, supportive environment during and after treatment may have genuine cardioprotective value — not merely comfort care
- **Pre-existing conditions:** Patients with cardiovascular disease, epilepsy, or psychiatric conditions involving autonomic dysregulation (schizophrenia, PTSD, depression) face elevated risk
- **Low-dose paradox:** Sub-therapeutic doses may carry disproportionate cardiac risk compared to full therapeutic doses that induce strong parasympathetic dominance
- **Set and setting as safety:** The Bwiti approach reframes "set and setting" from psychological preference to physiological cardioprotection

## Limitations

- **Hypothesis paper:** No original data — the autonomic dysregulation model is proposed but not experimentally validated
- **Published in Medical Hypotheses** — a journal designed for speculative theoretical papers, not peer-reviewed empirical research
- **Pre-hERG era:** Published before Alper et al.'s 2016 characterisation of ibogaine's direct hERG channel blockade, which provides a more direct mechanism for QT prolongation than the autonomic pathway proposed here
- **Limited fatality data:** Relies on published case reports and personal communications; full autopsy and toxicology details not available for all cases
- **Bwiti evidence is observational:** The traditional protective practices, while compelling, have not been subjected to controlled study
- **Does not address noribogaine:** The long-acting metabolite noribogaine, now known to have significant hERG blockade activity, is not discussed
