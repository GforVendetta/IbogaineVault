# Ibogaine therapy for addiction: Consumer views from online fora

**Authors:** Michaela Barber, John Gardner, Michael Savic, Adrian Carter

**Journal:** International Journal of Drug Policy (2020)

**DOI:** [10.1016/j.drugpo.2020.102857](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.drugpo.2020.102857)

---

## Key Findings

Online fora serve as DIY research hubs for ibogaine seekers; users value both pharmacological 'brain reset' and visionary psychological insights; clinic preferred for safety but cost drives home use.

---

## Abstract

**Background:** Ibogaine is a psychedelic drug used by for-profit clinics and lay-people to treat addiction, despite some reported fatalities and a lack of rigorous clinical research. Little is known about ibogaine therapy from a consumer perspective. Online discussions generate and disseminate information about ibogaine therapy and provide a window into how people understand ibogaine's risks and uses.

**Methods:** Thematic analysis of 40 threads comprising posts from 101 individual contributors from two popular online fora: Reddit (n=20) and Drugs Forum (n=20).

**Results:** Three primary themes identified:
1. Online fora as a resource for do-it-yourself research
2. The therapeutic interaction in ibogaine therapy
3. Therapeutic mechanisms of ibogaine

Online fora were a key resource for information about ibogaine therapy, where personal experiences and evidence-based information were valued. Treatment arrangements, risks, and harm reduction were discussed at length. Discussions of therapeutic effects focused on pharmacological mechanisms but positive psychological changes resulting from the psychedelic experience were also reported. Clinic-based treatment was preferred by many due to safety concerns, but cost and time sometimes necessitated lay-administration.

**Conclusion:** Online fora appear to have facilitated a sense of community where individuals are held to account for the success of ibogaine therapy. Fora discussions illustrate that neuroscientific explanations of addiction and behaviour have explanatory salience for people involved in ibogaine therapy.

---

## Study Design

- **Type:** Qualitative thematic analysis
- **Sample:** 40 threads from Reddit and Drugs Forum, 101 forum participants
- **Data collection:** March–July 2019
- **Analysis:** Braun and Clarke (2006) thematic analysis methodology

---

## Key Themes

### 1. Online Fora as DIY Research Resource

Forum participants emphasised being informed and taking responsibility for researching treatment options. Two types of information were sought:
- Practical/procedural information (cost, clinics, dosage, drug interactions)
- Personal experience reports (highly valued over clinic advertisements)

Forums provided community support and accountability for treatment progress.

### 2. Treatment Scenes

**Clinic-based treatment:**
- Preferred for safety due to trained staff, medical monitoring, medication management
- Cost ($3,000–5,000 USD) seen as barrier to access
- Specific doctors and clinics recommended based on expertise and reputation

**Home flood:**
- Often chosen due to financial/geographic constraints
- Required sourcing own ibogaine (quality concerns)
- Recommended having experienced sitter with medical knowledge

**Microdosing:**
- Discussed as post-flood management and alternative to flood dose
- Highlighted tension between pharmacological vs psychedelic mechanism advocates

### 3. Risk Management

- Cardiac risks explicitly discussed and understood
- Harm reduction prioritised by both communities
- Moderators addressed potentially dangerous misinformation
- Precautionary advice: physical exam, ECG, experienced sitter, abstinence from other substances

### 4. Therapeutic Mechanisms

**Neuropsychological understanding:**
- "Rebalancing" neurotransmitters, "repairing" receptors, "resetting" brain
- Diminished craving and reward response
- Long-term benefits attributed to booster doses/microdosing

**Psychological/spiritual understanding:**
- Dream-state healing deep-seated traumas
- Changed perception through self-reflection
- "10 years of therapy in one night"
- Biographical visions with new perspective

**Individual responsibility:**
- Long-term success attributed to patient's willingness to change
- Ibogaine framed as "addiction interrupter" not cure
- Environmental/lifestyle changes seen as essential

---

## Clinical Implications

1. Online fora could be platforms for clinician/peer-led harm reduction interventions
2. Cost barriers drive people toward riskier home administration
3. Consumer preference for medicalised approach suggests demand for regulated access
4. Microdosing practices warrant further empirical investigation
5. Recovery community support may enhance treatment outcomes

---

## Limitations

- Demographics data not available for forum users
- Most participants appeared to be from US, Canada, Europe, Australia
- Clinical severity of participants' addictions unknown
- Sampling based on thread relevance to research questions
