# Psychedelic Treatment for Trauma-Related Psychological and Cognitive Impairment Among US Special Operations Forces Veterans

**Citation:** Davis, A. K., Averill, L. A., Sepeda, N. D., Barsuglia, J. P., & Amoroso, T. (2020). Psychedelic Treatment for Trauma-Related Psychological and Cognitive Impairment Among US Special Operations Forces Veterans. *Chronic Stress*, 4, 1–11. https://doi.org/10.1177/2470547020939564

## Abstract

Background: U.S. Special Operations Forces Veterans are at increased risk for a variety of mental health problems and cognitive impairment associated with military service. Current treatments are lacking in effectiveness and adherence. Therefore, this study examined psychedelic treatment with ibogaine and 5-methoxy-N,N-dimethyltryptamine for trauma-related psychological and cognitive impairment among U.S. Special Operations Forces Veterans.

Method: We conducted a survey of Veterans who completed a specific psychedelic clinical program in Mexico between 2017 and 2019. Questions probed retrospective reports of mental health and cognitive functioning during the 30 days before and 30 days after treatment. A total of 65 people completed treatment during this time frame and were eligible for contact. Of these, 51 (78%) completed the survey and were included in data analyses (mean age 40; male = 96%; married = 55%; Caucasian/White = 92%; Operation Enduring Freedom/Operation Iraqi Freedom Service = 96%).

Results: Results indicated significant and very large reductions in retrospective report of suicidal ideation (p<.001; d=1.9), cognitive impairment (p<.001; d=2.8), and symptoms of posttraumatic stress disorder (p<.001; d=3.6), depression (p<.001; d=3.7), and anxiety (p<.001; d=3.1). Results also showed a significant and large increase in retrospective report of psychological flexibility (p<.001; d=2.9) from before-to-after the psychedelic treatment. Increases in the retrospective report of psychological flexibility were strongly associated with retrospective report of reductions in cognitive impairment, and symptoms of posttraumatic stress disorder, depression, and anxiety (rs range −0.61 to −0.75; p<.001). Additionally, most participants rated the psychedelic experiences as one of the top five personally meaningful (84%), spiritually significant (88%), and psychologically insightful (86%) experiences of their lives.

Limitations: Several limitations should be considered including the retrospective, self-report, survey design of the study, and the lack of randomization and blinding, thus making these finding preliminary.

Conclusion: U.S. Special Operations Forces Veterans may have unique treatment needs because of the sequela of problems associated with repeated trauma exposure and the nature of the exposure. Psychedelic-assisted therapy with these under-researched psychedelics may hold unique promise for this population. However, controlled studies are needed to determine whether this treatment is efficacious in relieving mental health and cognitive impairment among U.S. Special Operations Forces Veterans.

## Key Findings

This study represents the first investigation of combined ibogaine and 5-MeO-DMT treatment among US Special Operations Forces (SOF) veterans, a population characterised by high rates of combat-related PTSD, traumatic brain injury, and treatment resistance. The retrospective survey of 51 veterans who completed a three-day clinical programme in Mexico (2017–2019) produced effect sizes that substantially exceed conventional benchmarks for large effects across every outcome domain measured.

The magnitude of symptom reduction was striking across all psychiatric measures: PTSD symptoms dropped from a mean of 46.2 to 12.0 on the PCL-5 (d=3.6), depression fell from 4.1 to 0.9 on the PHQ-2 (d=3.7), anxiety decreased from 4.0 to 1.1 on the GAD-2 (d=3.1), cognitive impairment reduced from 2.4 to 1.0 on the MOS-CF (d=2.8), and suicidal ideation dropped from 2.7 to 0.4 (d=1.9). These effect sizes — ranging from roughly 2 to 4 times the threshold for a "large" effect — are among the largest reported in the psychedelic therapy literature, though the retrospective self-report design warrants caution in interpretation.

A particularly notable finding was the strong increase in psychological flexibility as measured by the AAQ-II (d=2.9), which showed robust negative correlations with reductions in PTSD, depression, anxiety, and cognitive impairment (r values −0.61 to −0.75). This aligns with emerging evidence that psychological flexibility may serve as a transdiagnostic mechanism of change in psychedelic-assisted therapy, paralleling findings from psilocybin research where flexibility mediated decreases in depression and anxiety.

The study population is distinctive: 82% reported deployment-related head injuries, 63% were Navy SEALs, 59% had five or more deployments, and 96% served in post-9/11 operations. This cohort represents veterans with extensive combat exposure and high rates of TBI — a population for which standard PTSD treatments show particularly limited efficacy. That 96% rated the programme "much better" than prior treatments they had attempted underscores the perceived inadequacy of conventional options for this population.

## Methodology

This was a retrospective survey study employing a quasi-experimental one-group pretest/posttest design with "then-test" items. From a pool of 65 eligible participants who completed the clinical programme between 2017 and 2019, 51 (78%) completed an anonymous online survey (Survey Gizmo) recruited via email from April to September 2019. Time since treatment ranged from one month to two years. The study used validated self-report instruments including the PCL-5 (PTSD), PHQ-2 (depression), GAD-2 (anxiety), Depressive Symptom Index Suicidality Subscale (suicidal ideation), MOS-CF (cognitive functioning), and AAQ-II (psychological flexibility), each administered as retrospective ratings for 30 days before and 30 days after treatment. Paired-sample t-tests with Cohen's d effect sizes and correlation analyses were conducted using SPSS v25. Procedures were approved by Solutions Institutional Review Board.

## Treatment Protocol

The clinical programme operated in a residential setting in Mexico over three days, combining ibogaine and 5-MeO-DMT in a structured sequence:

**Screening and eligibility:** Participants completed medical screening with a programme physician (medication history, medical conditions, VA disability rating, TBI history), followed by psychological intake with a clinician. Required laboratory tests included complete blood count with differential, metabolic panel, urine drug screen, and 12-lead ECG. Patients who were obese or over 55 required a stress test. Individuals on contraindicated medications could proceed only after adhering to a pharmacist-designed taper.

**Day 1 — Ibogaine session:** Following group preparation and urine toxicology/alcohol confirmation, participants received a single oral dose of ibogaine hydrochloride (10 mg/kg, 99% purity) in groups of four to five. Participants lay supine with continuous cardiac monitoring, IV fluids, and medical oversight throughout.

**Day 2 — Integration:** Dedicated to processing the ibogaine experience through journaling, one-on-one meetings with clinical staff, and optional group integration sessions.

**Day 3 — 5-MeO-DMT sessions:** After preparation, each participant was individually administered inhaled 5-MeO-DMT in an escalating dose protocol: 5 mg, 15 mg, and 30 mg (total ~50 mg, estimated 15% concentration from *Bufo alvarius* toad secretion). Participants who did not reach observed peak effects received a fourth dose (30 mg) and possibly a fifth (up to 45 mg). Individual and group integration followed.

The core outcome data demonstrate uniformly large reductions across every psychiatric domain measured, with effect sizes substantially exceeding the d=0.80 threshold typically considered "large":

Post-treatment means fell below clinical cutoffs on all symptom measures, suggesting participants moved from clinically elevated symptomatology to subclinical levels. The correlation between increased psychological flexibility and symptom reduction (r=−0.61 to −0.75 across PTSD, depression, anxiety, and cognitive impairment) suggests flexibility may operate as a transdiagnostic change mechanism — consistent with findings from psilocybin research linking psychological flexibility to therapeutic outcomes.

Prior to the programme, most participants had attempted multiple conventional interventions including nutritional/dietary changes (59%), yoga (55%), psychotherapy (43%), cannabis (43%), and psychiatric medications (41%) — indicating substantial treatment-seeking behaviour before turning to psychedelic-assisted approaches.

### Subjective Significance and Enduring Effects

Participants rated the programme among their top five most personally meaningful (84%), spiritually significant (88%), psychologically insightful (86%), and psychologically challenging (69%) experiences. They reported strong positive changes across domains including personal well-being (77%), life purpose (75%), social relationships (73%), and attitudes about self (72%). Treatment satisfaction was exceptionally high: 80% were very or completely satisfied, 92% would strongly recommend it, and 96% rated it "much better" than prior treatments.

## Clinical Implications

This study provides the foundational evidence for what has since become one of the most actively investigated psychedelic treatment paradigms for military veterans. The combination of ibogaine and 5-MeO-DMT administered sequentially over three days produced effect sizes that dwarf those typically seen in PTSD pharmacotherapy trials, where effect sizes of d=0.3–0.5 are common. While the retrospective design demands interpretive caution, the sheer magnitude of the effects — and their consistency across multiple independent symptom domains — generated the clinical signal that prompted subsequent controlled investigations.

The study's documentation of a structured three-day protocol with medical screening, cardiac monitoring, and staged dosing established a procedural template that influenced later clinical programmes. The exclusion criteria and safety procedures described here — ECG screening, contraindicated medication tapers, urine toxicology confirmation, and continuous cardiac monitoring during ibogaine administration — represent early codification of safety standards for ibogaine-assisted treatment in veteran populations, predating the more comprehensive protocols subsequently developed for the [MISTIC trial](../2024/Cherian2024_Magnesium_Ibogaine_TBI.md).

The finding that psychological flexibility strongly mediated symptom improvement carries implications for therapeutic design: it suggests that ibogaine and 5-MeO-DMT may promote recovery not merely through acute pharmacological action but by catalysing the kind of cognitive-emotional restructuring that conventional psychotherapies attempt over months or years. This positions the combination protocol as potentially complementary to trauma-focused psychotherapy approaches rather than purely pharmacological substitution.

The high prevalence of TBI in this cohort (82% with deployment head injuries) and the substantial reduction in cognitive impairment (d=2.8) are particularly significant. Few interventions for TBI-associated cognitive decline show effects of this magnitude, and this early signal directly informed the design of the [MISTIC trial](../2024/Cherian2024_Magnesium_Ibogaine_TBI.md), which incorporated neuroimaging and neuropsychological assessment to rigorously evaluate cognitive outcomes.

## Limitations

The authors identify several substantial methodological constraints. The retrospective, self-report design with "then-test" items introduces recall bias and the "present state effect," where participants may reconstruct their pre-treatment baseline as worse than it actually was in light of perceived improvement. The study lacked clinician-administered assessments, control groups, randomisation, or blinding — making it impossible to rule out placebo, expectancy, and selection effects. The combination protocol means individual contributions of ibogaine and 5-MeO-DMT cannot be disentangled. Generalisability is limited to predominantly white, non-Hispanic, male SOF veterans who actively sought psychedelic treatment — a self-selected population that may differ systematically from the broader veteran population in motivation, openness, and baseline severity. The study also did not assess adverse effects, a notable gap given ibogaine's established cardiac risks. The Schedule I status of both substances constrained the study design options available to the investigators.
