# Howard Lotsof — Map of Content

> **Role:** Discoverer of ibogaine's anti-addictive properties (1962) | Founder, NDA International | Patient advocate
> **Lifespan:** 1943–2010
> **Key Insight:** Self-experiment with ibogaine eliminated heroin withdrawal and craving
> **Legacy:** Shaped every subsequent ibogaine treatment protocol worldwide

---

## Overview

Howard Lotsof is the foundational figure of modern ibogaine treatment. In 1962, as a 19-year-old heroin-dependent drug experimenter in New York City, he ingested ibogaine and discovered—entirely by accident—that it eliminated his physical withdrawal and drug craving. This serendipitous finding launched six decades of advocacy, research coordination, and protocol development.

Unlike most figures in psychedelic history, Lotsof was not an academic or clinician. He was a drug user who became a scientist-advocate, operating at the intersection of underground treatment, regulatory development, and harm reduction activism. His approach—transparent about adverse events, focused on user empowerment, dismissive of abstinence-only frameworks—shaped the ethical orientation of the ibogaine community.

**Key Contributions:**
- Discovered ibogaine's anti-addictive properties (1962)
- Held original US patents for ibogaine treatment of opioid, stimulant, alcohol, nicotine dependence (1985–1995)
- Co-authored definitive clinical overview with Ken Alper (2007)
- Founded the Ibogaine Dossier (first ibogaine website, 1997)
- Mentored generation of practitioners including Clare Wilkins
- Co-authored foundational therapy manual (2003)
- Established NDA International as research coordination body

**His First Words to Clare Wilkins:** "Watch QT intervals and don't trust the media."

## Key Themes Across Sources

### 1. The Discovery Moment (1962)

From his collaborations with Alper and others, Lotsof documented the original discovery:

The group of drug experimenters, organised around an interest in the psychotherapeutic potential of hallucinogens, obtained ibogaine legally from chemical supply houses. The effects were entirely unknown to them. A heroin-dependent subgroup, to their surprise, experienced an unexpected elimination of physical withdrawal symptoms.

This was not a clinical trial or laboratory experiment—it was serendipity among young people exploring consciousness. Yet Lotsof recognised the significance immediately and spent the rest of his life pursuing regulatory development.

### 2. Trauma and Abreaction Framework

The 1996 paper established the psychological model that still guides ibogaine therapy:

> "Ibogaine initiates a pharmacologically induced process whereby patients gain insight into the trauma underlying their chemical dependence."

Key insight: Ibogaine allows non-emotional, non-threatening access to repressed memories. Patients can examine trauma "objectively" without being overwhelmed—a fundamentally different mechanism than traditional psychotherapy or other psychedelics.

### 3. Protocol Development

The 2003 therapy manual—co-authored with a team of underground providers—codified decades of treatment experience:

- **15-20 mg/kg** established as therapeutic dose
- **48-hour continuous monitoring** requirement
- **EKG screening** protocol
- **Inclusion/exclusion criteria** framework
- Integration of OOWS/SOWS withdrawal assessment scales

This manual became the template for GITA guidelines (2015) and all subsequent protocols worldwide.

### 4. Harm Reduction Philosophy

From the 2021 posthumous paper, Lotsof articulated why user control of ibogaine matters:

> "The ibogaine paradigm is so new and so unique as to be difficult to perceive, even in concept, except by those who have undergone this rite of passage or observed it firsthand."

> "To the drug user trapped in the morass of illegal drug use... ibogaine represents a quick and relatively painless way out."

He explicitly framed ibogaine activism as continuous with the Underground Railroad and needle exchange—moral imperatives in the face of state failure.

### 5. The DEA Conversation

A remarkable anecdote from the 2021 paper:

> "Lotsof was contacted while in the Netherlands by the DEA desk officer at the US embassy who asked Lotsof to explain why the Dutch government was allowing a demand reduction tool like ibogaine to be researched in the Netherlands?"

The DEA officer estimated ibogaine could reduce drug demand by 50%—making cartels "easier targets." This reframing of ibogaine as demand reduction (rather than harm reduction) reveals the strange bedfellows of drug policy.

## Research Arc: From Discovery to Protocol

1. **1962**: Discovery of anti-addictive properties (self-experiment)
2. **1963–1985**: FDA scheduling, underground continues
3. **1985–1995**: US patents obtained for multiple addiction indications
4. **1991–1995**: NIDA research programme (which Lotsof helped initiate)
5. **1993**: FDA approves Phase I clinical trial (later abandoned due to litigation)
6. **1996**: Establishes trauma/abreaction framework
7. **1997**: Launches Ibogaine Dossier website
8. **1999–2000**: Landmark case series with Alper (n=33) — largest systematic documentation of ibogaine for opioid withdrawal at that time
9. **2001–2003**: Codifies treatment protocols from underground experience
10. **2007**: Definitive clinical overview with Alper
11. **2008**: Census of the ibogaine treatment community with Alper — 3,414 individuals, 11 fatalities documented
12. **2010**: Death

## A Personal Note

Howard Lotsof was not merely a scientist or advocate—he was a drug user who discovered something profound and spent his life trying to make it available to others. His widow Norma Lotsof continued advocacy work after his death.

From Clare Wilkins (2021 Microdose talk):

> "After Howard died is when all the squabbling started."

The ibogaine community's fractures—between clinical and underground, between research and advocacy, between different treatment philosophies—emerged more sharply after losing its central coordinating figure.
