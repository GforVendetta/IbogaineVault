# Clare Wilkins — Map of Content

> **Role:** Director, Pangea Biomedics | Co-author, GITA Clinical Guidelines | GITA Board Member since 2010
> **Treatment Year:** 2005 (9 years methadone dependency)
> **Treatments Facilitated:** 800+ as of 2020
> **Mentors:** Howard Lotsoff, Ken Alper, Norma Lotsoff

---

## Overview

Clare Wilkins is one of the most influential clinical voices in ibogaine treatment, having facilitated over 800 treatments since 2006. Her work spans clinical protocol development (particularly cumulative dosing), harm reduction advocacy, and community organising through GITA. These primary sources document the evolution of her thinking across 15+ years.

**Key Contributions:**
- Developed cumulative dosing protocol after analysing fatalities
- Integrated orthomolecular medicine with ibogaine treatment
- Co-authored GITA Clinical Guidelines (2015)
- Pioneered Parkinson's disease treatment protocols
- Advocated for drug user rights and harm reduction framing

## Key Themes Across Sources

### 1. Cumulative Dosing Protocol
The pivotal insight that drove protocol change:

> "If this person had received 300mg rather than 1.2g, would he have died? They all said no." — 2020 MYEBOGA Interview

> "Since we switched our protocol, the safety issues that come up are so meagre. No one has had to ever go to a hospital since we changed our protocols." — 2018 Documentary

Evolution documented across:
- [[2017/Wilkins2017_Methadone_Low_Dose]] — **Published case report** establishing the protocol
- [[Primary_Sources/2017_BC_Clare_Wilkins_Methadone_Detox]] — ICEERS pilot methodology
- [[Primary_Sources/2017_PS_Clare_Wilkins_Methadone_Detox]] — Clinical trial data
- [[Pangea_Ops/Ibogaine_Stories_Transcripts/2018_Oliver_Wilkins_Pangea_Protocol]] — Protocol philosophy
- [[Primary_Sources/2020_MYEBOGA_Clare_Wilkins_Interview]] — Retrospective rationale

### 2. Orthomolecular Medicine Integration
From personal recovery tool to clinical standard:

- Brain Optimization Institute training post-treatment
- IV vitamin C for withdrawal symptom reduction
- Customised protocols for chronic pain, hepatic issues
- Cannabis oil as adjunct (replacing benzodiazepines)

Sources: [[Primary_Sources/2017_EntheoScience_Clare_Wilkins_Cannabis_Ethics]], [[Primary_Sources/2020_MYEBOGA_Clare_Wilkins_Interview]]

### 3. Howard Lotsoff Mentorship
His first words to Clare: **"Watch QT intervals and don't trust the media."**

Key teachings documented:
- Harm reduction framing (NAMA board member)
- "It's not about getting clean—it's about meeting people where they are"
- Transparency about adverse events
- "Why wouldn't you share what you've learned?"

Sources: [[Primary_Sources/2010_MAPS_Clare_Wilkins_Evolution]], [[Primary_Sources/2021_Microdose_Clare_Wilkins_Holotropic_Perspective]]

### 4. Community Trauma and Division
Norma Lotsoff: "After Howard died is when all the squabbling started."

Clare's observation: "We are more traumatised than all other psychedelic communities as a whole."

Three 15-year life phases: childhood trauma → using years → ibogaine work (all traumatic)

Sources: [[Primary_Sources/2017_EntheoScience_Clare_Wilkins_Cannabis_Ethics]], [[Primary_Sources/2021_Microdose_Clare_Wilkins_Holotropic_Perspective]]

### 5. Outcome Philosophy
From "abstinence" to "engagement":

> "The outcome is engagement, not abstinence. We're trying to interrupt the trajectory." — 2010 Horizons

> "Some of our clients don't even want to come off opioids completely. That's what allows them to do their best." — 2020 MYEBOGA
