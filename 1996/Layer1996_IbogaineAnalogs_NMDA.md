# Structurally modified ibogaine analogs exhibit differing affinities for NMDA receptors

**Citation:** Layer RT, Skolnick P, Bertha CM, Bandarage UK, Kuehne ME, Popik P (1996). Structurally modified ibogaine analogs exhibit differing affinities for NMDA receptors. *European Journal of Pharmacology*, 309(2), 159–165. DOI: 10.1016/0014-2999(96)00304-4

## Abstract

The potencies of a series of ibogaine analogs to inhibit [³H]MK-801 binding to NMDA receptors were examined. Among these compounds, ibogaine was the most potent inhibitor (Kᵢ ≈ 1.2 μM), whilst the compounds with greatest structural similarity — O-desmethylibogaine and O-t-butyl-O-desmethylibogaine — were less potent (Kᵢ ≈ 5.5 and 179.0 μM, respectively). In morphine-dependent mice, ibogaine, but not O-desmethylibogaine or O-t-butyl-O-desmethylibogaine, attenuated naloxone-precipitated withdrawal jumping. These findings are consistent with the hypothesis that inhibition of morphine dependence expression by ibogaine is related to its NMDA receptor antagonist properties.

## Key Findings

- **NMDA SAR:** Ibogaine was the most potent NMDA antagonist in the series (Kᵢ = 1.1 ± 0.03 μM). The 12-methoxy group is critical — O-demethylation (O-desmethylibogaine) reduced affinity ~5-fold; replacement with bulky t-butoxy reduced it ~160-fold
- **κ-Opioid dissociation:** O-desmethylibogaine was ~10–100× more potent than ibogaine at κ-opioid receptors — the opposite rank order to NMDA, enabling pharmacological dissection of mechanisms
- **In vivo selectivity:** Only ibogaine (80 mg/kg) attenuated naloxone-precipitated withdrawal jumping; O-desmethylibogaine and O-t-butyl-O-desmethylibogaine were inactive at equal doses
- **Ethyl group requirement:** Removal or substitution of the C4 ethyl group reduced NMDA affinity >10-fold, identifying this position as another key pharmacophore element
- **Against κ-opioid hypothesis:** If κ-opioid receptors mediated anti-withdrawal effects, O-desmethylibogaine should have been more potent — it was inactive, supporting the NMDA mechanism
- **Against σ receptor hypothesis:** O-t-butyl-O-desmethylibogaine has similar σ receptor affinity to ibogaine but was inactive in vivo, ruling out σ receptors for this effect

## Methodology

**Animals:** Male NIH Swiss mice (20–25 g), Sprague-Dawley rats (200–250 g), and Hartley guinea-pigs (300–500 g).

**[³H]MK-801 binding (NMDA):** Extensively washed rat forebrain membranes incubated 2 h at 25°C with 4–5 nM [³H]MK-801 in presence of 30 μM glycine and 30 μM glutamate. Nonspecific binding defined with 100 μM TCP. Competition curves fitted by iterative curve fitting (GraphPad-Inplot 4.01).

**[³H]U-69593 binding (κ-opioid):** Forebrain membranes from rat, mouse, and guinea-pig. 3 nM [³H]U-69593 in 50 mM HEPES-NH₄OH buffer, 30 min at 30°C. Nonspecific binding: 100 μM (−)U-50,488. Cross-species comparison included because κ-opioid binding site proportions vary across species.

**Morphine dependence model:** Mice given morphine 30 mg/kg twice daily for 3 days plus morning dose on day 4. Test compounds administered 2 h 15 min after final morphine. Naloxone challenge (4 mg/kg) 45 min after test compound. Jumps counted over 10 min. Rotorod assessment (4.6 rpm, 30 s) after jumping session. Statistics: Kruskal-Wallis ANOVA with Dunn's multiple comparisons (P < 0.05).

**Synthesis:** O-desmethylibogaine and O-t-butyl-O-desmethylibogaine synthesised and characterised (¹H NMR, EI-MS, HRMS, elemental analysis). The t-butyl derivative was designed to resist O-dealkylation in vivo.

## Discussion Highlights

The 12-methoxy group emerged as the critical pharmacophore for NMDA receptor binding. O-Demethylation (producing O-desmethylibogaine/noribogaine) reduced affinity ~5-fold, whilst the positional isomer tabernanthine (13-methoxy) was ~10-fold weaker than ibogaine. The C4 ethyl group was also important — methyl substitution or removal reduced affinity >10-fold.

The reciprocal structure-activity relationships at NMDA versus κ-opioid receptors enabled a clean pharmacological dissection: O-desmethylibogaine has ~10-100× higher κ affinity but ~5× lower NMDA affinity than ibogaine. Since only ibogaine attenuated withdrawal, the NMDA mechanism was supported and the κ-opioid mechanism effectively ruled out for this specific measure. The same logic excluded σ receptors, as the t-butyl derivative retained σ affinity but was inactive in vivo.

The authors noted an important caveat — naloxone-precipitated withdrawal is a complex phenomenon and only jumping was measured. Whether other withdrawal signs are differentially affected by these derivatives was unknown.

## Clinical Implications

This paper provides the pharmacological basis for understanding why ibogaine — rather than its principal metabolite noribogaine (O-desmethylibogaine) — is the active species for NMDA-mediated effects. This is clinically relevant because oral ibogaine undergoes extensive first-pass metabolism to noribogaine, meaning the NMDA-dependent therapeutic effects may be time-limited to the period of parent compound exposure, whilst the longer-acting noribogaine drives serotonergic and κ-opioid effects. This temporal dissociation between mechanism windows may explain the distinct phases of the ibogaine experience reported clinically.

## Limitations

- Single behavioural measure (jumping) — does not capture full withdrawal syndrome
- Racemic mixtures used for some compounds, potentially underestimating active isomer affinity
- Blood-brain barrier penetration of analogs not directly assessed (inferred from in vivo convulsant effects)
- No pharmacokinetic data for test compounds — dose-to-brain-concentration relationship unknown
- Mouse model only — species differences in NMDA receptor pharmacology possible
