# The Effect of Ibogaine on Cognitive Functioning

**Citation:** Cherian, K. N., Faerman, A., Brown, R., Anker, L., Keynan, O., Ford, T. J., Keller, J., & Williams, N. R. (2023). The Effect of Ibogaine on Cognitive Functioning. *Journal of the International Neuropsychological Society*, 29. doi:10.1017/S1355617723011049

## Abstract

This conference abstract presents cognitive outcomes from a prospective observational study of Special Operations Veterans (SOV) who received ibogaine (~20mg/kg) for treatment of sequelae from repeated blast and combat exposures, including traumatic brain injuries (TBI). This is the first prospective study examining neuropsychological test performance following full-dose ibogaine administration.

## Key Findings

- **First prospective cognitive study** using full ibogaine dose (~20mg/kg)
- **Significant improvements** in multiple cognitive domains at 1-month follow-up
- **No negative cognitive consequences** detected up to one month post-treatment
- **Processing speed** showed largest improvement (107→117 standard score)

## Study Design

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Design | Prospective observational |
| Sample | n=30 SOV (n=27 at 1-month) |
| Dose | ~20 mg/kg ibogaine |
| Location | Specialized clinic in Mexico |
| Timepoints | Baseline, post-treatment, 1-month |

## Demographics

| Characteristic | Mean (SD) |
|----------------|-----------|
| Age | 44.9 (7.5) years |
| Education | 15.7 (2.1) years |
| Estimated IQ | 114.0 (10.3) |
| Years Active Military | 17.1 (6.7) |
| Combat Deployments | 5.5 (3.0) |
| TBI severity | 28 mild / 1 moderate / 1 moderate-severe |

## Neuropsychological Battery

- **WASI-II:** 2-subscale IQ estimate (baseline only)
- **WAIS-IV:** Working Memory Index (WMI), Processing Speed Index (PSI)
- **D-KEFS:** Trail Making, Verbal Fluency, Color-Word (Inhibition & Inhibition/Switching), Tower Test
- **HVLT:** Hopkins Verbal Learning Test (with alternate forms)
- **BVMT-R:** Brief Visuospatial Memory Test - Revised (with alternate forms)

## Results Summary

### Significant Improvements (Corrected for Multiple Comparisons)

| Test | Baseline | Post-Tx | 1-Month | p-value |
|------|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| CW3 Inhibition (ss) | 11.74 | 13.04 | 13.04 | <0.001* |
| CW4 Inhibition/Switching (ss) | 11.74 | 12.78 | 13.04 | 0.004* |
| WAIS-IV PSI (SS) | 107.04 | 115.52 | 117.3 | <0.001* |
| Verbal Fluency Switching (ss) | 12.00 | 11.44 | 13.37 | 0.003* |

*Passes correction for multiple comparisons

### Additional Improvements

| Test | Baseline | Post-Tx | 1-Month | p-value |
|------|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| HVLT Delayed Recall (T) | 46.08 | 49.44 | 52.88 | 0.048 |
| Tower Test Total (ss) | 11.84 | 13.04 | 13.08 | 0.023 |
| TMT Switching (ss) | 11.07 | 11.96 | 12.07 | 0.071 |

### Non-Significant Changes

| Test | Baseline | Post-Tx | 1-Month | p-value |
|------|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| WAIS-IV WMI (SS) | 108.54 | 111.12 | 111.42 | 0.150 |
| BVMT-R Delayed Recall (T) | 58.22 | 61.04 | 59.04 | 0.145 |

## Domains Showing Improvement

1. **Processing Speed** — Most robust improvement (+10 standard score points)
2. **Cognitive Inhibition** — Ability to suppress prepotent responses
3. **Cognitive Flexibility** — Inhibition/switching tasks improved
4. **Verbal Memory** — Delayed recall improvement
5. **Planning/Problem-Solving** — Tower Test total achievement

## Proposed Mechanisms

The authors propose several explanations for cognitive improvements:

1. **Neuroplastic changes** — Ibogaine acts across multiple neurotransmitter systems
2. **Neurotrophic effects** — Upregulates BDNF and GDNF transcription (observed in rats)
3. **Secondary to mood improvement** — Reduction of PTSD/mood effects on cognition
4. **Practice effects** — Cannot be fully ruled out, though alternate forms were used

## Limitations

- Small sample size (n=30)
- No control group
- Observational design
- Potential practice effects
- One-month follow-up only
- Conference abstract format limits detail

## Clinical Implications

This study provides preliminary evidence that:
- Full-dose ibogaine may improve cognitive functioning in TBI patients
- No acute cognitive deterioration was observed
- Processing speed and executive functions may be particularly responsive
- Results support further investigation of ibogaine's therapeutic potential
