# (+)-Catharanthine and (-)-18-methoxycoronaridine induce antidepressant-like activity in mice by differently recruiting serotonergic and norepinephrinergic neurotransmission

**Citation:** Arias, H. R., De Deurwaerdère, P., El-Kasaby, A., Di Giovanni, G., Eom, S., Lee, J. H., Freissmuth, M., & Chagraoui, A. (2023). (+)-Catharanthine and (-)-18-methoxycoronaridine induce antidepressant-like activity in mice by differently recruiting serotonergic and norepinephrinergic neurotransmission. *European Journal of Pharmacology*, 939, 175454. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ejphar.2022.175454

## Abstract

The antidepressant-like activity of (+)-catharanthine and (-)-18-methoxycoronaridine [(-)-18-MC] was studied in male and female mice using forced swim (FST) and tail suspension tests (TST). The underlying molecular mechanism was assessed by electrophysiological, radioligand, and functional experiments. The FST results showed that acute administration (40 mg/kg) of (+)-catharanthine or (-)-18-MC induces similar antidepressant-like activity in male and female mice at 1 h and 24 h, whereas the TST results showed a lower effect for (-)-18-MC at 24 h. Repeated treatment at lower dose (20 mg/kg) augmented the efficacy of both congeners. The FST results showed that (-)-18-MC reduces immobility and increases swimming times without changing climbing behavior, whereas (+)-catharanthine reduces immobility time, increases swimming times more markedly, and increases climbing behavior. To investigate the contribution of the serotonin and norepinephrine transporters in the antidepressant effects of (+)-catharanthine and (-)-18-MC, we conducted in vitro radioligand and functional studies. Results obtained demonstrated that (+)-catharanthine inhibits norepinephrine transporter with higher potency/affinity than that for (-)-18-MC, whereas both congeners inhibit serotonin transporter with similar potency/affinity. Moreover, whereas no congener activated/inhibited/potentiated the function of serotonin receptor 3A or serotonin receptor 3AB, both increased serotonin receptor 3A receptor desensitization. Depletion of serotonin decreased the antidepressant-like activity of both congeners, whereas norepinephrine depletion only decreased (+)-catharanthine's activity. Our study shows that coronaridine congeners induce antidepressant-like activity in a dose- and time-dependent, and sex-independent, manner. The antidepressant-like property of both compounds involves serotonin transporter inhibition, without directly activating/inhibiting serotonin receptors 3, while (+)-catharanthine also mobilizes norepinephrinergic neurotransmission.

## Key Findings

This study establishes that two coronaridine congeners — (+)-catharanthine and (-)-18-MC — achieve antidepressant-like effects in mice through mechanistically distinct monoaminergic pathways. Both compounds reduced immobility and increased swimming in the FST at 40 mg/kg (acute) and 20 mg/kg (14-day repeated dosing), with effects persisting to 24 hours and no significant sex differences across any behavioural measure (p > 0.9 for male-female comparisons throughout). Repeated low-dose administration matched the efficacy of single high-dose treatment, suggesting sensitisation without tolerance development.

A notable time-course divergence appeared in the TST: (+)-catharanthine reduced immobility at both 1 hour and 24 hours, whereas (-)-18-MC failed at 1 hour (p = 0.576) but succeeded at 24 hours (p < 0.0001), suggesting slower serotonergic engagement without the immediate norepinephrinergic contribution that catharanthine provides.

The central mechanistic dissociation emerged from climbing behaviour in the FST and running behaviour in the TST — both markers of norepinephrinergic engagement. (+)-Catharanthine robustly increased climbing (p < 0.0001) and running scores, while (-)-18-MC produced no change in either parameter (p = 0.9389 for climbing). This behavioural divergence mapped directly onto neurotransmitter depletion experiments: serotonin depletion via pCPA abolished the effects of both compounds, whereas norepinephrine depletion via DSP-4 selectively blocked (+)-catharanthine's activity while leaving (-)-18-MC's antidepressant-like profile intact.

Radioligand binding at human monoamine transporters confirmed the mechanism. Both congeners inhibited SERT with comparable affinity (Kᵢ: 8.4 ± 1.7 μM for catharanthine vs 12.6 ± 2.3 μM for 18-MC), but (+)-catharanthine bound NET with approximately 15-fold greater affinity than (-)-18-MC (Kᵢ: 16.6 ± 1.4 vs 244 ± 48 μM). (-)-18-MC displayed marked SERT selectivity — its SERT affinity exceeded DAT affinity by 16-fold and NET affinity by 19-fold. Neither compound activated, inhibited, or potentiated 5-HT₃A or 5-HT₃AB receptor currents, though both facilitated 5-HT₃A desensitisation through an allosteric mechanism (p < 0.001 for normalised charge and recovery time reductions in pre-treatment protocols).

## Methodology

Adult male and female Swiss albino CD1 mice (30–35 g) were assessed across two standard behavioural paradigms: the forced swim test (FST, n = 10/group) measuring swimming, immobility, and climbing; and the tail suspension test (TST, n = 14/condition) measuring immobility, lateral swing, and running. Acute protocols used 40 mg/kg i.p.; repeated protocols used 20 mg/kg i.p. daily for 14 days with testing on days 7 and 14. Neurotransmitter contribution was dissected using pCPA (150 mg/kg/day × 3 days) to deplete serotonin and DSP-4 (50 mg/kg single dose) to ablate norepinephrine, each administered before acute congener challenge.

In-vitro pharmacology used HEK293 cells stably expressing human DAT, NET, and SERT for both radioligand displacement (tritiated CFT, nisoxetine, and citalopram respectively) and substrate uptake inhibition assays across 0.3–300 μM concentration ranges. Electrophysiological characterisation of 5-HT₃A and 5-HT₃AB receptor activity employed two-electrode voltage-clamp recordings in *Xenopus* oocytes at −70 mV holding potential, testing both co-treatment and pre-treatment (4 min) protocols at 100 μM. Statistical analysis used two-way ANOVA with Tukey's post-hoc for behavioural data and one-way ANOVA for electrophysiology, with significance set at p < 0.05.

## Pharmacological Profile

### Monoamine Transporter Binding and Uptake Inhibition

The three coronaridine congeners displayed a consistent affinity hierarchy across all three human monoamine transporters, with (-)-ibogaine showing the highest affinity, followed by (+)-catharanthine, then (-)-18-MC. The pharmacologically significant distinction lies in selectivity: (-)-18-MC is functionally a serotonin-selective compound at the transporter level, while (+)-catharanthine retains substantial norepinephrine transporter engagement.

A notable discrepancy emerged for (-)-18-MC at NET: its uptake inhibition potency (IC₅₀ 121 ± 10 μM) substantially exceeded what its binding affinity (Kᵢ 244 ± 48 μM) would predict. The authors attribute this to (-)-18-MC preferentially binding the inward-facing transporter conformation — a state favoured during the transport cycle but not during equilibrium binding assays. Since NET's return step is characteristically slow, compounds that trap the inward-facing state achieve disproportionate uptake blockade relative to their static binding affinity.

### 5-HT₃ Receptor Activity

Neither congener produced agonist, antagonist, or potentiating effects at 5-HT₃A or 5-HT₃AB receptors. Peak currents evoked by 100 μM serotonin were unchanged by co-application or pre-incubation with either compound at 100 μM. However, both compounds significantly accelerated 5-HT₃A desensitisation, as measured by reduced normalised charge and shortened deactivation recovery times. This effect was selective for the 5-HT₃A homopentamer — the 5-HT₃AB heteropentamer showed no significant changes in any parameter.

### Behavioural Dissociation Summary

The divergent behavioural profiles across FST and TST, combined with selective neurotransmitter depletion results, provide the core mechanistic evidence. Climbing (FST) and running (TST) are established markers of norepinephrinergic engagement, while swimming (FST) reflects serotonergic activity. The pattern is unambiguous: (-)-18-MC recruits serotonergic pathways exclusively, while (+)-catharanthine engages both serotonergic and norepinephrinergic systems.

## Clinical Implications

This study's primary translational value lies in its mechanistic dissection of two ibogaine-related compounds that lack ibogaine's problematic cardiac and psychoactive profiles. The finding that (-)-18-MC functions essentially as a serotonin-selective agent — with minimal norepinephrine or dopamine transporter engagement — positions it as a pharmacologically cleaner tool for probing serotonergic contributions to ibogaine-family therapeutics. (+)-Catharanthine's dual SERT/NET profile more closely resembles established antidepressants like venlafaxine or duloxetine, suggesting that structural modifications within the coronaridine scaffold can tune monoaminergic selectivity with meaningful behavioural consequences.

The absence of tolerance after 14-day repeated dosing is noteworthy for any compound being evaluated for chronic psychiatric indications. The sex-independence of effects across all measures provides reassurance for translational models, though the mouse-to-human gap remains substantial. The 5-HT₃A desensitisation finding, while not directly driving antidepressant activity, adds to the increasingly complex picture of how coronaridine congeners modulate serotonergic tone through multiple convergent mechanisms beyond simple reuptake inhibition.

## Limitations

The authors note that (+)-catharanthine's rapid clearance (half-life 0.8–1.2 hours) is difficult to reconcile with 24-hour behavioural persistence without invoking active metabolites, yet no metabolite identification was performed. The 40 mg/kg doses, while producing clear behavioural effects, substantially exceed typical ibogaine clinical doses on a mg/kg basis, limiting direct dose extrapolation. All behavioural data rely on the FST/TST paradigm, which models behavioural despair rather than the full clinical phenotype of depression. The transporter pharmacology used supraphysiological concentrations (0.3–300 μM) in recombinant systems, and the Kᵢ values for (-)-18-MC at DAT and NET were estimated from incomplete inhibition curves.
