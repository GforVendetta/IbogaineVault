# Psychedelics Reopen the Social Reward Learning Critical Period

**Citation:** Nardou, R., Sawyer, E., Song, Y. J., Wilkinson, M., Padovan-Hernandez, Y., de Deus, J. L., Wright, N., Lama, C., Faltin, S., Goff, L. A., Stein-O'Brien, G. L. & Dölen, G. (2023). Psychedelics reopen the social reward learning critical period. *Nature*, 618, 790–798. DOI: 10.1038/s41586-023-06204-3

## Abstract

Psychedelics are a broad class of drugs defined by their ability to induce an altered state of consciousness. These drugs have been used for millennia in both spiritual and medicinal contexts, and a number of recent clinical successes have spurred a renewed interest in developing psychedelic therapies. Nevertheless, a unifying mechanism that can account for these shared phenomenological and therapeutic properties remains unknown. Here we demonstrate in mice that the ability to reopen the social reward learning critical period is a shared property across psychedelic drugs. Notably, the time course of critical period reopening is proportional to the duration of acute subjective effects reported in humans. Furthermore, the ability to reinstate social reward learning in adulthood is paralleled by metaplastic restoration of oxytocin-mediated long-term depression in the nucleus accumbens. Finally, identification of differentially expressed genes in the 'open state' versus the 'closed state' provides evidence that reorganization of the extracellular matrix is a common downstream mechanism underlying psychedelic drug-mediated critical period reopening. Together these results have important implications for the implementation of psychedelics in clinical practice, as well as the design of novel compounds for the treatment of neuropsychiatric disease.

## Key Findings

### Critical Period Reopening Is a Shared Property Across Psychedelics

All five tested psychedelics — psilocybin (0.3 mg/kg), LSD (1 μg/kg), MDMA (10 mg/kg), ketamine (3 mg/kg), and ibogaine (40 mg/kg) — reopened the social reward learning critical period when administered i.p. to adult mice (P96), assessed 48h later via the social conditioned place preference (sCPP) assay. Ibogaine-treated mice showed significant social reward learning (P = 3.17 × 10⁻⁵). Effects were dose-dependent and specific to social reward — psychedelic pretreatment did not alter cocaine reward learning or amphetamine-induced locomotor sensitization.

### Duration of Open State Is Proportional to Human Subjective Effects

The duration of the critical period open state varied systematically across compounds, in direct proportion to the duration of acute subjective effects reported in humans:

| Psychedelic | Human subjective effects | Mouse open state duration |
|---|---|---|
| Ketamine | 30 min – 2 h | 48 h (closed by 4 days) |
| MDMA | 3–6 h | 2 weeks |
| Psilocybin | 3–6 h | 2 weeks |
| LSD | 8–10 h | 3 weeks |
| **Ibogaine** | **36–72 h** | **≥4 weeks (still open at 4 wk)** |

Ibogaine produced the longest-lasting open state of any psychedelic tested — still open at 4 weeks, the latest time point measured. Increasing LSD dose to 50 μg/kg did not extend its open state duration.

### Metaplasticity, Not Hyperplasticity

Psychedelic pretreatment induced metaplastic restoration of oxytocin-mediated long-term depression (LTD) in the nucleus accumbens (NAc). Oxytocin application decreased mEPSC frequency (but not amplitude) in medium spiny neurons from psychedelic-pretreated mice, including ibogaine (P = 0.013), but not from saline- or cocaine-treated mice. Critically, no baseline changes in mEPSC amplitude or frequency were observed in the NAc or mPFC — ruling out hyperplasticity and distinguishing psychedelics from addictive drugs.

### 5-HT₂A Receptor Is Not a Universal Mechanism

Ketanserin (5-HT₂A antagonist) blocked critical period reopening by LSD and psilocybin but did not block MDMA or ketamine. This demonstrates that the critical period reopening property unifies psychedelics across receptor classes, violating the 5-HT₂A-binding classification boundary.

### β-Arrestin-2 Is Not a Universal Mechanism

In β-arrestin-2 knockout mice, LSD and MDMA failed to reopen the critical period, but ketamine and ibogaine retained full efficacy. This indicates ibogaine operates through a β-arrestin-2-independent signalling pathway for critical period reopening.

### Convergent Molecular Mechanism: Extracellular Matrix Remodelling

RNA sequencing of microdissected NAc identified 65 differentially expressed genes in "open state" versus "closed state" conditions (q ≤ 0.1, Benjamini–Hochberg). Gene set enrichment revealed significant ontologies for endothelial development, angiogenesis, vascular development, and tissue morphogenesis. Top-scoring genes included ECM components and regulators: Fn1 (fibronectin), Mmp16 (matrix metalloproteinase-16), Trpv4, Tinagl1, Nostrin, Cxcr4, Adgre5, Robo4, and Sema3g. Immediate early genes (Fos, Junb, Arc, Dusp) were also differentially expressed.

The working model proposes that psychedelics act on diverse binding targets (SERT, 5-HT₂A, NMDAR, KOR) to trigger downstream activity-dependent ECM degradation, which is the permissive event enabling metaplasticity. Transcriptional upregulation of ECM components (e.g., FN1) and downregulation of proteolytic enzymes (e.g., MMP-16) reflects homeostatic response to these changes.

## Methodology

- **Species:** Male C57BL/6J mice (congenic wild-type); β-arrestin-2 KO mice (Jackson Labs stock no. 011130)
- **Age:** Adult (P96–P98) for treatment; tested at P98 and subsequent time points
- **Behavioural assay:** Social conditioned place preference (sCPP) — bedding cue pairing with social vs. isolation conditioning
- **Drug administration:** All i.p.; ibogaine 40 mg/kg (gift from NIDA)
- **Electrophysiology:** Whole-cell voltage-clamp recordings from MSNs in NAc; mEPSC frequency and amplitude measured after oxytocin bath application
- **RNA sequencing:** Microdissected NAc, strand-specific libraries, NovaSeq 6000, ~77M paired-end reads/sample; transcript-level abundances via kallisto, gene-level analysis via sleuth
- **Statistics:** Two-tailed paired/unpaired t-tests, MANOVA, natural spline regression model, likelihood ratio tests; n = 4–23 mice per group depending on experiment

## Ibogaine-Specific Findings

Ibogaine occupies a unique position in this study's findings:

1. **Longest open state:** ≥4 weeks — still open at the latest time point measured, consistent with ibogaine's 36–72 h duration of subjective effects in humans (the longest of any tested psychedelic)
2. **5-HT₂A-independent:** Critical period reopening by ibogaine was not tested with ketanserin, but ibogaine's principal binding target is KOR, not 5-HT₂A
3. **β-Arrestin-2-independent:** Ibogaine retains full efficacy in β-arr2 KO mice (P = 0.016), unlike LSD and MDMA — consistent with ibogaine acting primarily via ionotropic rather than metabotropic GPCR signalling
4. **Metaplasticity confirmed:** Ibogaine pretreatment restored oxytocin-mediated LTD in NAc (P = 0.013)
5. **No addiction-like enhancement:** Psychedelic pretreatment, including ibogaine, did not alter cocaine CPP or amphetamine sensitization

## Clinical Implications

This study provides a foundational mechanistic framework for understanding ibogaine's durable therapeutic effects:

**Integration period rationale:** The proportionality between subjective effect duration and critical period open state duration provides a neurobiological basis for the post-treatment integration period. Ibogaine's ≥4 week open state — longer than any other psychedelic tested — suggests the therapeutic window following ibogaine treatment may be substantially longer than for other psychedelics. This directly supports extended integration protocols and suggests that therapeutic interventions during this window (psychotherapy, behavioural restructuring, social reconnection) may be particularly effective.

**Metaplasticity vs. hyperplasticity distinction:** The finding that psychedelics induce metaplasticity (increased capacity for plasticity) rather than hyperplasticity (direct structural changes) is clinically important. It suggests ibogaine creates a permissive state for change that requires appropriate environmental context (set and setting) to produce therapeutic benefit — consistent with clinical observations that ibogaine alone without integration support yields poorer outcomes.

**ECM remodelling as shared mechanism:** The convergent downstream mechanism (ECM remodelling) applies regardless of primary binding target. This explains how ibogaine, which acts primarily at KOR rather than 5-HT₂A, produces therapeutic effects comparable to serotonergic psychedelics.

**"Master key" hypothesis:** The authors propose psychedelics may serve as a "master key" for unlocking a broad range of critical periods beyond social reward learning. This framework expands the potential therapeutic scope of ibogaine to include conditions involving developmental critical period closure (autism, stroke recovery, sensory restoration).

## Limitations

- Mouse model only; translation to human critical period dynamics is inferred but not demonstrated
- Only male mice were used, limiting generalisability
- Ibogaine's open state duration may extend beyond 4 weeks — the latest time point tested was the maximum duration observed
- RNA sequencing was performed for saline, cocaine, ketamine, LSD, and MDMA conditions only — ibogaine was not included in the transcriptomic analysis
- The "critical period" framing for social reward learning in mice may not directly map to human therapeutic processes
- Ibogaine dose (40 mg/kg i.p. in mice) may not be directly comparable to human oral dosing
