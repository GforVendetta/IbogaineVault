# The Ibogaine Story — Chapter 8: Nico Adriaans

## Overview

Chapter 8 documents the treatment of Nico Adriaans — founder of the Dutch Junkiebond (junkie union) and community field worker at Erasmus University's Addiction Research Centre — on 5 May 1990. This was arguably the most politically significant ibogaine treatment of the early era: Nico was the European analogue of a civil rights leader, and his conversion to ibogaine advocacy opened the door to the Dutch harm reduction establishment. The chapter contains the most detailed phenomenological account in the book's first half, including the encounter with "the Voice" that became a touchstone of ibogaine literature.

The chapter also embeds significant WHITE political history: the founding of the Dutch Junkiebond, the squatter-junkie alliance, Dana Beal's ACT UP organising, and the media campaign that produced the *Village Voice* "MIRACLE CURE?" article.

---

## The Dutch Junkiebond and Harm Reduction Origins (WHITE)

Nico's backstory is a compressed history of European harm reduction. In 1979, when the mayors of Amsterdam, Rotterdam, The Hague, and Utrecht proposed forced therapy for heroin addicts, Nico — whose organising instincts were forged in an orphanage — assembled nearly 100 addicts for the founding meeting of the Junkiebond. They produced a manifesto for junkie liberation, a bill of rights, and a telegram to the mayors declaring forced commitment unacceptable.

The Junkiebond formed an alliance with the squatters' movement. Together they staged a direct action at Schlauffenberg's Bank — laundering heroin profits into real estate — showering the lobby with photocopied black-and-white currency and bags of talcum powder (imitating heroin). Days later, police raided the bank and found double books and money-laundering evidence. Thereafter, street-level harassment of junkies diminished.

At peak strength, the Junkiebond had approximately 25 locals across Holland and several in Germany. They established the **world's first needle exchange** (1981, for hepatitis prevention) and conducted the **first AIDS education in the world** among injecting drug users.

## Treatment: 5 May 1990

Nico was using three-quarters of a gram of high-grade Dutch heroin daily. He had tried to quit many times but could never sustain withdrawal: "First you go on a holiday; then you think, 'Just five more minutes,' then you say, 'Shit, it's for the fishes.'" He read Sisko's *Nine Case Histories* and Chapter 18 of Fernandez's *BWITI* in preparation.

The night before treatment, he did his last shot (~150mg of 50% pure Dutch heroin). The morning of, only tea on an empty stomach.

**Dosing protocol:** Allergy test dose (10mg), then first half of full dose. Within ten minutes, Nico recognised "the first little vibrations" from LSD experience. After the second half (30 minutes later), he became aware that **he no longer had any junk sickness**. Object edges began oscillating. He requested coffee, became briefly confused, then suddenly clear: "he knew."

## Phenomenological Account (PURPLE)

Nico's experience unfolded in distinct phases:

**Phase 1 — Sensory distortion and cosmic impact:** "Certain sounds would suddenly become like synthesized voices... I felt sea legs." Behind a curtain in a darkened room, the moisture on the surface of his closed eyes "started running all over each other, becoming blue, yellow... depending on how thick the tears were." Then came the sensation the Bwiti call "the splitting of the skull" — cosmic impact without pain. "There you are — O God, O God, O God. Oh, man! What an experience, Ibogaine!"

**Phase 2 — Archetypal visions:** On the darkened walls he saw images corresponding to the African tradition: "the woman in the tent, the Priest... archetypes." He became certain he had been "introduced by... the Bwiti."

**Phase 3 — Encounter with the Voice:** Articulated more fully nine months later:

"At the end of the visions... I had a meeting with God, or the Supreme Being. There was clouds. And it was blue. And then, up there somewhere, some sort of a rectangle opened up. And there came a lot of light... increasing, increasing, increasing... And then a Voice came to me and said: 'Do you KNOW, now?' And I only could say: 'Yes, I know now.' And then the Voice said: 'So you know! — ACT like it!'"

**Phase 4 — Integration and mild withdrawal:** Diminishing visualisations gave way to a prolonged reflective period, then nausea (purgative, not withdrawal-type). Finally, minor withdrawal signs — hot and cold flashes, sweating — but no running nose, diarrhoea, intestinal contractions, or muscle cramps. "You're still not totally in control of your body... you want to be left alone."

## Cigarette Interruption (Confirmed)

Two-and-a-half days post-treatment, Nico — who had only intended to quit heroin — attempted a cigarette. After one drag, "he tried to spit the taste out of his mouth and threw it out the window." With nicotine tolerance stripped, the cigarette was sickening. His hand-rolling habit persisted (motor pattern), but the pharmacological reward was gone. This confirmed Sisko's earlier finding that ibogaine interrupts cigarette addiction.

---

## Craving Suppression and the Ibogaine Learning Curve

During his truncated withdrawal, Nico's girlfriend arranged for him to slip out for heroin. But at the appointed time: "The Ibogaine let me stay in the bed... I couldn't want to go out." The familiar craving that had organised his daily existence for his entire adult life was simply absent.

Both Nico and Josien later relapsed, requiring re-treatment. When asked how someone could relapse after such a profound experience, Sisko replied: "He forgot. On Ibogaine he will remember again." This exchange crystallised the concept of the **ibogaine learning curve** — through repeated treatments, sustained freedom becomes possible because the individual has "learned how to quit."

---

## Subsequent Impact: Dutch Treatments and the Nico Video

Nico became a convert. He and Josien, using Sisko's remaining ibogaine supply, tested ibogaine on worst-case addicts at Amsterdam's Central Station — without Bwiti orientation, only "Take this, it will stop withdrawal." A methadonian couple were treated: the man went from **180mg to 30mg methadone** in a single jump (treatment staff had "never seen anyone go from 180 to 30 milligrams in one jump — it would be fatal" under normal circumstances), and the woman discontinued thorazine, no longer believing herself possessed.

The videotape of Nico's treatment, re-edited by Dana Beal and distributed in hundreds of copies worldwide, became the key media artefact that unlocked support among AIDS activists and the legalisation movement.
