# The Ibogaine Story — Chapter 12: VALIS

## Overview

Chapter 12 is the theological and theoretical heart of the book, framing ibogaine through Philip K. Dick's gnostic cosmology in *VALIS* (1981) while documenting two clinically significant treatments and advancing the cerebellar mechanism hypothesis. The chapter operates on three simultaneous registers: PURPLE phenomenological framework (VALIS as "roadmap to understanding gnostic substances, Ibogaine and Bwiti"), WHITE political timeline (1990–1993, from Omnichem supply deal through Clinton election to Dhoruba Bin Wahad's sentence vacation), and ORANGE pharmacological theory (cerebellar receptor site, REM-like states, NDE reflex model).

The chapter's centrepiece clinical events are the treatment of **Carol Baker** — the first HIV-positive person treated with ibogaine, detoxed from dual 80mg methadone/\$250-per-day heroin addiction in a single uneventful 22-hour session — and **Mark Lamontia**, whose January 1993 treatment in Rotterdam/Leiden produced the first rigorous follow-up in ibogaine history: weekly urines and a complete physical examination by Dr Clark at 45 days. Both treatments were conducted under the supervision of Jan Bastiaans, the Dutch psychiatrist who pioneered LSD treatment for concentration camp survivors.

Pharmacologically, the chapter synthesises Carlo Contoreggi's cerebellar receptor hypothesis with Deportere's EEG data and Goutarel's monograph to construct a unified model: ibogaine triggers a near-death experience (NDE) reflex that activates both serotonergic and cholinergic pathways simultaneously, enabling direct conscious access to PGO wave material — a "stereoscopic" scanning of ancestral memory encoded in non-nucleated genetic material.

## VALIS as Gnostic Framework (PURPLE)

The chapter opens with Dana Beal re-reading Philip K. Dick's *VALIS* in late July 1990, recognising in it "a roadmap to understanding gnostic substances, Ibogaine and Bwiti." Dick's novel, based on his own 1974 near-death experience (from which he awoke speaking ancient koine Greek), provides what Beal presents as the closest Western literary analogue to ibogaine phenomenology.

The key parallel is Dick's concept of **two-world superimposition** — seeing both present-day California and ancient Rome simultaneously, perceiving within that overlay a shared Gestalt: the "Black Iron Prison," a supra-temporal constant in which all humanity is enclosed without realising it. This maps directly onto ibogaine's characteristic "splitting of the skull," where initiates perceive their ordinary reality overlaid with visionary content. Dick's "secret early Christians" — beings who exist trans-temporally and are engaged in demolishing the Prison from within — become, in Beal's reading, an analogue for the Bwiti initiatory tradition.

The chapter foregrounds Dick's *Exegesis* Entry 48 as the passage that convinced Beal of the parallel. This entry describes humanity as "memory coils (DNA carriers capable of experience) in a computer-like thinking system" suffering a "malfunction — a failure — of memory retrieval." Salvation comes through *anamnesis* (loss of amnesia), and the external information or *gnosis* "consists of disinhibiting instructions, with the core content actually intrinsic to us — that is, already there (first observed by Plato; viz: that learning is a form of remembering)." The sacramental technologies of the Greco-Roman mystery religions and early Christianity were techniques for inducing this "firing and retrieval."

Beal connects this to James Fernandez's ethnographic observation that for Bwiti practitioners, "religion was not a matter of faith... It was a very pragmatic technique for understanding, predicting and controlling — in short a science or pre-science of hidden things." Both Gnosticism and Bwiti, unlike mainstream Western religion, rest on direct experiential evidence via plant sacrament rather than faith despite absence of evidence.

## Political Timeline: 1990–1993 (WHITE)

### Omnichem Supply Deal and Early Manoeuvres

Howard Lotsof secured a deal with Belgian pharmaceutical giant Omnichem, which manufactured ibogaine as a by-product of the leukaemia drug vincristine and had been stockpiling rather than disposing of 99.7% pure semi-synthetic ibogaine. Lotsof effectively cornered the world supply. Simultaneously, Deborah Mash — married to the Chairman of the Dade County Democratic Committee — leveraged the importance of the Florida primary to arrange a meeting with Hillary Clinton, briefing her for over an hour on ibogaine and securing a promise to act if the Clintons reached the White House.

### "Hank the Skank" and the Philadelphia Inquirer Breakthrough

In April 1992, a disgruntled individual posted stickers around New York reading "DRUG MULES: Earn Big Bux" with the project's phone number. When a *Philadelphia Inquirer* reporter called to investigate, Beal redirected: "Oh, a sticker. It's put up by a cranker in Brooklyn who's trying to discredit what we really do, which is to work on a cure for hard drugs, an African rainforest alkaloid called Ibogaine." This accidental contact produced the 4 July 1992 front-page *Inquirer* story — "TO BATTLE ADDICTION, HE ADVOCATES USE OF A DRUG" — which Beal describes as "a Declaration of Independence for Addicts."

### Herb Kleber's Counter-Campaign

Behind the scenes, Herb Kleber (Columbia University Substance Abuse Division) mounted systematic opposition. After torpedoing an ACT UP/CASA meeting, Kleber activated associates embedded in NIDA's peer review process to cut off new grants for ibogaine in the extramural system — the mechanism Stanley Glick had used to fund all his research. When Carlo Contoreggi began engaging with ACT UP, calls from Kleber to his superiors at the ARC followed immediately: "What is Contoreggi up to? Who's he seeing in New York?"

### 1992 Cocaine Efficacy Evidence

Three significant animal studies coalesced: M.R. Dzoljic presented data showing ibogaine diminished cocaine self-administration in rats by 90% (on all other substances, they self-administered until death). Henry Sershen (Nathan Kline Institute) confirmed reduction of cocaine-induced dopaminergic and locomotor activity. Patricia Broderick published further interaction data. A scientific consensus was forming around cocaine efficacy, undermining bureaucrats advocating for a cocaine blocker.

### Dhoruba Bin Wahad and the Queens Proceedings

Dhoruba appeared on Phil Donohue via Flo Kennedy's connection, becoming "the most articulate Black radical since Malcolm." On 23 December 1992, Judge Bruce Allen vacated Dhoruba's life sentence a second time, finding FBI and Red Squad misconduct so egregious it had materially influenced the original trial. Meanwhile, Beal navigated his own Queens court proceedings: a Clayton motion (arguing society's need for his ibogaine work should be balanced against marijuana charges), a 60-day plea bargain, and ultimately a sentence converted to community service after the judge read the *Newsday* ibogaine feature.

## Carol Baker Treatment: First HIV-Positive Ibogaine Treatment

Carol Baker was the first HIV-positive person treated with ibogaine. She presented with dual addiction: 80mg methadone daily (to manage the day) and $250 of heroin nightly (as a successful real estate broker, she could afford it). She had no veins left and wanted to live out her remaining life free of methadone fog.

After an **uneventful 22-hour treatment** and a short nap, Baker reported feeling as though "her armour had been stripped away." She underwent post-treatment evaluation at the University of Miami with Dr Mash and Dr Sergio Ramirez. Presenting at ACT UP, she stated: "I kicked methadone once before and it was five months of Hell. This time I woke up, I hadn't had a fix in 24 hours, and I wasn't dopesick. It was like a miracle."

On the visualisations: "You see millions of visions when you close your eyes. But when you open them, you come back to where you are. You realise it was all mental." Baker later reported a qualitative shift in craving management: whereas previously, once the craving for heroin locked in, she could never break out of the routine of going to cop, after ibogaine she was always able to divert herself into alternative activities.

## Mark Lamontia Treatment: First Rigorous Follow-Up

Mark Lamontia participated in the January 1993 Rotterdam/Leiden international treatment seminar organised by Sisko, Boaz, and Lotsof. He had been on 35mg methadone daily and was in imminent danger of ejection from Reality House, unable to keep his urines free of cocaine or heroin for longer than a week.

The treatment team comprised Rommell Washington, Dr Clark (head of Harlem Hospital's Substance Abuse Unit), Hans-Georg Behr, Boaz, the Israelis, Sergio Ramirez, and Deborah Mash. Jan Bastiaans supervised: when Mash asked what he could tell them about how it really worked after his ten supervised treatments, Bastiaans replied: "Only 10 treatments. But you're missing the important thing. It works."

Mash tracked the treatment on remote video, sending samples on dry ice by Federal Express to the University of Miami. **Lamontia came off 35mg methadone in 3 days with an uncanny absence of withdrawal.** Another subject in the same seminar came off 125mg methadone. At 45 days, Dr Clark performed a complete physical examination — the first rigorous post-treatment follow-up in ibogaine history, with weekly urines throughout.

This was the "newsflash" Clark took back to Harlem: "You have no idea of what it means to treatment professionals to be able to give a patient something that works. An informational shock wave is sweeping through Harlem, as the word is confirmed by people they respect: It works. It really works."

## Cerebellar Mechanism Hypothesis (ORANGE)

Molliver's discovery of selective brain toxicity at 100mg/kg i.p. in rats (equivalent to approximately 11 times Lotsof's therapeutic dose, given intraperitoneal injection is roughly twice as toxic as oral administration) pointed to a possible ibogaine receptor site in the cerebellum — a brain region considered unrelated to the dopamine "pleasure pathway."

Contoreggi's insight was that such selective toxicity is often a strong clue to receptor localisation. The cerebellum governs input from muscles, joints, and tendons; it regulates balance, spatial location, and body position. Crucially, it is where you learn to walk at age one — deep procedural conditioning that, once acquired, operates without conscious oversight.

The hypothesis: **true addiction involves the same kind of deep conditioning of the cerebellum as learning to walk.** Addiction doesn't occur solely at the dopamine "common narcotic receptor" but through the pleasure pathways *in the cerebellum.* Once you learn to short-circuit the conscious brain — once addicted — you do it without thinking about it. "The addict's feet literally walk him down to the dope spot on their own."

This implies there is no "magic bullet" for addiction without going into the cerebellum. And there is no cerebellar action without the tremor or visualisations — both are effects of the acetylcholine pathways involved in REM, which key directly into the brain regions governing walking, running, and movement. Ibogaine works through these acetylcholine pathways; therefore there can be no interrupter effect without behavioural immobility and tremor.

## REM-Like States and the NDE Reflex (ORANGE)

Deportere's paper on "Neocortical Rhythmic Slow Activity During Wakefulness and Paradoxical Sleep in Rats" established that ibogaine tremor and behavioural immobility are a function of stimulation of acetylcholine pathways — those active during sleep and REM. However, an EEG signature of true REM activity was absent under ibogaine, leading Beal to modify the characterisation of the visualisation phase to "REM-like" rather than true REM.

Robert Goutarel's monograph provided the pharmacological scaffolding. Ibogaine inhibits MAO-mediated oxidation of serotonin while catalysing catecholamine oxidation. As a potent serotoninergic, it reduces cerebral catecholamines (especially dopamine), explaining blockade of dopamine stimulation induced by morphine or cocaine. The decreased catecholamines combined with increased cerebral serotonin suppress REM sleep while producing hallucinatory phenomena. Goutarel notes ibogaine's domain is "that of the subterranean world of Freud, of animal impulse and of regression" — fundamentally different from LSD's "high and angelic domain of aesthetic sensations."

Michel Jouvet (letter of 7 November 1990) clarified that hallucinations and dreams cannot be directly linked as one phenomenon. The principal difference is in how wakefulness stages are reorganised: suppression of REM sleep with intrusion of PGO waves into the arousal and NREM stages. The new organisation becomes: waking stage → PGO wave stage → hallucination stage → sleep stage. Hallucinatory manifestations — the waking dream — eliminate "residues" stirred up by PGO wave activity in the absence of REM sleep. This aligns with Jouvet and Crick's hypothesis that PGO waves serve as the principal coding tool for recording genetic and epigenetic acquisitions necessary for individuation, while REM sleep sorts and disposes of residues.

### The NDE Model

The chapter synthesises these elements into a unified model: the brain is capable of generating a state the conscious mind recognises as "dreamlike" but which is not ordinary dreaming. A genuine emergency can trigger a **survival reflex — the NDE** — which activates both halves of the brain simultaneously and "stereoscopically." The serotonergic pathways (organised as the ego) gain direct access to the disorganised activity of the cholinergic pathways, perceived as "five or six television programmes going at once."

Ibogaine triggers this NDE reflex chemically, without requiring proximity to death. The "splitting of the skull" is equivalent to the hypnic jerk at sleep onset, massively amplified because serotonin and acetylcholine are pumping simultaneously while dopamine is suppressed — a neurochemical configuration that normally never occurs. In this state, the conscious mind accesses PGO wave material: direct genetic instructions from non-nucleated genetic material in all cells. These maternal-line genes — passed on without recombination, losing nothing from generation to generation unless struck by cosmic radiation — encode ancestral memories extending back approximately a quarter of a million years.

## Gnostic Synthesis: Grail, Blood, Burning Bush

The chapter's final movement weaves together Dick's VALIS cosmology, Bwiti initiation, and Wagner's *Parsifal* into a unified gnostic interpretation. Dick's "Two Source Cosmogony" (Exegesis Entry 47) — describing paired hyper-universes, one healthy (NOMMO) and one decaying (YORUGA), projecting a hologramatic interface that is our perceived reality — maps onto Bwiti's origins: the Dogon people of Western Sudan, ancestral homeland of the Fang before the Fulani expansion.

The convergence of Grail, Blood, and Burning Bush in Bwiti theology provides the framework: the Grail (the healing sacrament of the ancient Gnostics, lost for two millennia), the Blood (the living transformative substance), and the Burning Bush (the plant that speaks with divine voice) are one. Fibonacci's constant — the "perfect rectangle" of 3:5 proportions, Parmenides' doorway — appears as the characteristic signature of chaos, the same higher-order pattern emerging in phenomena from galactic spirals to conch shells. In ibogaine phenomenology, this manifests as the "rectangle that opens up" described by Nico and other initiates as the visual field for visions.

Dick's concept of *Salvator Salvandas* — the "saved saviour" — maps onto Baker's and Lamontia's experiences: the addict whose wound will not heal (Amfortas in *Parsifal*) is healed by the same spear that wounded them. "Suffering is 'blessed' which frees you from suffering — as in getting you through the withdrawal instead of prolonging it with more heroin." The sacrament that heals the addiction was itself born from addicts' need.

The chapter concludes with what Beal calls the "Ibogaine Challenge" to established religion: "Bring us, O Cardinal, three junkies. Give them the wine and the wafer, and sit with them for forty-eight hours. We will bring three junkies also."
