## Adjunc've Medica'on U'lisa'on in Ibogaine-Assisted Detoxifica'on Therapy

## Strategic Implementa'on of Long-Ac'ng Benzodiazepines &amp; Cannabidiol-Rich Cannabis Oil:

The	judicious	incorporation	of	long-acting	benzodiazepines	(e.g.	diazepam,	clonazepam) and	full-spectrum	extract	cannabis	oil	with	a	high	CBD:	THC	ratio,	albeit	with	a	signiCicant	delta-9tetrahydrocannabinol	(THC)	presence,	is	critical	in	the	context	of	ibogaine-assisted	opioid detoxiCication	protocols.

Ibogaine	has	been	shown	to	have	negligible	effects	on	the	GABAergic	system	(Popik	et	al., 1995),	nor	does	ibogaine	bind	to	cannabinoid	CB1	or	CB2	receptors	(Li	et	al.,	2017).	Therefore, unlike	other	drugs	that	are	contraindicated	with	ibogaine,	benzodiazepines	and	cannabis	are	not considered	to	interact	dangerously	with	ibogaine.

It	is	imperative	to	note	that	this	document	primarily	covers	protocols	for	treating	patients who	are	non-benzodiazepine	dependent.	For	such	patients,	it	is	necessary	to	maintain	their regular	dose	of	a	long-acting	benzodiazepine;	it	is	important	to	emphasise	that under	no circumstances	should	they	attempt	to	reduce	their	dose	prior	to	treatment, as	this	can	cause	QT prolongation.	If	patients	seeking	treatment	are	taking	a	regular	dose	of	a	short-acting (alprazolam,	lorazepam,	triazolam,	etc.)	or	medium-acting	benzodiazepines	(temazepam, oxazepam,	estazolam,	etc.),	then	they	should	switch	to	a	long-acting	benzodiazepine	at	least	1 week	prior	to	treatment.	The	Ashton	Manual	provides	effective	clinical	protocols	for	calculating half-lives	and	equivalent	doses	of	different	benzodiazepines.

In	many	cases,	patients	who	are	benzodiazepine-dependent	are	polydrug	users	also dependent	on	one	or	more	other	substances	(opioids,	alcohol,	methamphetamine,	etc.);	these patients	must	be	informed	that	ibogaine	will	not	treat	their	benzodiazepine	dependency	to eliminate	any	expectation	that	they	can	cease	benzodiazepine	use	after	treatment.

## Objectives :

Anxiolytic	Facilitation	and	Enhanced	Psychotropic	Engagement :	Administration	is	aimed	at mitigating	psycho-physical	distress,	aiding	in	fostering	a	profound	connection	with	the introspective	journey	facilitated	by	ibogaine.

Post-therapeutic	Sleep	Recuperation	Enhancement :	These	adjunctive	agents	counteract ibogaine-induced	hyperarousal,	facilitating	essential	post-interventional	restorative	sleep. Residual	Withdrawal	Symptomatology	Mitigation :	Effective	in	addressing	residual	opiate withdrawal	symptoms,	which	are	prevalent	in	cases	of	substantial,	chronic	opioid	dependence.

## Risk	Management	Objectives :

- A. Careful	Consideration	of	Concomitant	Administration's	Effects	on	Blood	Pressure	(BP)	&amp; Heart	Rate	(HR) :	Administration	of	ibogaine	alone	will	lower	BP	&	HR	in	the	majority	of patients,	which	is	why	cardiac	monitoring	is	essential	during	treatment.	Both	benzodiazepines and	cannabis	oil	similarly	lower	BP	&	HR,	emphasising	the	importance	of	judicious	use	&	extrajudicious	dosage.	An	example	of	judicious	use	would	be	administering	10-20mg	of	diazepam	to a	patient	experiencing	distress	(BP	~110/70	&	HR	~80)	during	ibogaine	treatment. Psychophysical	distress	tends	to	counteract	the	BP	&	HR-lowering	effects	of	ibogaine,	making the	administration	of	benzodiazepines	or	cannabis	oil	safer.
- B. Pre-Screening	&	Assessment	During	Treatment	for	Suitability :	Patients	with	a	very	low resting	HR	(<50bpm),	or	with	a	BP	of	90/60	or	less	(while	not	taking	blood	pressure medication)	are	typically	excluded	from	ibogaine	treatment	altogether.	However,	even	if	a lower	dose	protocol	is	used	in	order	to	prevent	bradycardia	or	dangerously	low	BP,	such	a patient	would	not	be	suited	to	concomitant	administration	of	benzodiazepines	or	cannabis	oil for	the	same	reason.
- C. Psychological	Suitability	to	Cannabis :	A	sizeable	minority	of	individuals	will	respond adversely	to	the	administration	of	cannabis.	Although	this	number	is	reduced	with	respect	to minuscule	dosages	of	cannabis	oil,	it	is	important	to	consider	that	certain	patients	(particularly those	who	have	experienced	psychosis,	are	chronic	methamphetamine	or	cocaine	users,	or have	a	history	of	adverse	reactions	to	cannabis)	will	respond	negatively	to	cannabis	oil.	In these	cases,	if	ruled	safe	and	necessary,	it	is	more	appropriate	to	use	a	long-acting benzodiazepine	to	deal	with	psychophysical	distress.	The	most	ideal	candidates	for	cannabis oil	administration	during	ibogaine	treatment	are	patients	who	are	already	cannabis	users	and express	that	they	enjoy	the	psychoactive	effects	of	cannabis.
- D. 'Dulling'	of	Visionary	or	Psychotherapeutic	Effects :	Higher	doses	of	benzodiazepines (>30mg	of	diazepam)	may	attenuate	some	of	ibogaine's	psychologically	therapeutic	effects,	a phenomenon	that	should	be	taken	into	account	when	administering	benzodiazepines	to	a

- benzodiazepine-naive	patient.	Risks	and	beneCits	must	be	weighed	appropriately	when considering	judicious	use.
- E. AmpliLication	of	Visionary	or	Psychotherapeutic	Effects :	Conversely,	cannabis	may	amplify ibogaine's	psychoactive	effects,	which	must	also	be	taken	into	account	when	dosing.	It	is	often more	appropriate	to	administer	cannabis	oil	later	on	during	the	treatment	(particularly	during treatment	of	opioid-dependent	patients	to	minimise	residual	withdrawal)	after	the	'visionary' phase	has	passed	and	the	patient	has	entered	the	'contemplative	phase'	(approximately	8 hours	after	a	Clood	dose).	It	can	also	be	beneCicial	to	administer	cannabis	oil	after	the	primary psychoactive	effects	of	ibogaine	have	subside, such	as	on	the	'grey	day'	when	many	patients experience	signiCicant	psychophysical	discomfort.

## Methodology

## 1. Selec'on of Benzodiazepines Based on Pharmacokine'c Profiles:

The	pharmacological	preference	for	long-acting	benzodiazepines	over	short-acting	variants	is predicated	on	their	extended	half-lives,	ensuring	a	protracted,	gradual	decrease	in	plasma	drug levels.	This	pharmacokinetic	property	is	instrumental	in	averting	abrupt	neurochemical Cluctuations	that	may	precipitate	'rebound'	phenomena	resembling	benzodiazepine	withdrawal, which	is	particularly	dangerous	in	the	context	of	ibogaine	therapy.

## 2. Cardiac Electrophysiological Considera'ons and QT Interval Modula'on:

Empirical	observations	suggest	a	dose-dependent	attenuation	of	the	QT	interval	prolongation,	a known	cardiotoxic	risk	of	ibogaine,	through	the	concomitant	(and	to	a	lesser	extent,	individual) administration	of	the	aforementioned	adjunctive	medications.	This	cardioprotective	effect	is particularly	salient	in	patients	with	baseline	QT	interval	prolongation.

## 3. Cannabis Oil Pre-Treatment and Its Cardiac Implica'ons:

A	preparatory	regimen	involving	moderate	dosing	of	cannabis	oil	for	three	days	preceding ibogaine	administration	has	been	noted	to	induce	a	reduction	in	the	QT	interval,	typically	ranging between	20-40	milliseconds.	This	pre-emptive	measure	potentially	augments	cardiac	safety.

## 4. Opioid Withdrawal Ameliora'on and Synergis'c Poten'a'on with Cannabis Oil:

Independently,	cannabis	oil	exhibits	properties	conducive	to	the	amelioration	of	opioid withdrawal	symptoms.	When	used	in	conjunction	with	ibogaine,	these	effects	appear	to	be synergistically	enhanced,	offering	a	more	holistic	approach	to	managing	opioid	withdrawal.

## 5. Pa'ent-Centric Cannabis Oil Administra'on:

Essential	to	this	therapeutic	approach	is	the	meticulous	assessment	of	patient-speciCic	responses to	cannabis,	particularly	in	individuals	with	a	history	of	stimulant	misuse,	psychosis,	or	prior adverse	reactions	to	cannabis.	Such	assessment	is	crucial	in	determining	the	appropriateness	of cannabis	oil	as	an	adjuvant	treatment,	predominantly	in	opioid	detoxiCication	contexts.	The objectives	of	this	approach	include:

Dose-Dependent	EfLicacy	and	High-Risk	Patient	Management :	It	is	imperative	to acknowledge	the	dose-dependent	nature	of	cannabis	oil's	pharmacodynamic	effects.	Even	in populations	deemed	at	higher	risk	for	cannabis	administration	(e.g.,	individuals	with	a	history	of stimulant	misuse	or	psychotic	disorders),	therapeutic	beneCits	can	be	garnered	through meticulous,	incremental	dosing	strategies.	This	approach	necessitates	initiating	treatment	with minimal	doses,	followed	by	gradual	titration	based	on	patient	response	and	tolerability.

Benzodiazepine	Co-Administration	as	an	Anxiolytic	Strategy :	Concurrent	administration	of benzodiazepines	may	be	strategically	employed	to	mitigate	any	anxiogenic	or	psychotropic distress	potentially	induced	by	cannabis	oil.	This	combinatory	pharmacotherapy	approach leverages	the	anxiolytic	properties	of	benzodiazepines	to	create	a	more	controlled	and	reassuring therapeutic	environment	for	the	patient.

Differentiating	Oral	and	Inhaled	Cannabis	Experiences :	Orally	administered	cannabis	oil, processed	through	Cirst-pass	metabolism,	yields	11-hydroxy-THC,	a	metabolite	that	imparts	a fundamentally	different	experience	from	the	delta-9-THC	found	in	smoked	cannabis.	This difference	often	manifests	as	a	longer-duration	effect	that	is	subjectively	described	as	a	"body high,"	akin	to	a	comforting	"coating"	or	"security	blanket,"	contrasting	with	the	more	cerebral	and

potentially	anxiety-	or	paranoia-inducing	effects	of	certain	inhaled	cannabis	strains.	For	patients undergoing	ibogaine-assisted	opioid	detox,	understanding	this	distinction	is	crucial.	Oral	cannabis oil	can	be	an	invaluable	aid	in	easing	the	detoxiCication	process,	not	only	for	the	patient	but	also for	the	clinical	team.	Patients	are	encouraged	to	view	cannabis	oil	as	a	therapeutic	tool	that signiCicantly	diverges	in	its	effects	and	beneCits	from	their	previous	experiences	with	inhaled cannabis.

Embracing	a	Holistic	Philosophy	of	Compassionate	Care	and	Radical	Empathy :	In	the context	of	ibogaine-assisted	opioid	detoxiCication,	the	guiding	principle	is	rooted	in	an	ethos	of compassionate	care	and	detoxiCication.	This	philosophy	acknowledges	and	addresses	the	inherent complexities	and	adversities	of	life,	with	a	particular	focus	on	the	challenges	encountered	during opioid	detoxiCication,	seeking	to	alleviate	pain	and,	where	possible,	create	enjoyable	moments.

This	patient-centric	approach	marks	a	signiCicant	departure	from	traditional	punitive models	of	addiction	treatment,	embodying	a	paradigm	shift	that	may	be	unfamiliar	and	perhaps initially	perplexing	to	many	patients.	This	divergence	is	especially	notable	given	the	prevalent societal	narratives	surrounding	drug	dependency	and	its	management,	which	often	stigmatise and	marginalise	individuals	who	have	a	substance	use	disorder.	Adopting	this	compassionate approach	aims	to	foster	an	environment	of	understanding	and	support,	countering	the internalised	negative	perceptions	and	promoting	a	more	holistic,	empathetic	pathway	to	recovery.

## Outcomes	Achieved:

1. Facilitation	of	Neuropsychiatric	Equilibrium: enhanced	cardiac	safeguarding ,	psychophysical comfort ,	and patient	engagement during	ibogaine	therapy.	The	integrated administration	of	long-acting	benzodiazepines	alongside	cannabis	oil	engenders	a	state	of neuropsychiatric	homeostasis, minimising	instances	of	unnecessary	psychogenic	distress .	This equilibrium	is	pivotal	in	maintaining	mental	health	integrity	throughout	the	detoxiCication continuum.
2. Augmentation	of	Patient-Directed	Therapeutic	Engagement :	The	delineation	of	the pharmacological	distinctions	between	oral	and	inhaled	cannabis	serves	to	enhance patient autonomy	in	the	therapeutic	process .	This	educational	strategy empowers	patients ,	fostering	an environment	conducive	to	informed	self-management	and	active	participation	in	their	recovery journey.
3. Diminution	of	Recidivism	Through	Empathetic	Care	Practices :	The	principles	of compassionate	care and radical	empathy, by	holistically	addressing	the	biopsychosocial complexities	inherent	in	opioid	dependency,	serve	to	mitigate	the	propensity	for	relapse	postdetoxiCication.	This	is	achieved	by	cultivating	a	treatment	milieu	that	transcends	conventional punitive	paradigms,	promoting	a	more afLirmative	and	supportive	recovery	experience .
4. ReconKiguration	of	Patient	Perceptions	Regarding	Substance	Dependency	and Rehabilitation :	This	therapeutic	modality	aims	to	recalibrate	patient	perceptions	surrounding substance	use	and	the	recovery	process.	It	endeavours	to transition	from	internalised stigmatisation	to	a	more compassionate	and	insightful	understanding	of	their	dependency narrative ,	which	is	instrumental	in	fostering	long-term	recovery	and	psychological	resilience.
5. Consolidation	of	the	Therapeutic	Alliance :	Emphasizing	a patient-centred	approach and compassionate	care	fortiCies	the therapeutic	alliance	between	clinicians	and	patients .	This alliance	is	a	fundamental	component	in	the	efCicacy	of	treatment	modalities,	correlating	strongly with	positive	treatment	outcomes.
6. Elevation	of	Overall	Treatment	Satisfaction	Metrics :	The	positive	reception	to	the	distinct pharmacodynamic	experience	provided	by	oral	cannabis	oil	during	ibogaine	treatment,	in conjunction	with	the	empathetic	treatment	framework,	is	anticipated	to heighten	overall	patient satisfaction with	the	detoxiCication	process.
