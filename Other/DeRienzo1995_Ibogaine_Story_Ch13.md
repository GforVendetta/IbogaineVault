# The Ibogaine Story — Chapter 13: Bwiti

## Overview

Chapter 13 is the spiritual and cosmological heart of the book, articulating the Bwiti initiatory framework that underpins ibogaine's phenomenological dimension. Dana Beal weaves together Philip K. Dick's VALIS cosmogony, Fang ancestral memory traditions, holographic brain theory, and the Gnostic concept of the "saved saviour" (*salvator salvandus*) into an argument that ibogaine's visionary properties are not incidental side effects but the core mechanism — the original Gnostic sacrament that the wine and wafer only *represent*. Interlaced with this speculative theology are concrete clinical and political developments: the treatment of Mark Lamontia (off 35mg methadone in three days), the 1992 scientific consensus building around ibogaine's cocaine efficacy, and Dhoruba Bin Wahad's advocacy in the Black community.

---

## VALIS and the African Tradition

The chapter opens with Beal sharing the VALIS prophecy passage with Dhoruba after his return from Ghana. The passage — "The goal of your lives has been reached... the grim king, the king of tears, will not surrender his power. But you will take his power from him" — resonated immediately with Dhoruba's understanding of the African tradition, where the near-death experience (NDE) is considered a **medical fact**, not a metaphor or delusion. This sets the chapter's epistemological frame: Bwiti treats visionary experience as empirically real, contrasting sharply with the Western psychiatric framing of ibogaine's effects as "psychotomimetic" (the CIA-era classification by Harris Isbell and colleagues).

Beal's pivotal statement to Dhoruba captures the chapter's tension between scientific empiricism and cosmological speculation: "My orientation is primarily scientific... But something is happening."

## Bwiti Cosmogony

The chapter includes Barabe's summary of Fang cosmogony ("The Religion of Eboga or the Bwiti of the Fangs," May/June 1982), which Beal placed in the Harlem Hospital workshop workbook alongside VALIS selections for direct comparison:

**Mebeghe**, the supreme being without mother, father or spouse, engenders three divinities by bursting the "divine primordial egg." Nzame-Mebeghe (God) remains pure; Nyingone-Mebeghe (the female principle, goddess of fertility and night) commits *Nsem* (incest) with None at the instigation of Evus; None-Mebeghe (the male principle) likewise transgresses. Ekurana issues from the placenta of the divine egg and possesses thunder; Evus, Ekurana's twin, is the tempter punished by thunderclap. All these divinities are represented in the **Mbandja** — the temple where night-time ceremonies, initiations involving massive quantities of iboga root (often at puberty), funeral dances, and community gatherings take place.

Beal notes the sexual conservatism: Bwiti is described as a safe-sex-oriented, matriarchal religion, reacting to a devastating gonorrhoea epidemic at the turn of the century. Catholic colonial authorities harassed Bwiti, giving it a unifying effect during the Gabonese drive for independence.

The critical initiatory element is **meeting the ancestors**, which every person carries "holographically in their memory, on the cellular level." Collectively, the ancestors *are* Bwiti. During initiation, the neophyte may meet a trusted guide (often a parent) who accompanies them through the visions. Some initiates meet the Bwiti itself — what Evans Schultes described as "the universal African Ancestor."

## VALIS Two-Source Cosmogony and the Dogon Connection

Beal juxtaposes the Bwiti framework with Dick's Two-Source Cosmogony (VALIS entry #47): the One generates a diploid sac containing twin androgynies spinning in opposite directions (Yin and Yang). The counterclockwise twin breaks through prematurely, creating defective FORM II; the fullterm twin emerges as FORM I. From their dialectical interaction, they project the holographic universe we inhabit. The decaying condition of hyperuniverse II introduces entropy, suffering, chaos, death — "the Empire, the Black Iron Prison."

Crucially, Dick identifies these twins by name: **NOMMO** and **YORUGA** — "known to the Dogon people of Western Sudan in Africa." The ancestral home of the Fang, before being pushed south by the Fulani expansion, was the savannah of Western Sudan. According to Fernandez, the destination at the end of many initiates' visions is a grassy upland resembling the Fang ancestral homeland, where the initiate may find a village of the Ancestors or the "clear glass house" of their guardian angel or of Bwiti itself. This convergence — Dick's Gnostic cosmogony naming Dogon entities, the Fang originating from the same region — forms the core of Beal's argument that Bwiti and Gnostic Christianity share deep structural roots.

## The Fibonacci Doorway and Chaotic Attractors

During the ibogaine experience, serotonergic pathways react to the "splitting of the skull" — the shock of cholinergic pathway activation — by creating what many describe as a "rectangle" opening on the ceiling or wall for the visions. This is Nico's "rectangle that opens up" through which the Supreme Being speaks. Beal identifies this as a characteristic **signature of chaos**: the Fibonacci constant (3:5 ratio), the "perfect rectangle" of Parmenides used to build the Parthenon. Throughout the VALIS trilogy, it appears as the "doorway to the Other Realm."

The mechanism proposed: if the NDE reflex generates the same chaotic attractor shape as VALIS's Fibonacci reference beam, and the two lock together — "you have a match, and Nico meets God." This reframes the ibogaine visionary experience not as hallucination but as a resonance phenomenon between individual neurological chaos and a collective information field stored in non-nucleated genetic material.

## The Grail, the Saved Saviour, and the Ibogaine Challenge

Beal develops an extended reading of Wagner's *Parsifal* through Dick's Gnostic lens. Amfortas's unhealable wound can only be healed by the same spear that caused it — the *salvator salvandus*, the "saved saviour" concept from Gnostic tradition. Dick's interpretation: "Fat's Savior is Fat himself... Zebra is all the selves along the linear time-axis, laminated into one supra- or trans-temporal self which cannot die."

Applied to ibogaine: the "healing sacrament of the ancient Gnostics" is found when the shrine is opened — the Grail is the ibogaine experience itself, "the living blood of the Savior" that transforms any vessel it touches. The verse Dick claims not to understand — "May your suffering be blessed, / Which gave the timid fool / Pity's highest power / And purest knowledge's might!" — is explained by Nico's experience: suffering is "blessed" which frees you from suffering, as ibogaine gets you through withdrawal instead of prolonging it. "Pity's highest power" is the capacity to free all whose veins have been pierced by addiction by means of a healing alkaloid.

The chapter culminates in the **Ibogaine Challenge** to institutional Christianity — a direct comparison: "Bring us, O Cardinal, three junkies. Give them the wine and the wafer, and sit with them for forty-eight hours. We will bring three junkies also, give them Ibogaine, and sit with them for forty-eight hours."

## Pharmacological Note: Ibogaine and Vinblastine

In a passing but clinically notable observation, Beal reports that ibogaine is "the left half" of **vinblastine** (dimeric ibogaine, from the Madagascar periwinkle *Catharanthus roseus*) and the common component of **vincristine** — both Kaposi's Sarcoma chemotherapy drugs. Ibogaine is described as far less toxic than either. Contoreggi is reported to have submitted a protocol to study ibogaine as an anti-viral, with another for KS in preparation. Additionally: "Rats pre-treated with ibogaine recovered from electro-shock twice as fast as untreated rats."

## Treatment and Advocacy Developments (Late 1992–Early 1993)

### Mark Lamontia Treatment (January 1993)

Mark Lamontia, on 35mg daily methadone and unable to keep urines free of cocaine or heroin for longer than a week at Reality House, travelled to Holland for an international treatment seminar organised by Sisko, Boaz, and Howard Lotsof. Rommell Washington accompanied him as part of the treatment team, along with Dr Clark (head of Harlem Hospital's Substance Abuse Unit). Deborah Mash tracked the treatment on remote video, sending urine samples on dry ice to the University of Miami.

Key outcome: **Mark came off 35mg methadone in three days with "an uncanny absence of withdrawal."** Another subject came off 125mg. Forty-five days later, Dr Clark performed a complete physical — Mark "was like a new man." His follow-up included weekly urines — "the very thing NIDA MDD has been demanding." Dr Clark brought the confirmation back to Harlem's treatment community, generating what Beal describes as an "informational shock wave."

### Cocaine Efficacy Consensus (1992)

At the June 1992 Keystone session of the Conference on Problems of Drug Dependency, a body of evidence coalesced for ibogaine's efficacy against cocaine:
- **Dzoljic**: ibogaine diminished cocaine self-administration in rats by 90% (on everything else, rats injected until death)
- **Sershen** (Nathan Kline Institute): confirmed reduction of cocaine-induced dopaminergic and locomotor activity with ibogaine
- **Broderick**: published further data on ibogaine–cocaine interactions

Beal notes the strategic significance: there was no known pharmacological treatment for cocaine. All maintenance drugs had proven to be cocaine analogues, and attempts to find a dopamine transporter "blocker" (analogous to naltrexone for opioids) were failing because occupying a dopamine transporter without inhibiting reuptake is pharmacologically impossible — making all "blocker" candidates cocaine-like in effect.

### Dhoruba Bin Wahad and Political Organising

Dhoruba's life sentence was vacated for the second time on 23 December 1992 by Judge Bruce Allen, who found FBI and Red Squad misconduct had materially influenced the original trial. Dhoruba had already "gone out on a limb with the Black community on Ibogaine," discussing it on WBAI and at the Harlem Hospital teach-in, putting the Movement on record for an early community-based trial. Flo Kennedy (Billie Holiday's former lawyer, executor of the Holiday and Charlie Parker estates) became a significant ibogaine and Dhoruba supporter.

## Holographic Memory and VALIS

The chapter's underlying theoretical framework extends the holographic brain model from Chapter 14. Ancestors exist "holographically on the cellular level, in all the non-nucleated genetic material of all humans." This non-nucleated material generates what Dick calls VALIS — a vast electromagnetic field that "knows only holographically, therefore perceives the universe as a hologram." Direct communication with this field during waking consciousness risks permanent neurological damage from bioelectric overload; but in the dreamtime, VALIS can communicate digitally "via perturbation of non-nucleated genetic material," generating a Fibonacci-ratio reference beam to project the visionary doorway.

The slime mould analogy captures the collective-intelligence dimension: ordinarily individual amoebae, under stress they signal each other, cluster in wave patterns, and form a single differentiated organism. Beal suggests the ibogaine "shock wave" sweeping through communities functions analogously — "strange synchronicities play across the wavefront. Initiates gain augmented perception of time."

## Verification of Visionary Content

The chapter addresses the epistemological problem of distinguishing genuine theophany from delusion, drawing on Dick's two criteria: **(1) sudden healing** — "It was like a miracle," said Carol Baker; and **(2) imparted information that turns out to be true** — knowledge the subject had no way of possessing beforehand. Beal cites Fernandez's informant (see Chapter 18) who saw his brother lying dead along a road three weeks before it happened, and Geerte's boyfriend Adam who foresaw a friend falling from a fourteenth-storey window months in advance.

This empirical-verification framework positions Bwiti epistemology as testable rather than purely faith-based — an important distinction for the vault's PURPLE category, where phenomenological accounts need to be distinguished from purely subjective interpretation.
