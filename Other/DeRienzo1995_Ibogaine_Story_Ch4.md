# The Ibogaine Story — Chapter 4: Dana Beal

## Overview

Chapter 4 is the extended biography of Dana Beal, from his formative political experiences (shaking hands with JFK in 1960, witnessing the "I Have a Dream" speech at 16) through two decades of YIPPIE organising to the crystallisation of ibogaine as a political project in December 1980. The chapter documents how Beal's political trajectory — from New York Provos to White Panther Field Marshall to YIPPIE leader — established the organisational infrastructure that would later support ibogaine development. It also contains a significant phenomenological account of ALD-52 (5-acetyl-LSD), framed through Robert Goutarel's distinction between the "clear and angelic" LSD series and the "oneiric" (REM-generating) iboga-harmala group.

---

## Political Trajectory

The chapter traces Beal's path through the American counterculture:

- **1966:** First LSD experience; founded New York Provos (inspired by Dutch Provos article in the VILLAGE VOICE); initiated smoke-in movement in Tompkins Square Park
- **1967:** Busted for acid; 3,000 people marched from a Fugs concert to the federal holding pen
- **1968–69:** Fled to Canada; returned on Canadian ID; appointed White Panther Party Field Marshall in Ann Arbor
- **1970:** Organised first D.C. July 4 smoke-in after Abbie Hoffman cancelled it; drew three times more people than Nixon's "Honor America Day"
- **1971:** July 4 smoke-in included anti-CIA/Vietnam heroin march (covered on CBS Evening News); arrested in Madison, Wisconsin
- **1972:** At Miami Demcon, confronted McGovern on live television about CIA heroin trafficking and marijuana legalisation — a five-minute exchange that permanently blacklisted him in Democratic Party circles
- **1974:** First learned about ibogaine's anti-addiction properties from Lotsof during morning visits to his Brooklyn apartment
- **1980:** December — decided to initiate ibogaine development, framed as "willing to pay any price, bear any burden to win the war against addiction"

---

## The ALD-52 Vision (Spokane, 1974)

At a YIPPIE conference in Spokane, the inner core took ALD-52 (5-acetyl-LSD), which proved "ten or twenty times more psychedelic than LSD" — its effects lying "far more in the direction of Ibogaine" than typical LSD. Robert Goutarel's taxonomy is invoked: indole-alkalamines of the LSD series are fundamentally different from the iboga-harmala group — "clear and angelic" versus oneiric (REM-generating).

Beal's experience included Philip K. Dick-style "laminate personality" — co-existing at multiple points in time simultaneously (2,000 years ago, present, and future). A dominant personality from another era looked at the polluted river and judged it unacceptable for baptisms. When this personality noticed Beal's presence and learned he was a Baptist, it said: "Just make sure you don't get your head cut off this time!" Beal interpreted this as a directive to avoid unnecessary risks until his mission was accomplished.

When Beal later asked Lotsof whether ibogaine was more psychedelic than ALD-52, Lotsof replied: "On Ibogaine, you would have visualized the other personality as sitting there talking to you" — distinguishing ibogaine's waking-dream quality from LSD's ego-dissolution.

---

## The "Cure Not War" Thesis

The chapter culminates in Beal's attendance at a screening of PRINCE OF THE CITY (1981), the story of Danny Ciello and the NYPD narcotics corruption exposed by the Knapp Commission. The film crystallises two structural arguments:

1. **Prohibition generates corruption:** To penetrate above street level, agents must deal drugs or maintain dealer-informants, inevitably creating police-protected drug monopolies
2. **Policing depends on addiction:** Without junkie informants, police lose community intelligence — creating a structural incentive to maintain rather than eliminate addiction

The chapter ends with Beal's conclusion: "What we need is a cure, not a war" — the thesis that would drive the next fifteen years of ibogaine advocacy.

---

## Limitations

This is autobiography-as-history, written by the subject's co-author. The political narrative is teleological — every event in Beal's life is presented as leading toward ibogaine. The ALD-52 phenomenological account is a single experience from one individual. The McGovern confrontation and its consequences are presented entirely from Beal's perspective.
