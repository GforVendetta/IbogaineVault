# Kush in Sierra Leone: West Africa's Growing Synthetic Drugs Challenge

**Citation:** Bird Ruiz Benitez de Lugo, L., & de Bruijne, K. (2025). *Kush in Sierra Leone: West Africa's Growing Synthetic Drugs Challenge*. Global Initiative Against Transnational Organised Crime / Clingendael Institute.

> [!warning] External Context Document
> This is not ibogaine research. It is included as contextual intelligence for understanding the evolving synthetic opioid landscape that may affect patients presenting for ibogaine treatment.

---

## Why This Matters for Ibogaine Practice

1. **Nitazenes are now in West Africa** – patients with opioid histories may have unknowing exposure to synthetic opioids 25× more potent than fentanyl
2. **Withdrawal profiles differ** – nitazene withdrawal may present differently than traditional opioid withdrawal
3. **Harm reduction context** – understanding the broader drug landscape informs patient assessment
4. **Naloxone implications** – higher/multiple doses may be needed for nitazene overdose reversal

---

## Executive Summary

A synthetic drug known as "kush" emerged in Sierra Leone in the late 2010s and spread across West Africa (Liberia, Guinea, The Gambia, Guinea-Bissau, Senegal) with devastating effects. By April 2024, Sierra Leone and Liberia declared national emergencies.

### Key Chemical Findings

**Chemical testing conducted for this research concluded kush contains:**

| Component | Substances Detected | Potency vs Fentanyl |
|-----------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Nitazenes** (53% of samples) | Protonitazene, metonitazene, protonitazepyne | 1× to 25× |
| **Synthetic cannabinoids** (47% of samples) | MDMB-4en-PINACA | 7.5–9× THC |

**This is believed to be the first case of nitazenes penetrating West African drug markets.**

### What Kush Is NOT

Testing debunked myths that kush contains:
- ❌ Fentanyl
- ❌ PCP (phencyclidine)
- ❌ Methamphetamine
- ❌ Tramadol
- ❌ Human bones

## Nitazenes: The Critical Intelligence


Nitazenes are potent synthetic opioids that have spread rapidly across global retail drug markets since 2022.

### Potency Comparison

| Nitazene | Potency vs Fentanyl | Prevalence in Kush Samples |
|----------|--------------------|-----------------------------|
| Protonitazene | 3× more potent | 47% |
| Metonitazene | Similar or greater | 29% |
| Protonitazepyne | **25× more potent** | 18% |

### Clinical Implications

> "Brilliant students end up on my slab. Kush kills and deaths are on the increase."
> — Senior medical examiner, Freetown, June 2024

**Pathologies consistent with opioid overdose:**
- Foaming at nose and mouth ("foam cone")
- Aspiration of gastric contents
- Pulmonary oedema
- Ulceration of stomach and small intestine

**Naloxone considerations:** Naloxone is reportedly effective in reversing nitazene overdoses, but **large or multiple doses may be required**.

---

## MDMB-4en-PINACA: Synthetic Cannabinoid

- First detected in Europe 2017
- By 2021, most identified synthetic cannabinoid in Europe
- 7.5–9× more potent than delta-9 THC
- Common in UK ("spice"), Netherlands, Mauritius
- **No known antidote** for synthetic cannabinoid poisoning (unlike opioid overdose)

## Supply Chain Intelligence

### Points of Origin

| Source | What's Exported | Route |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| **China** | Nitazenes, MDMB-4en-PINACA, precursors | Postal courier services, online purchase (Alibaba, Made in China) |
| **UK** | Preprocessed kush, possibly active ingredients | Maritime |
| **Netherlands** | Preprocessed kush (confirmed seizure 2024), possibly nitazenes | Maritime |

### Local Synthesis

From ~2022, large-scale local synthesis began in Sierra Leone:
- Basic equipment: pot, cloth, mask, gloves, squeeze bottles
- Components: marshmallow leaf (imported), nail varnish remover (acetone), "chemical spray/powder", formalin
- Mistakes by inexperienced "cooks" contribute to overdose spikes

## Health Impact Data

### Mortality
- Group cremations organised as emergency measures (2022)
- Bodies placed in streets by kush-use space owners, collected by authorities in early morning
- PWUD report knowing 2–50 individuals who died from kush (Dec 2023–June 2024)

### Hospital Admissions
- Sierra Leone Psychiatric Teaching Hospital: **4,000% increase** in presentations for problematic drug use since 2020

## Harm Reduction Recommendations (from report)

1. **Increase naloxone access** – free distribution programme, training for first responders
2. **Opiate substitution therapy** – relevant for nitazene-kush users
3. **Alternatives to incarceration** for PWUD
4. **Enhanced training** for health professionals treating PWUD
5. **Early warning systems** – regional monitoring of synthetic drug compositions

---

## Relevance to Ibogaine Practice

### Patient Assessment Considerations

1. **Unknown nitazene exposure** – patients reporting opioid use may have consumed substances far more potent than assumed
2. **Withdrawal timeline** – nitazene withdrawal kinetics may differ from traditional opioids
3. **Tolerance assessment** – standard opioid tolerance metrics may underestimate actual exposure
4. **Geographic history** – patients with West African connections warrant specific inquiry

### Protocol Implications

- Pre-treatment urine screens may not detect novel synthetic opioids
- Withdrawal stabilisation may require longer observation periods
- Naloxone protocol review for emergency response
