## Phenomenological Characteristics

### The "Oneirophrenic" Quality
Ibogaine produces dream-like, visionary states distinct from "classical" serotonergic psychedelics.

- [[1969/Naranjo1969_Psychotherapeutic_Possibilities_Fantasy-Enhancing_Drugs|Naranjo 1969]] — **FOUNDATIONAL**: Claudio Naranjo's early work on ibogaine as "fantasy-enhancing." Compares ibogaine and harmaline as oneirophrenics.
- [[2025/LaTorre2025_Phenomenology_Thematic_Analysis|La Torre 2025]] — **LARGEST PHENOMENOLOGY STUDY**: n=471 participants. Thematic analysis capturing emotional amplification, life review, perceptual alteration, visionary states, and ibogaine's perceived "agency." Beond clinical setting data.
- [[2017/Heink2017_Phenomenology_Ibogaine_ASC|Heink 2017]] — **FIRST 5d-ASC APPLICATION TO IBOGAINE**: n=27 online survey using the 5-Dimensional Altered States of Consciousness scale. Ibogaine exceeded high-dose psilocybin (315 μg/kg) across all six ASC dimensions, with the largest discrepancy in Auditory Alterations (54.87 vs 11.53). Visual Restructuralisation correlated with perceived change (rho=.679); 92% reported craving reduction at 4 weeks. Critically, no correlation between ASC intensity and perceived effectiveness — suggesting pharmacological and experiential therapeutic mechanisms may be dissociable.
- [[2024/Ivan2024_Nonclassic_Psychedelic_Ibogaine_Disrupts_Cognitive_Maps|Ivan 2023]] — "Nonclassic psychedelic" — disrupts cognitive maps differently from serotonergic psychedelics
- [[2020/Kohek2020_Qualitive_Study_Acute_Subjective_Effects_of_Ibogaine|Barsuglia 2020]] — Acute subjective effects characterisation

### Transpersonal Dimensions
- [[2023/Dickinson2023_Transpersonal_Intersubjectivity_in_Ibogaine_Experiences|Dickinson 2023]] — **TRANSPERSONAL INTERSUBJECTIVITY**: Three in-depth narrative case reports spanning clinical (Mexico), traditional Bwiti initiation (Gabon), and solo home use. Goes beyond phenomenological *content* categorisation to investigate the *relational* quality of ibogaine experiences — all three participants reported a distinct sense of transpersonal communication with ibogaine itself or entities encountered in the oneirogenic state. Connects this to waking-REM phenomena (sleep paralysis). Author (Ambio Life Sciences) brings decade of practitioner experience and Bwiti initiation. Published in *Anthropology of Consciousness*.
- [[2024/Ozmat2024_Subjective_Ibogaine_Experiences_Intersection_Social-Ecological_Dimensions|Özmat 2024]] — Subjective experiences across intersecting social-ecological dimensions

### Cultural/Ritual Context
- [[2018/Rodger2018_Healing_Potential_Ibogaine|Rodger 2018]] — Comparative phenomenological analysis integrating Bwiti context
- [[2022/Rodriguez-Cano2022_Underground_Ibogaine_Use_for_SUD_Tx_Qualitative_Analysis_Subjective_Experiences|Rodríguez-Cano 2022]] — **UNDERGROUND SETTINGS**: ICEERS-led grounded theory analysis (n=13) of ibogaine use in non-clinical contexts — homes, motels, clinics without official recognition. International sample (7 countries). Three core psychological categories emerged: autobiographical/interpersonal processing, abreactive resolution, and transpersonal experiences. Uniquely documents the ecological reality of unregulated ibogaine use: 11/13 motivated by SUD, all reported mixed pleasant/unpleasant experience, and participants universally emphasised that ibogaine alone does not guarantee recovery — integration, motivation, and aftercare are decisive.
- [[Other/Albert2008_MyEboga_Experience_Reports|MyEboga Experiences (~2008)]] — **LARGEST FIRST-PERSON COMPILATION**: 12 detailed narrative accounts (6 chemical dependence, 6 non-dependence) from myeboga.com. Corroborates three-phase model from inside; documents fear as therapeutic gateway, perceived guiding intelligence, somatic processing, life review. Richest primary-source phenomenological archive in the vault.
- [[Primary_Sources/2020_MYEBOGA_Clare_Wilkins_Interview|MYEBOGA 2020]] — Clare Wilkins podcast interview: 800+ treatments, community perspectives

## Psychological & Therapeutic Mechanisms

### Trauma Processing
- [[1996/Lotsof1996_Trauma_Abreaction|Lotsof 1996]] — **FOUNDATIONAL**: Howard Lotsof's framework for trauma and abreaction in ibogaine therapy
- [[2004/Bastiaans2004_Life_After_Ibogaine|Bastiaans 2004]] — "Life After Ibogaine": qualitative outcomes research
- [[2018/Camlin2018_Phenomenological_Ibogaine_Opioid_Use|Camlin 2018]] — **PHENOMENOLOGICAL INVESTIGATION (CROSSROADS)**: Constant comparative method (n=10 OUD, Barsuglia/Polanco clinic). 100% reported withdrawal attenuation, craving elimination, and increased self-acceptance; 90% had traumatic histories driving substance use. Novel finding: 70% described subjective localised brain activation — "zapping" or "cleaning" of neurons — a phenomenon undocumented in prior literature. 80% experienced mixed dark/scary and joyful/peaceful imagery, consistent with exposure-based trauma processing. Authors propose integration with CBT, dream analysis, and logotherapy.
- [[2019/Brown2019_Ibogaine_Subjective_Experience_OUD|Brown & Noller 2019]] — **SCQ + THEMATIC ANALYSIS (MEXICO/NZ)**: Two-site observational study (n=44; Mexico n=30, New Zealand n=14). 43% achieved "complete mystical experience" on the States of Consciousness Questionnaire. Thematic analysis revealed a hierarchical process: anxiety → life review → confrontation → remorse → empathy → letting go → transformation (the most prominent theme, 32 utterances). Critically, no significant correlation between SCQ scores and treatment outcomes at 1, 6, or 12 months — consistent with Heink 2017's finding that ASC intensity and clinical effectiveness may be dissociable. Proposes ibogaine's oneiric effects as a discrete therapeutic element, not mere epiphenomenon. Co-authored with Geoff Noller.
- [[2026/Olash2026_Subjective_Experience_Magnesium_Ibogaine|Olash 2026]] — **MISTIC PHENOMENOLOGY** (npj Mental Health Research): Qualitative companion to Cherian 2024. n=30 SOF veterans describe four domains: dialogic trauma reappraisal, altered-self/mystical connection, emotional resolution, embodied brain healing. Frames ibogaine as "accelerated auto-psychotherapy" compressing CBT, exposure, and humanistic processes into single session. *Dedicated to Nolan Williams (d. 2025).*
- [[2025/Brown2025_Mystical_Experience_PTSD_Veterans|Brown R 2025]] — **MISTIC: MYSTICAL EXPERIENCE PREDICTS PTSD OUTCOMES** (Journal of Affective Disorders): Quantitative companion to Olash 2026. n=30 SOF veterans with TBI. MEQ30 intensity predicted larger PTSD reductions both immediately (B=-5.89, p<.001) and at 1 month (B=-4.45, p=.007). Greater mystical experience also associated with sustained decreases in peak alpha frequency on EEG — a neurophysiological marker previously linked to ibogaine's clinical effects. Unlike Heink 2017 and Brown 2019's null findings with 5d-ASC/SCQ, the MEQ30 captured a significant experience-outcome relationship, suggesting instrument sensitivity matters for ibogaine's atypical phenomenology.

### Narrative Self & Identity Transformation
- [[2025/Underwood2025_PhD_Experiences_Part_of_Journey|Underwood 2025]] — **PhD THESIS: FIRST PCI APPLICATION TO IBOGAINE**: Canterbury Christ Church doctoral research comprising PCI consciousness mapping (n=21), narrative self questionnaire (n=38) + IPA interviews (n=10), and 32-month longitudinal case study. Visual imagery correlates with self-awareness. 97% report narrative self transformation across 11 dimensions (agency, autobiographical reasoning, meaning-making, etc.). Proposes integration protocol framework positioning psychedelic experiences as central to recovery.

### Integration & Aftercare
- [[2005/Kroupa2005_Boosters_Maintenance|Kroupa & Wells 2005]] — The "noribogaine window" concept for integration

### Consumer Perspectives & Online Communities
- [[2020/Barber2020_Consumer_Views_Online_Fora|Barber et al. 2020]] — **FIRST SYSTEMATIC STUDY OF ONLINE COMMUNITIES**: Qualitative thematic analysis of Reddit and Drugs Forum (n=101 participants). Documents how fora serve as DIY research hubs; reveals tension between pharmacological "brain reset" and visionary experience frameworks; shows cost barriers driving home administration despite safety preference for clinics. Important harm reduction implications.

## Quantitative Assessment

### Scale Development
- [[2025/Espejito2025_Ibogaine_Experience_Scale_Psychometrics_Subjective|Espejito 2025]] — **IES VALIDATION**: 70-item Ibogaine Experience Scale capturing oneiric/dream-like subjective effects. ICEERS-led psychometric development.
- [[2025/Underwood2025_PhD_Experiences_Part_of_Journey|Underwood 2025]] — **FIRST PCI APPLICATION**: Phenomenology of Consciousness Inventory applied to ibogaine (n=21). Visual imagery strongly correlates with self-awareness (r=.61). Establishes baseline for future consciousness mapping research.
- [[2017/Heink2017_Phenomenology_Ibogaine_ASC|Heink 2017]] — **FIRST 5d-ASC APPLICATION**: Establishes ibogaine's ASC profile as quantitatively more intense than high-dose psilocybin across all six dimensions. Provides benchmark data for future comparative psychometric research.
- [[2019/Brown2019_Ibogaine_Subjective_Experience_OUD|Brown & Noller 2019]] — **SCQ APPLICATION**: States of Consciousness Questionnaire (Griffiths et al. 2006) applied to ibogaine (n=42). 43% achieved complete mystical experience. Both this study and Heink 2017 found no ASC-outcome correlation — prompting the question of whether instruments designed for classical psychedelics capture ibogaine's therapeutic phenomenology.
- [[2025/Brown2025_Mystical_Experience_PTSD_Veterans|Brown R 2025]] — **MEQ30 APPLICATION**: Revised Mystical Experiences Questionnaire (n=30 veterans). Unlike 5d-ASC and SCQ, the MEQ30 captured a significant experience-outcome relationship for PTSD, with EEG correlates. Suggests the MEQ30's focus on mystical/spiritual dimensions may better index ibogaine's therapeutically relevant experiential features.

## Adverse Psychological Effects

Not all experiences are positive — psychiatric risks require documentation.

- [[2015/Marta2015_Ibogaine_Mania_Case_Reports|Ibogaine & Mania 2015]] — Three case reports of manic episodes following ibogaine
- [[2015/Breuer2015_Herbal_Seizures_Case_Report|Breuer 2015]] — "Herbal seizures" — atypical symptoms including seizure (see also [[RED_Cardiac_Safety_Hub]])
- [[2018/Knuijver2018_HPPD_Ibogaine_Case_Report|Knuijver 2018]] — **FIRST REPORTED IBOGAINE HPPD** (BLUE primary): A 31-year-old opioid-dependent male re-experienced oneirogenic imagery 48 hours post-dose — his mother abandoning him as a foundling, discovering a deceased friend in a bathtub after a heroin overdose he had supplied. The re-experiencing triggered intense rejection, shame, and guilt, persisting ~5 weeks and meeting DSM-5 HPPD criteria. A single EMDR session targeting the distressing imagery produced substantial relief — images faded over 14 days — but craving persisted unchanged and the patient relapsed to heroin, demonstrating a clean dissociation between the phenomenological disturbance and the anti-addictive mechanism. The emotional specificity of the HPPD content (abandonment, survivor guilt) suggests unresolved traumatic material surfacing during the oneirogenic phase can become substrate for persisting re-experiencing. From the same EUDRACT trial as [[2021/Knuijver2021_Safety_Opioid_Detox|Knuijver 2021]].

## Phenomenological Distinctions

| Feature | Ibogaine | Classical Psychedelics |
|---------|----------|----------------------|
| Primary quality | Oneiric (dream-like) | Perceptual/mystical |
| Duration | 24-36 hours | 4-8 hours |
| Visual content | Biographical, ancestors | Geometric, abstract |
| Body load | Heavy (ataxia, nausea) | Variable |
| Insight type | Life review, trauma | Ego dissolution |
