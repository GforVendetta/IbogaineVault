## Opioid Use Disorder (Primary Indication)

### Landmark Clinical Papers
- [Brown & Alper 2018](../2018/Brown2018_OUD_Detoxification_Outcomes.md) — **FIRST PROSPECTIVE STUDY**: 12-month follow-up (n=30). 50% opioid-free at 1 month (vs. 8.6–26% conventional detox). ASIC Drug Use improved at all time points. Methadone exposure predicted worse outcome (p=.002). Detailed test–flood–booster dosing protocol. Clare Wilkins acknowledged. MAPS-funded.
- [Mash 2018](../2018/Mash2018_Ibogaine_Detox_Opioid_Cocaine_Clinical_Observations_Tx_Outcomes.md) — **MAJOR**: Detoxification transitions opioid/cocaine abusers between dependence and abstinence. Large cohort from Healing Visions clinic.
- [Noller 2017](../2017/Noller2017_Ibogaine_Opioid_12Month_Outcomes.md) — **KEY NZ STUDY**: 12-month follow-up showing sustained reductions in opioid use (n=14)
- [Alper 1999](../1999/Alper1999_Acute_Opioid_Withdrawal.md) — Early clinical observations on acute withdrawal: N=33 opioid-dependent patients (1962–1993), 76% showed complete withdrawal resolution within 24h sustained at 72h. Also published as NYAS proceedings ([Alper 2000](../2000/Alper2000_Ibogaine_Acute_Opioid_Withdrawal.md)), which additionally discusses 18-MC as a potential congener with lower psychoactivity — connecting forward to Glick's synthetic ibogaine analogue programme.
- [Malcolm 2018](../2018/Malcolm2018_Opioid_Withdrawal_Craving_Ibogaine.md) — **LARGEST VALIDATED WITHDRAWAL DATA**: Retrospective chart review (n=50, Crossroads). COWS ↓ 8.2→3.3, SOWS ↓ 20.5→10.0, BSCS ↓ 6.6→1.9 at 48h post-ibogaine (all p<0.01). 78% showed no clinical withdrawal signs, 79% minimal cravings. Effect sizes η²=0.54–0.69. First study using all three standardised withdrawal/craving instruments simultaneously.
- [Davis 2017](../2017/Davis2017_Ibogaine_Opioid_Outcomes.md) — Retrospective survey of 88 opioid patients (Crossroads, Mexico 2012–2015). 80% reported withdrawal elimination or drastic reduction. 30% never used opioids again; 54% of abstainers sustained ≥1 year. Treatment responders showed lower depression/anxiety, higher well-being, and rated treatment as more spiritually meaningful.
- [Hittner & Quello 2004](../2004/Hittner2004_Ibogaine_Treatment_Recommendations_Model_Fitting.md) — **FIRST DOSE-RESPONSE ANALYSIS**: Reanalysis of Sheppard 1994 data (N=6) applying sigmoidal model fitting. Dose-to-weight ratio (DWR) is superior predictor of abstinence vs absolute dose (R²=.88). Identifies a critical threshold at ~16 mg/kg — below which minimal therapeutic effect, above which steep improvement curve. The mathematical confirmation of what [Sheppard 1994](../1994/Sheppard1994_Preliminary_Investigation_Ibogaine.md) observed clinically: inadequate dosing predicts failure. Also the earliest paper to argue that ibogaine treatment requires psychosocial scaffolding before and after the pharmacological intervention — a principle now universally accepted (see [Gomes 2025](../2025/Gomes2025_Ibogaine_Tool_Substance_Addiction.md) for the contemporary Brazilian model where integration psychotherapy is mandatory). First call for standardised outcome reporting, still partially unheeded two decades later (see [Swieczkowski 2025](../2025/Swieczkowski2025_Clinical_Trials_Landscape.md)).

### Case Reports
- [Sheppard 1994](../1994/Sheppard1994_Preliminary_Investigation_Ibogaine.md) — **FIRST PUBLISHED HUMAN CASE SERIES**: N=7 opiate-dependent individuals treated 1989–1990, Amsterdam (Hirez squatter community). Doses 700–1,800 mg (11.7–25 mg/kg). Universal withdrawal suppression within 24–38h psychoactive period. 3/7 remained drug-free ≥14 weeks at higher doses — the earliest dose-response signal. Established the trial-dose protocol (100–200 mg) that became permanent clinical practice. Collateral reductions in alcohol and tobacco noted spontaneously. Methodologically primitive (no controls, no urine screens), yet it planted three foundational observations: (1) ibogaine eliminates opiate withdrawal within 24–38 hours, (2) higher mg/kg doses correlate with better outcomes, and (3) effects extend beyond the primary drug of dependence. A landmark from the Amsterdam underground — ibogaine's extraordinary journey from squatter community to Nature Medicine begins here.
- [Luciano 2000](../2000/Luciano2000_Neurologic_EEG_Medical_Observations.md) — **FIRST HUMAN EEG DURING IBOGAINE**: Neurological and medical observations in **n=5** opioid-dependent subjects treated with **~25 mg/kg** oral ibogaine HCl (St. Kitts, with domperidone pretreatment). All EEG recordings were normal throughout — an important negative finding ruling out epileptiform activity at a clinically aggressive dose. No withdrawal symptoms at 24h in all five subjects; **2/5 symptom-free at 1 week**. Universal transient cerebellar ataxia (resolved by 24h) and movement-induced vomiting (3/5) document consistent acute side-effects that would later become expected clinical features. The protocol included domperidone antiemetic pretreatment and rectal re-administration for persistent vomiting — early innovations that anticipated modern protocol refinements. Continuous **12h EKG monitoring showed no abnormalities**, though this predates systematic QTc reporting — see [RED_Cardiac_Safety_Hub](RED_Cardiac_Safety_Hub.md) for contextualisation of the cardiac monitoring data.
- [Wilkins 2017](../2017/Wilkins2017_Methadone_Low_Dose.md) — Methadone detoxification using low, repeated, increasing doses
- [Wilson 2020](../2020/Wilson2020_Novel_Tx_OUD_Ibogaine_Iboga_Case_Study.md) — Two adult case reports
- [Lotsof 2001](../2001/Lotsof2001_Case_Studies_Patient_Management.md) — Foundational case studies (Alper & Lotsof)
- [Cloutier-Gill 2016](../2016/CloutierGill2016_Ibogaine_OUD_Case_Report.md) — Treatment-refractory OUD (19-year heroin history, multiple failed treatments including MMT) achieved 18-month sustained abstinence after 4-day ibogaine protocol (32 mg/kg total). Patient attributed success to transformative experience rather than withdrawal relief. Mild bradycardia observed (51–67 bpm). Published from St Paul's Hospital, Vancouver — notable for institutional-setting reporting.

### Polysubstance & Stimulant Dependence
- [Prior & Prior 2014](../2014/Prior2014_Ibogaine_Cocaine_Pilot_RCT.md) — ⚠️ **THE ONLY IBOGAINE RCT**: Double-blind, placebo-controlled pilot (N=20, cocaine dependence, Brazil). Single 1,800 mg ibogaine extract dose produced dramatic craving reduction (MCCS 7.4→1.3 at 72h, p<0.0001) sustained at 24 weeks (2.6, p<0.0001), with significantly fewer positive urine screens vs placebo (p=0.023) and 10% relapse rate. Zero attrition across the entire 6-month follow-up. ECGs every 6h for 72h: zero cardiovascular events. Published in a low-impact journal and limited by small sample, single-site design, and use of non-pharmaceutical-grade extract (75% purity) — yet it remains the sole placebo-controlled trial of ibogaine itself (as distinct from noribogaine; see [dos Santos 2017](../2017/DosSantos2017_Antiaddictive_Ibogaine_Systematic_Review.md) re: noribogaine RCT). The field's most conspicuous evidence gap is the absence of further RCTs in the decade since.
- [Schenberg 2014](../2014/Schenberg2014_Treating_Drug_Dependence_Retrospective_Study.md) — **LANDMARK NON-OPIOID DATA**: Brazilian retrospective (n=75), 72% polysubstance users (alcohol, cannabis, cocaine, crack). 61% abstinent at follow-up. Single treatment: median 5.5 months abstinence; multiple treatments: 8.4 months (p<0.001). Zero serious AEs or fatalities. First systematic evidence that ibogaine works beyond opioids — critical for the stimulant dependence gap where no pharmacotherapy exists.

### The Mission Within Model
- [Polanco & Black 2025](../2025/Polanco2025_War_Trauma_Ibogaine_5-MeO.md) — **LARGEST VETERAN COHORT**: 850+ veterans treated (2017–2023). Comprehensive book chapter documenting the ibogaine + 5-MeO-DMT combination treatment model. Three-phase integration framework (Pink Cloud → Between Worlds → Clarity Arising). See [GREEN_Clinical_Protocols_Hub](GREEN_Clinical_Protocols_Hub.md) for protocol details.
- [Staveley 2025](../2025/Staveley2025_PhD_Ibogaine_Veterans_PTSD.md) — **PhD THESIS: GNOSIS AS MECHANISM**: University of West Georgia doctoral study. N=12 special ops veterans, 10-36 months post-treatment. Constructivist grounded theory methodology. 100% reported immediate PTSD relief. Proposes "gnosis"—direct experiential knowledge via transpersonal encounter—as theoretical mechanism for sustained healing. Benefits span intrapersonal (emotional regulation, self-forgiveness), interpersonal (relationship repair), and transpersonal (spiritual awakening) domains.

### Nature Medicine Landmark
- [Cherian 2024](../2024/Cherian2024_Magnesium_Ibogaine_TBI.md) — **NATURE MEDICINE**: n=30 veterans with TBI. Massive effect sizes:
  - PTSD (PCL-5): Cohen's d = **3.16**
  - Depression: Cohen's d = **3.51**
  - Disability (WHODAS): Cohen's d = **3.23**
  - With magnesium co-administration: **ZERO QTc prolongation**
- [Olash 2026](../2026/Olash2026_Subjective_Experience_Magnesium_Ibogaine.md) — **PHENOMENOLOGY COMPANION** (see [PURPLE_Phenomenology_Hub](PURPLE_Phenomenology_Hub.md)): Same cohort's subjective experience. Four themes: dialogic trauma reappraisal, altered-self/mystical connection, emotional resolution, embodied brain healing. Frames ibogaine as "accelerated auto-psychotherapy." *Dedicated to Nolan Williams (d. 2025).*

### Cognitive Outcomes
- [Cherian 2023](../2023/Cherian2023_Ibogaine_Cognitive_Functioning.md) — **FIRST PROSPECTIVE COGNITIVE STUDY**: Same SOV cohort (n=30), ibogaine ~20 mg/kg. Significant improvements in processing speed (+10 standard score points, 107→117), cognitive inhibition, and verbal memory at 1-month follow-up. No negative cognitive consequences detected. Critical finding: ibogaine does not impair — and may enhance — neuropsychological functioning, countering assumptions about hallucinogen neurotoxicity.

### Mechanistic Follow-up
- [Williams 2025](../2025/Williams2025_Magnesium_Ibogaine_TBI.md) — Neural correlates: cortical oscillations and complexity changes documented via EEG
- [Geoly 2026](../2026/Geoly2026_Cortical_Thickness_Brain_Age_MISTIC.md) — **MRI MORPHOMETRY**: Same SOV cohort (n=25). MISTIC therapy associated with increased cortical thickness in 13 regions, subcortical volume expansion in 8 regions, and predicted brain age reduction of 1.3 years at 1 month. First structural neuroimaging evidence that ibogaine-assisted therapy produces measurable cortical changes — complementing Williams 2025's EEG findings with anatomical data. Published in *iScience*.
- [Calvey 2026](../2026/Calvey2026_Neurorestorative_Properties_Ibogaine.md) — **MECHANISTIC SYNTHESIS** (see [ORANGE_Mechanisms_Hub](ORANGE_Mechanisms_Hub.md)): Proposes remyelination as unifying mechanism across OUD, MS, and TBI. Maps ibogaine's receptor affinities to white matter repair pathways. Reframes the 7-day noribogaine persistence as a therapeutic window for myelin repair, not merely a PK curiosity.

### Sleep & PTSD
- [Faerman 2025](../2025/Faerman2025_MISTIC_12Month_Followup.md) — MISTIC 12-month follow-up: 84% PTSD remission sustained
- [Faerman 2023](../2023/Faerman2023_Ibogaine_Veterans_PTSD.md) — **Sleep mechanisms**: n=30 SOF veterans. Sleep improvements NOT predicted by PTSD improvement (only 23% variance), suggesting distinct mechanisms. First study of ibogaine effects on sleep.

## Persisting Effects & Psychological Outcomes

- [Davis 2018](../2018/Davis2018_Mixed_Method_Persisting_Effects.md) — Mixed-methods analysis of persisting effects post-ibogaine detox (n=73, Crossroads). 81% classified as treatment responders. Responders showed large persisting gains: meaning in life (d=1.27), tolerance of painful feelings (d=1.22), sacredness (d=1.18), authenticity (d=1.12), love/openheartedness (d=1.01). Integration difficulty was top challenge (23%). First quantitative evidence that ibogaine's lasting benefits extend well beyond withdrawal relief into enduring psychological and spiritual transformation — with implications for aftercare design. ## Adverse Events

Ibogaine's adverse event profile extends beyond cardiac risk. These cases establish psychiatric screening criteria now standard in clinical protocols.

- [Houenou et al. 2011](../2011/Houenou2011_Ibogaine_Psychosis_Schizophrenia.md) — **FIRST PSYCHOSIS CASE REPORT**: 26-year-old male with 5 years' involvement in Gabonese shamanism, escalating to daily iboga powder consumption. Hospitalised with persecutory/mystic delusions and visual + acoustico-verbal hallucinations. Resolved with olanzapine (10 mg/d) within 2 weeks. Two subsequent psychotic relapses *without* ibogaine confirmed DSM-IV schizophrenia — ibogaine acted as a precipitant in a neurobiologically vulnerable individual, analogous to how cannabis or psilocybin can trigger first-episode psychosis in those with prodromal symptoms (attenuated psychotic symptoms since age 22). Established psychotic spectrum disorders as a firm contraindication — a screening criterion now universally adopted in clinical protocols. See [GREEN_Clinical_Protocols_Hub](GREEN_Clinical_Protocols_Hub.md).
- [Knuijver 2018](../2018/Knuijver2018_HPPD_Ibogaine_Case_Report.md) — **FIRST REPORTED HPPD CASE**: 31-year-old male in phase II OUD trial (Netherlands) developed hallucinogen persisting perception disorder 48h after ibogaine HCl 10 mg/kg. Re-experiencing of oneirogenic imagery (abandonment, friend's overdose death) persisted ~5 weeks. One EMDR session produced substantial relief. QTc 420→516 ms (ΔQTc=96 ms, resolved in 24h). Patient relapsed to heroin. Critical for clinical practice: psychiatric monitoring needed alongside cardiac monitoring. Also cross-referenced in [RED_Cardiac_Safety_Hub](RED_Cardiac_Safety_Hub.md).

## Foundational Preclinical Evidence

Before human clinical data existed, the case for ibogaine's anti-addictive properties rested on animal models. These foundational studies demonstrated effects across multiple substances of abuse and — critically — provided the first evidence that ibogaine's active metabolite, not the parent compound, drives sustained therapeutic effects.

- [Cappendijk & Dzoljic 1993](../1993/Cappendijk1993_Ibogaine_Cocaine_Self_Administration.md) — **FIRST PRECLINICAL ADDICTION STUDY**: Single ibogaine 40 mg/kg IP reduced rat cocaine self-administration for >48h; weekly repeated dosing (3 weeks) produced cumulative, more sustained inhibition than consecutive-day dosing. 10 and 20 mg/kg were ineffective — a clear dose threshold. The critical observation: ibogaine's ~1h rodent half-life yet multi-day suppression of cocaine-seeking was the first experimental signal pointing toward a long-acting metabolite — what we now know as noribogaine. The finding that weekly-spaced doses outperformed consecutive-day dosing foreshadowed clinical observations about optimal booster spacing. Ibogaine HCl donated by Howard Lotsof. Predates all human clinical data on stimulants.
- [Rezvani et al. 1995](../1995/Rezvani1995_Ibogaine_Alcohol_Preferring_Rats.md) — **KEY ALCOHOL PRECLINICAL + METABOLITE INSIGHT**: Ibogaine dose-dependently reduced alcohol intake across three strains of alcohol-preferring rats (P, FH, AA); 60 mg/kg IP produced 51–65% reduction. The pharmacological revelation: subcutaneous administration (bypassing hepatic first-pass) was completely ineffective, while IP and oral routes succeeded — among the earliest experimental evidence that a liver-generated metabolite (noribogaine) is the active anti-addictive species. No tolerance with 5-day repeated dosing. No effect on food or water intake, demonstrating specificity for alcohol reward rather than general consumption suppression. Together with [Cappendijk 1993](../1993/Cappendijk1993_Ibogaine_Cocaine_Self_Administration.md), these two papers built the preclinical foundation that launched human investigation.

## Systematic Reviews & Meta-Analyses (GREY Subcategory)

### Comprehensive Reviews
- [Brown 2013](../2013/Brown2013_Ibogaine_Treatment_Substance_Dependence.md) — **PRE-PROSPECTIVE BENCHMARK**: T.K. Brown's comprehensive narrative review in *Current Drug Abuse Reviews*, synthesising the state of ibogaine science circa 2012. Covers history (1864→2013), preclinical evidence, human clinical data, and safety (19 fatalities to 2008). Notable for documenting the growth of the "ibogaine medical subculture" — underground treatment providers operating in parallel with (and often ahead of) formal research. Discusses 18-MC and noribogaine as alternative compounds. The same author would later conduct the first prospective study ([Brown & Alper 2018](../2018/Brown2018_OUD_Detoxification_Outcomes.md)), making this review a useful benchmark for how the evidence base expanded over a five-year period. Secondary categories: RED (cardiac safety data), WHITE (historical), ORANGE (mechanisms).
- [Underwood & Bright 2021](../2021/Underwood2021_Narrative_Review_Ibogaine_Literature.md) — Narrative review of clinical evidence
- [Köck 2022](../2022/Kock2022_Systemic_Review_Clinical_Trials_Therapeutic_Applications_Ibogaine.md) — Systematic review of clinical trials
- [Canessa 2020](../2020/Canessa2020_Ibogaine_Noribogaine_in_Tx_Review_Safety_Efficacy.md) — MSc dissertation systematic review (2000–2020): ibogaine/noribogaine safety and efficacy. Confirms anti-addictive properties with need for medical supervision.
- [Mosca 2023](../2023/Mosca2023_Ibogaine_Noribogaine_SUDs_Systematic_Review.md) — **MAJOR**: Systematic review of 31 studies with meta-analysis. Demonstrates efficacy for SUD but documents significant cardiotoxicity—most deaths in uncontrolled settings.
- [dos Santos 2017](../2017/DosSantos2017_Antiaddictive_Ibogaine_Systematic_Review.md) — **PRISMA SYSTEMATIC REVIEW**: 8 human studies identified (7 open-label case series + 1 noribogaine RCT). Consistent finding: single ibogaine dose reduces opioid withdrawal within 24h, effects lasting weeks to months. Doses ≥17 mg/kg associated with stronger outcomes. Noribogaine (n=27 RCT) showed no significant effect — raising the question of whether the psychoactive experience itself is necessary for efficacy.
- [Oña 2024](../2024/Ona2024_PhD_Thesis_Opioid_Dependence.md) — **PhD THESIS (ICEERS/Universitat Rovira i Virgili)**: Four-part investigation — (1) systematic AE review confirming QTc risk and need for medical supervision; (2) Phase II RCT (n=20) of low-dose ibogaine (100 mg) during methadone detox: 17/20 patients halved methadone dose in 7 days without withdrawal; (3) metabolomic analysis suggesting ibogaine reverses chronic opioid effects on energy metabolism; (4) preclinical transcriptomics revealing oxytocin/vasopressin pathway upregulation and sex-specific gene expression changes. First controlled evidence that even sub-psychoactive doses may have clinically meaningful anti-withdrawal effects.
- [Gomes 2025](../2025/Gomes2025_Ibogaine_Tool_Substance_Addiction.md) — **BRAZILIAN CLINICAL EXPERIENCE**: Book chapter synthesising >30,000 estimated treatments since 2007 with the scientific literature. Describes 3-phase experience model (acute visionary → evaluative → residual stimulation), 30-day cocaine washout protocol (due to persistent QTc effects), and integration psychotherapy as essential — not optional. Authors' own protocol: ≥4 pre-treatment + ≥4 post-treatment psychotherapy sessions with hospital-based administration. See also [GREEN_Clinical_Protocols_Hub](GREEN_Clinical_Protocols_Hub.md).

## Clinical Trials Landscape

- [Swieczkowski 2025](../2025/Swieczkowski2025_Clinical_Trials_Landscape.md) — **GLOBAL TRIAL REGISTRY ANALYSIS**: Cross-sectional analysis of 9 ibogaine clinical trials extracted from ClinicalTrials.gov, EU registries, and WHO ICTRP. Documents considerable methodological heterogeneity: fixed-dose vs ascending-dose designs, varying inclusion/exclusion criteria, differing primary endpoints. Early-phase trials dominate; no large-scale Phase III exists. Calls for a standardised clinical framework drawing on lessons from classical psychedelic and MDMA trial design. Essential context for understanding where the field stands and what is needed to advance ibogaine toward regulatory approval.

> ⚠️ **Cross-indication evidence fragmentation:** Swieczkowski's SUD-only inclusion criteria correctly excluded the MISTIC TBI trial ([Cherian 2024](../2024/Cherian2024_Magnesium_Ibogaine_TBI.md), NCT04313712) — which represents the most rigorously monitored ibogaine administration to date, with magnesium co-administration and comprehensive cardiac screening. This means the most thorough clinical trial registry analysis available is structurally blind to the highest-quality safety data in the field. Ibogaine's pharmacology does not change by indication: cardiac risk, CYP2D6 variability, and dosing constraints apply identically whether treating OUD or TBI. Vault users querying clinical trial evidence should cross-reference the [Veterans, TBI & PTSD (Breakthrough Population)](#veterans-tbi-ptsd-breakthrough-population) section above and the [RED_Cardiac_Safety_Hub](RED_Cardiac_Safety_Hub.md) to close this gap. The separation of ibogaine evidence by therapeutic indication risks fragmenting safety signals that should be synthesised across all clinical contexts.

## How We Got Here: The Veterans/TBI Breakthrough

**From underground treatment to Nature Medicine (2020→2025)**

Military veterans with treatment-resistant PTSD and traumatic brain injury became the breakthrough population that brought ibogaine mainstream scientific credibility.

### Early Observations (2020)
[Davis 2020](../2020/Davis2020_SpecialOps_Veterans_Trauma.md) — First systematic data on SOF veterans receiving ibogaine + 5-MeO-DMT combination. Signals massive effects on trauma symptoms. The patient population with "nothing left to lose" becomes the proof-of-concept group.

### The MISTIC Protocol (2023–2024)
- [Davis 2023](../2023/Davis2023_Ibogaine_5MeO-DMT_for_SEALS.md) — Open-label study, prospective data. Documents the combination protocol.
- [Cherian 2024](../2024/Cherian2024_Magnesium_Ibogaine_TBI.md) — **NATURE MEDICINE LANDMARK**: n=30, massive effect sizes (PTSD d=3.16, depression d=3.51). With Mg co-admin: ZERO cardiac events.

### Neural Mechanisms (2025–2026)
- [Williams 2025](../2025/Williams2025_Magnesium_Ibogaine_TBI.md) — Cortical oscillations and neural complexity changes documented via EEG. Mechanism validation.
- [Calvey 2026](../2026/Calvey2026_Neurorestorative_Properties_Ibogaine.md) — Integrative review proposing remyelination as the unifying mechanism. Provides the "why" for TBI, OUD, and emerging MS clinical data.

*Arc*: Underground veterans → Observational data → Nature Medicine validation → Mechanistic understanding → Remyelination synthesis

## Limitations & Gaps

**What we don't have yet:**
- Only one RCT exists ([Prior & Prior 2014](../2014/Prior2014_Ibogaine_Cocaine_Pilot_RCT.md), cocaine, N=20, pilot); no RCTs for opioid dependence or against active comparators
- Large-scale (n>100) prospective studies
- Long-term (>1 year) outcomes data
- Dose-response curves from controlled trials (though [Hittner 2004](../2004/Hittner2004_Ibogaine_Treatment_Recommendations_Model_Fitting.md) provides a retrospective sigmoidal model with ~16 mg/kg threshold)
- Head-to-head comparison with existing OUD treatments (buprenorphine, methadone)

**Why this matters:** Current evidence is overwhelmingly observational, open-label, or retrospective. The sole RCT (Prior 2014) is limited to cocaine dependence, a single site, and a low-impact journal — yet its dramatic effect sizes (craving MCCS 7.4→1.3, p<0.0001) and zero attrition across 6 months are striking. The field's most conspicuous evidence gap is the absence of further RCTs in the decade since. See [Swieczkowski 2025](../2025/Swieczkowski2025_Clinical_Trials_Landscape.md) for a mapping of the current trial landscape and what is needed.
