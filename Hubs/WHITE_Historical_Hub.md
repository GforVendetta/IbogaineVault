## Bwiti & Traditional Context

Iboga has been used in Gabon and surrounding regions for centuries in Bwiti spiritual practice.

- [[2001/Fernandez2001_Returning_to_the_Path_Bwiti_Ritual_Context|Fernandez & Fernandez 2001]] — **FOUNDATIONAL ETHNOGRAPHY**: The Fernandezes revisit their 1950s fieldwork among Fang Bwiti to compare African iboga initiation with Western therapeutic settings (INTASH). Introduces "time and space binding" framework — Bwiti iboga use restores ancestral connection, communal place, and generational continuity fractured by colonialism. Includes Bwiti dosing data (Table I, with Lotsof calculations) and argues the collectivist aftercare model (lifelong weekly ritual with mild pick-up doses) outperforms Western individualistic approaches. From the same Alkaloids Vol. 56 as [[2001/Alper2001_Ibogaine_Review|Alper 2001]].
- [[2025/Samorini2025_Studies_Iboga_Cults_Ethnobotanical_Complex|Samorini 2025]] — Studies on iboga cults: ethnobotanical scholarship
- [[2021/Bekale2021_Holy_Spirit_Iboga_Bwiti|Bekale 2021]] — Contemporary Bwiti: Holy Spirit and iboga
- [[2018/Rodger2018_Healing_Potential_Ibogaine|Rodger 2018]] — Phenomenological analysis integrating Bwiti context (see [[PURPLE_Phenomenology_Hub]])
- [[2006/Maas2006_Cardiac_Fatalities_Autonomic_Dysfunction|Maas & Strubelt 2006]] — **BWITI AS CARDIOPROTECTION**: Analyses Gabonese initiation practices through the lens of autonomic neuroscience. Days-long isolation, right-hemispheric trance induction, and theta-rhythm music map onto specific arrhythmia prevention mechanisms. Draws on experience of 100,000+ Bwiti initiations. The only paper in the vault that directly bridges traditional healing knowledge and Western cardiac physiology. (Primary category: RED — see [[RED_Cardiac_Safety_Hub]])

- [[Industry_Documents/ICEERS2020_Future_Iboga_Gabon|ICEERS 2020]] — **BWITI VOICES ON SUSTAINABILITY**: Phase II qualitative field study (n=56 interviews, Gabon 2019) documenting Bwiti community perspectives on iboga sustainability, spiritual healing mechanisms, cultivation, and Nagoya Protocol compliance. Seven community aspirations for 2030 including sacred forest regeneration, ethical markets, and global Bwiti recognition. Documents sustainability crisis (illegal Cameroon trafficking, 850% markup, logging encroachment), plantation models (A2E community 4,300 plants, Bibasse private 20,000 plants), toxic false siblings (*Rauvolfia mombasiana* confirmed fatality), and traditional ontology where iboga "cannot kill." Essential counterpoint to Western extraction narratives — these are the knowledge-holders speaking for themselves.

**Critical consideration:** Western ibogaine use exists in tension with traditional Bwiti practice. Questions of cultural appropriation, sustainability of *Tabernanthe iboga*, and respect for indigenous knowledge are ongoing.

## The Lotsof Era (1962-2010)

### Addict Self-Help Movement
- [[2001/Frenken2001_RootsUp_INTASH_SelfHelp|Frenken 2001]] — **GRASSROOTS PHARMACEUTICAL DEVELOPMENT**: Chapter from Alkaloids Vol. 56 documenting the Dutch addict self-help movement (INTASH/ICASH/DASH). Eight opiate-dependent subjects treated with 20mg/kg ibogaine HCl; 7/8 achieved 6-month abstinence with complete acute withdrawal resolution. Peer-driven "pro-choice" harm reduction model founded by Geerte Frenken, Nico Adriaans, and Josien Harms following Lotsof/Sisko's 1990 introduction to the Dutch community. Companion to [[2001/Alper2001_Contemporary_History_Ibogaine_USA_Europe|Alper 2001]] and [[2001/Lotsof2001_Case_Studies_Patient_Management|Lotsof 2001]] in the same volume.

## Historical Reviews

### Comprehensive Histories
- [[1995/Popik1995_100Years_Ibogaine_Review|Popik 1995]] — **"100 Years of Ibogaine"**: Comprehensive historical review from first isolation (1901) to 1995
- [[2011/Rodger2011_SocialContext_Subcultural_DrugPolicy|Rodger 2011]] — **SUBCULTURAL IDENTITY AS DRUG POLICY**: Medical anthropologist/psychiatrist conceptual analysis. Central thesis: ibogaine's appeal lies in social meanings drawn from West African origins and subcultural identity formation inverting dominant culture values (community over individualism, natural over industrial, spiritual over secular). By 2006: 3,414 treatments documented, 12 deaths since 1990. Draws on Fernandez 1982 Bwiti ethnography, positions ibogaine advocacy as resistance to medicalisation parallel to Bwiti resistance to colonialism. Essential for understanding *why* ibogaine generates such fierce loyalty — it's not just pharmacology, it's identity.
- [[1998/Wittier1998_Ibogaine_GeneralReview|Witter 1998]] — **THE TIMES MAGAZINE PANORAMA**: Long-form journalism capturing the ibogaine landscape at a pivotal moment — Lotsof's Panama programme ($8,000–$20,000), Mash's fledgling St. Kitts clinic ($10,000), Taub's Costa Rica/Italy operations ($2,200), and perhaps a dozen underground outfits. Published when only ~300 people had been treated, it preserves the raw texture of the era: first-person experience reports ranging from the ecstatic to the terrifying, Lotsof insisting ibogaine "opens up the soul of the most repressed person," and Ken Alper's threshold-of-belief moment watching a patient walk off 70mg/day methadone in Panama. Captures the paradox that would define the next two decades — a treatment available mainly to "the privileged rich, in many cases paying through the nose to be research guinea pigs."
- [[2001/Alper2001_Contemporary_History_Ibogaine_USA_Europe|Alper, Beal & Kaplan 2001]] — **THE DEFINITIVE CONTEMPORARY HISTORY**: The single most important document in the vault for understanding how ibogaine went from one man's 1962 self-experiment to a global medical subculture. Alper, Dana Beal, and Charles Kaplan map every treatment scene — Lotsof's NDA International, Mash's St. Kitts programme, Eric Taub's initiatory sessions, the Dutch ICASH/DASH/INTASH addict self-help network, Bastiaans's hallucinogen psychotherapy legacy, the Slovenian and Czech scenes, the disputed Christiana commune claims, and the UK's late-1990s emergence. Equally important is the political history: ACT UP's "Storm NIDA" action (July 1991) that catalysed the NIDA ibogaine project, Cures-not-Wars activism, and the Junkiebond's European harm reduction lineage. From the same Alkaloids Vol. 56 as [[2001/Fernandez2001_Returning_to_the_Path_Bwiti_Ritual_Context|Fernandez & Fernandez 2001]] and [[2001/Alper2001_Ibogaine_Review|Alper 2001 (Review)]].
- [[2023/Mash2023_Ibogaine_Legacy_Psychedelic_Renaissance|Mash 2023]] — Deborah Mash on ibogaine's legacy within the psychedelic renaissance
- [[2023/Fernandes-Nascimento2023_Scientometric_Analysis_Three_Decades_of_Ibogaine_Research|Yuan-Pang 2023]] — Scientometric analysis of three decades of ibogaine research

### Foundational Texts
- [[Other/DeRienzo1995_Ibogaine_Story_Ch1|De Rienzo & Beal 1997: *The Ibogaine Story*]] — **FOUNDATIONAL MOVEMENT HISTORY**: The definitive insider account of ibogaine's journey from Howard Lotsof's 1962 self-experiment to the mid-1990s NIDA review process, published by Autonomedia. Written by journalist Paul De Rienzo and activist Dana Beal, this book-length oral history captures the political, scientific, and subcultural forces that shaped the ibogaine movement — from the heroin infiltration of NYC's counterculture ([[Other/DeRienzo1995_Ibogaine_Story_Ch1|Ch1: The War With the Junkies]]) through the key scientific personalities (Glick's first dose-response data in [[Other/DeRienzo1995_Ibogaine_Story_Ch7|Ch7]], Molliver's primate neurotoxicity controversy in [[Other/DeRienzo1995_Ibogaine_Story_Ch15|Ch15]]) to the political forces that ultimately blocked the Phase I/II trial ([[Other/DeRienzo1995_Ibogaine_Story_Ch18|Ch18: Deborah Mash's Brain]]). Essential primary source for understanding the activist-driven origins of ibogaine research and the NIDA/ACT UP dynamics that no subsequent academic review fully captures. 18 chapters in `Other/`, covering Bwiti context ([[Other/DeRienzo1995_Ibogaine_Story_Ch13|Ch13]]), the Dutch addict self-help scene ([[Other/DeRienzo1995_Ibogaine_Story_Ch8|Ch8]]), and the Staten Island Project ([[Other/DeRienzo1995_Ibogaine_Story_Ch5|Ch5]]). Read alongside [[2001/Alper2001_Contemporary_History_Ibogaine_USA_Europe|Alper, Beal & Kaplan 2001]] for the scholarly counterpart to this journalistic account.

### Reference Resources
- *ICEERS 2012 Literature Compilation* — Bibliography (not yet in vault)
- [[2012/Sisko2012_Vanishing_World_Iboga_Sustainability|Sisko 2012]] — Vancouver Conference: T. iboga endangerment, sustainable sourcing
- [[2009/Paskulin2009_Translated_French_Iboga_Between_Myth_Reality|Paškulin 2009]] — **FRANCOPHONE BRIDGE**: A Slovenian researcher's French-language synthesis, published in *Phytothérapie*, that performs double duty — reviewing iboga's traditional uses (convalescence, anti-fatigue, spiritual ritual) alongside original proteomics data showing ibogaine upregulates glycolysis and Krebs cycle enzymes in rat brain at 72 hours post-dose. The energy metabolism finding offers a mechanistic explanation for ibogaine's prolonged effects that outlast its pharmacokinetic presence. Bridges Francophone ethnobotanical scholarship into the English-language literature and situates iboga within the broader phytotherapy tradition rather than purely as an addiction treatment.

### Milestone Journalism
- [[Primary_Sources/1996_WBAI_Mash_FDA_Trials_Ibogaine|Mash & DeRienzo 1996]] — **"UNCOVERING IBOGAINE"**: Pacifica Radio broadcast on WBAI-99.5-FM, New York (July 1996). Paul DeRienzo interviews Deborah Mash at the critical inflection point of the only FDA-approved ibogaine trial in US history — a Phase I dose-escalation study she was leading at the University of Miami. Captures contemporaneous accounts of the noribogaine discovery ("a magical structure"), her three-line rebuttal of the Johns Hopkins Purkinje cell neurotoxicity findings, the "chemical bar mitzvah" psychological framework, and the funding drought that was slow-tracking the trial "to the point of stopping." Predates the 2018 Oliver documentary by 22 years — this is Mash in the arena, not in retrospect. Also documents two early ibogaine-associated deaths (Baumann patient, Netherlands case) and her prescient warning that unsupervised adverse events could "close the door on research permanently."
- [[2005/Vastag2005_Ibogaine_Vast_Uncontrolled_Experiment|Vastag 2005]] — **"A VAST, UNCONTROLLED EXPERIMENT"**: Published in *Science* — the title itself, quoting NIDA's Frank Vocci, became shorthand for the state of ibogaine treatment. By 2005, Alper estimated >5,000 people treated, Wachtel counted 30–40 clinics worldwide, and the field was at a crossroads: NIDA had spent millions on preclinical work then walked away, Mash's FDA-approved Phase I trial was finally launching in Miami (funded by a $250,000 anonymous donation), and an Israeli efficacy study was in planning. Captures the persistent tension between growing anecdotal evidence and the regulatory barriers preventing controlled trials — a tension that would define ibogaine research for the next two decades. Also documents early GDNF mechanism findings (Dorit Ron, UCSF) and Glick's 18-MC development.

## Legal & Policy

### Global Medical Subculture & Legal Geography
- [[2008/Alper2008_Ibogaine_Medical_Subculture|Alper, Lotsof & Kaplan 2008]] — **FOUNDATIONAL CENSUS**: Quantitative ethnography of the global ibogaine medical subculture. Census of 3,414 individuals treated outside Africa by Feb 2006 (~30% annual growth from ~857 in 2001). Four scene typology: medical model (n=824), lay provider/guide (n=1,213), activist/self-help (n=200), religious/ceremonial (n=1,177). 68% for substance disorders, 53% specifically opioid withdrawal. Documents 11 fatalities (1990–2006) and emergence of screening protocols (EKG, liver function). The quantitative backbone for understanding how the subculture evolved before formal regulation.
- [[2014/Kingsley-Brown2014_Ibo_Tx_for_Substance_Dependence|Kingsley-Brown 2014]] — **COMPREHENSIVE OVERVIEW**: Global ibogaine medical subculture development, legal geography (Mexico/Canada/NZ vs US Schedule I), *T. iboga* sustainability concerns, Baja California long-term outcomes research. Conference paper documenting the state of ibogaine treatment circa 2014.

### Expert Testimony & Policy Advocacy
- [[2023/Alper2023_Expert_Testimony_KY|Alper 2023 — Kentucky Opioid Abatement Commission]] — **LANDMARK POLICY DOCUMENT**: Kenneth Alper's expert testimony advocating for opioid settlement funds to support ibogaine research. Summarises three decades of ibogaine science: medical ethnography, forensic toxicology (19 autopsies), and novel mechanism evidence. Key quote: "Ibogaine may provide a valid, and potentially transformative objective."

### 2025 US Policy Developments
- [[2025/Yockey2025_Reconsidering_Ibogaine|Yockey 2025]] — **POLICY REVIEW**: Synthesises US state-level policy developments. Documents Texas $50M public-private partnership for FDA-regulated ibogaine trials, Colorado Natural Medicine Health Act, and legislative initiatives in Arizona, New York, Washington, and Kentucky.
- [[Industry_Documents/LAPPA2025_Ibogaine_Legislative_Analysis|LAPPA 2025]] — **LEGISLATIVE LANDSCAPE**: Policy brief from the Legislative Analysis and Public Policy Association — an ONDCP-funded 501(c)(3) nonprofit. The most comprehensive single-source survey of US ibogaine scheduling in the vault: Schedule I in 46 states, with four exceptions (Alaska Schedule II, Maine Schedule X, Massachusetts Class C, Vermont regulated drug rule). Documents seven states introducing ibogaine access or research bills in 2025 (AZ, CT, KY, MN, NJ, OK, TX), Colorado's Natural Medicine Code framework including state-licensed healing centres, and DC's lowest-priority enforcement model. That an ONDCP-funded organisation is producing balanced policy analysis on ibogaine access is itself a significant signal — analogous to the trajectory psilocybin followed from 2018–2023. Complements [[2025/Yockey2025_Reconsidering_Ibogaine|Yockey 2025]] with granular legislative detail.

### Regulatory Analysis
- *Marks 2018 Psychedelic Medicine Legal Obstacles* — 72-page law review article (not yet in vault)
- [[2011/Donnelly2011_Ibogaine_Drug_Alcohol_Addiction_Legal|Donnelly 2011]] — **LEGAL CASE FOR RESCHEDULING**: A *Journal of Legal Medicine* commentary arguing ibogaine should move from Schedule I to Schedule II. Marshals the economic case — $181 billion/year in US drug abuse costs, $185 billion in alcohol abuse costs — against the evidence base: 83% withdrawal-free outcomes at Mash's Caribbean clinic, Lotsof/Frenken's 33-patient series, and the UK's 33% two-year sobriety rate (favourable against 70–90% first-year relapse in conventional treatment). Catalogues nine ibogaine-associated deaths (pre-2011) and contextualises them against nearly 10,000 methadone deaths over two years. Proposes three pathways: Schedule II reclassification, taxpayer-funded clinical studies, and state-level decriminalisation following the medical marijuana model. Written by a law student, not a clinician — but captures the legal reasoning that would eventually underpin the state-level policy movements documented in [[2025/Yockey2025_Reconsidering_Ibogaine|Yockey 2025]].

### Policy Reports

### 2026 Policy Critiques
- [[2026/Terasaki2026_Ibogaine_OUD_Unrecognised_Risk|Terasaki 2026]] — **MOUD CRITIQUE**: *Journal of Addiction Medicine* commentary warning that ibogaine promotion opposing MOUD may increase overdose risk. Survey data: 41% of OUD-diagnosed respondents had tried ibogaine. Includes fatal case report of patient who favoured ibogaine over buprenorphine, maintained subtherapeutic doses, died during taper. Critiques MAPS/advocacy framing ibogaine as *alternative to* rather than *complement to* evidence-based MOUD (which shows 53% mortality reduction). A vital counterweight to the field's enthusiasm — the question isn't whether ibogaine works, but whether positioning it against MOUD costs lives in the interim.

### Foundational Memoir
- [[Primary_Sources/Wachtel2025_Bastiaans_Ibogaine_Historical_Account|Wachtel 2025]] — **FIRSTHAND MEMOIR**: Boaz Wachtel (Ibogaine Group member: Lotsof, Sisko, Wachtel) documents the formative years 1988–1993 in the Netherlands. Jan Bastiaans — retired Leiden psychiatry chair, LSD/psilocybin pioneer for WWII trauma — observed/sponsored 18 of the first 40–45 Dutch treatments. Bastiaans' crucial insight: significant trauma underlies most addiction — anticipating Maté (2012) by two decades, validated by Williams et al. (2024) Nature Medicine MISTIC study. Documents the Nicola fatality (1993): 25-year-old German woman, concealed heroin use during treatment, opioid-ibogaine potentiation — the case that ended Bastiaans' licence and catalysed the screening/monitoring protocols now codified in [[Clinical_Guidelines/Lotsof2003_Ibogaine_Therapy_Manual|Lotsof/Wachtel 2003 Manual]]. Essential reading alongside [[2001/Alper2001_Contemporary_History_Ibogaine_USA_Europe|Alper 2001]].

### Clare Wilkins Conference Talks (2010–2021)
Chronological evolution of Clare Wilkins' clinical approach — from peer-driven model through cumulative dosing to holotropic framework. These six talks constitute the most comprehensive primary source record of how a single practitioner's protocol evolved over a decade of practice with 800+ sessions.

- [[Primary_Sources/2010_MAPS_Clare_Wilkins_Evolution|Wilkins 2010 (MAPS)]] — **PEER-DRIVEN MODEL**: Importance of ibogaine-experienced staff; stabilisation before dosing; death analysis driving safety improvements (hep locks, pulmonary embolism prevention); integration of meditation, nutrition, bodywork. Howard Lotsof mentorship: *"Watch for the QT intervals."* Rejects disease model: *"I was perfect… something touched something in me that I knew was inside the whole time."*
- [[Primary_Sources/2010_Horizons_Clare_Wilkins_400_Sessions|Wilkins 2010 (Horizons)]] — **400+ SESSION OUTCOMES**: 92% leave detoxed, 18% maintain 6mo–4yr abstinence, 42% relapse within 3 months. Engagement over abstinence philosophy. Family systems approach: *"You can't treat just an arm on a body."* MAPS longitudinal study launch. GDNF mechanism discussion. Nutrition as "profound root nutrition."
- [[Primary_Sources/2014_11_MAPS_Clare_Wilkins_Architecture_of_Emergency|Wilkins 2014 (MAPS)]] — **FLOOD → INCREMENTAL DOSING**: Evolution from single large dose to cumulative protocol; total alkaloid safer than HCl (modulating factors like coca vs cocaine); family-centred treatment; "demythologising ibogaine" — not 10–20 years therapy in one night. Anthropocene framing: mass extinction era parallels emergency approach to treatment.
- [[Primary_Sources/2017_EntheoScience_Clare_Wilkins_Cannabis_Ethics|Wilkins 2017 (EntheoScience)]] — **CANNABIS ADJUNCT & MOVEMENT ETHICS**: Cannabis oil replacing benzodiazepines for sleep/pain; 40% cartel profit reduction from cannabis legalisation linked to fentanyl crisis; GITA alliance essential; rat park communities forming around treatment centres; *"it's us that is against us"* — competition/division vs collaboration.
- [[Primary_Sources/2020_MYEBOGA_Clare_Wilkins_Interview|Wilkins 2020 (MyEboga)]] — **800+ TREATMENTS & PROTOCOL REFINEMENT**: Cumulative dosing protocol developed after fatality analysis (*"If this person had received 300mg rather than 1.2g, would he have died? They all said no"*); orthomolecular medicine integration (IV vitamin C clinically proven to reduce opioid withdrawal); GITA restructuring; Parkinson's case studies emerging; community fragmentation; diversity issues in predominantly white movement.
- [[Primary_Sources/2021_Microdose_Clare_Wilkins_Holotropic_Perspective|Wilkins 2021 (Microdose)]] — **HOLOTROPIC FRAMEWORK**: Lotsof's advice: *"Watch QT intervals and don't trust the media"*; community trauma and division post-Howard's death; three 15-year life phases (trauma, using, ibogaine work — all traumatic); holotropic vs holistic framing (Stan Grof: "towards wholeness"); drug user rights (Carl Hart perspective); ibogaine community *"more traumatised than all other psychedelic communities as a whole."*

### Legal Documents & Patent Disputes

Primary source legal filings documenting the institutional fractures that shaped ibogaine's development trajectory.

- Mash v. NDA International (1997) — **PRIMARY SOURCE: THE MASH–LOTSOF RUPTURE** *(primary source, not publicly distributed)*. Amended Complaint filed 28 April 1997, US District Court, Southern District of Florida. Deborah C. Mash vs NDA International/Howard S. Lotsof — four counts: patent cancellation, breach of contract, rescission, fraud. At the centre: noribogaine. Mash discovered noribogaine's pharmacological significance during NDA-funded research at the University of Miami (1992 agreement), filed a patent application in July 1994, but NDA never prosecuted it. Lotsof then filed his own "Noribogaine Plus" patent (October 1994, issued January 1997) naming himself sole inventor. This filing documents the precise moment the two lineages of ibogaine development diverged: the academic track (Mash → University of Miami IND → Phase I → St. Kitts → DemeRx → noribogaine clinical development) and the activist track (Lotsof → NDA patents → advocacy → the underground treatment network that [[2008/Alper2008_Ibogaine_Medical_Subculture|Alper 2008]] would later map as a "medical subculture"). The complaint was posted to the ibogaine mailing list (puzzlepiece.org) on 11 January 1998 — itself documenting the radical transparency and community governance culture of that era. See [[2001/Alper2001_Contemporary_History_Ibogaine_USA_Europe|Alper 2001]] for the broader historical context, [[1995/Mash1995_Noribogaine_SERT_Identification|Mash 1995]] for the noribogaine discovery paper at the centre of the dispute, and [[2008/Alper2008_Ibogaine_Medical_Subculture|Alper 2008]] for the "medical subculture" that grew from the Lotsof lineage.

## How We Got Here: The Historical/Cultural Thread

**Understanding iboga's origins and Lotsof's legacy (1969→2025)**

Ibogaine didn't emerge from pharma—it came from Bwiti tradition and one man's self-experiment.

### Pre-history
[[1969/Naranjo1969_Psychotherapeutic_Possibilities_Fantasy-Enhancing_Drugs|Naranjo 1969]] — Claudio Naranjo's early work on "oneirophrenics." Documents ibogaine phenomenology before Lotsof—the first Western scientific engagement with the psychoactive properties.

[[2001/Fernandez2001_Returning_to_the_Path_Bwiti_Ritual_Context|Fernandez & Fernandez 2001]] — Retrospective reflection on ethnographic fieldwork conducted among Fang Bwiti in the late 1950s — the deepest historical anchor for iboga use in the vault. Published in the same Alkaloids Vol. 56 as Alper and Lotsof's chapters, bridging the traditional context directly into the Western therapeutic conversation. Their "time and space binding" framework remains the most compelling anthropological account of why iboga works in its original cultural setting.
