## CLINICAL SUMMARY & KEY FINDINGS

### **1. ABSOLUTE EXCLUSION CRITERIA**

**Psychiatric:**
- Schizophrenia
- Bipolar disorder (hospitalized/medicated)
- Depersonalization/Derealization Disorder
- Cerebellar dysfunction
- Epilepsy (rule out benzo/alcohol withdrawal misdiagnosis)
- Active psychosis/confusional state
- Organic brain disease, Dementia

**Cardiac:**
- **QTc >450ms (males) or >470ms (females)**
- Heart failure, enlarged/hypertrophic heart
- Active blood clots (pulmonary embolism, DVT)

**Respiratory:**
- Emphysema, COPD, Cystic Fibrosis (with low O₂, steroid requirements)

**Gastrointestinal:**
- Bleeding ulcer
- Leaky gut syndrome

**Other:**
- **Abnormal electrolytes** (K, Mg outside normal ranges - must correct)
- **Liver enzymes >2.5x normal**
- Dialysis, abnormal BUN/creatinine
- Active infection/abscess
- <6 months post-major surgery
- Pregnancy

---

### **2. CARDIAC RISK FACTORS (Require Cardiologist Review)**

**Borderline QTc:** 430-450ms (M), 450-470ms (F) - monitor closely, check electrolytes

**Heart Conditions Requiring Assessment:**
- Arrhythmias, AV blocks, ventricular arrhythmias
- Atrial fibrillation (clot risk)
- Childhood congenital defects
- Myocardial infarction history
- Murmur (echocardiogram to rule out valve disease)
- Pericarditis/Endocarditis history (echocardiogram required)
- Family history sudden cardiac death <50 years
- Pacemaker (internal/external)
- Previous stroke/TIA (require clearance)
- Resolved blood clots (PE/DVT)

**Vital Signs:**
- **HR ≥120 or ≤50 bpm** (resting)
- **BP ≥170/105 or ≤90/60** (off medication)

**Other Risk Factors:**
- Hypertension, hypotension, diabetes
- Nicotine use, high cholesterol
- Peripheral vascular disease
- Chest pain/SOB (with/without exertion)
- Unexplained fainting

---

### **3. CRITICAL PHARMACOLOGY**

**Cardiac Mechanisms:**
- **QT prolongation** from hERG channel blockade (IC50 = 4µM)
- **Bradycardia** (typical response)
- **Hypotension** (typical response)
- **T-wave morphology changes:** flattening, biphasic, anterior slope decrease
- **Duration:** T-wave changes 12-24 hours; adverse events reported up to 76h post-dose

**Repolarization Reserve Depletion:**
- Potassium dysregulation (intracellular exchange affected by hERG blockade)
- Low magnesium
- Other QT-prolonging substances
- Cocaine/alcohol/amphetamine withdrawal
- Bradycardia itself

**Metabolism:**
- **Ibogaine:** Peaks 2h, half-life up to 7h
- **CYP2D6 → Noribogaine** (stored in fat, released over weeks/months)
- **CYP2D6 phenotype critical:** Poor/ultra-rapid metabolizers need dosing adjustments

**Neurotransmitter Effects:**
- Acts on: glutamate, opioid, dopamine, serotonin, acetylcholine
- **90% effective** at reducing opioid withdrawal (subjective reports)
- **Resets tolerance** to opioid-naïve levels → CRITICAL overdose risk
- **Potentiates opioids & stimulants** when co-administered

**Seizure Risk:**
- Ibogaine doesn't induce seizures
- **BUT:** Seizures under ibogaine influence → lethal arrhythmias
- **Several fatalities** from benzo/alcohol withdrawal seizures (Alper 2012)

---

### **4. OPIOID-SPECIFIC PROTOCOLS**

**Long-Acting Opioid Switch (CRITICAL):**

**Methadone:**
- **Target:** ≤2mg residual dose before ibogaine
- **Conservative (24h half-life):** 7 days to clear from 100mg/day
- **Cautious (48h half-life):** 14 days to clear from 100mg/day
- **Methadone itself prolongs QT** (Kornick 2003)
- **DO NOT treat within 24h of last dose** - multiple mechanisms of QT prolongation

**Buprenorphine:**
- **Target:** ≤0.125mg residual dose before ibogaine
- **Conservative (24h half-life):** 6 days from 4mg/day
- **Cautious (72h half-life):** 18 days from 4mg/day

**Switch Protocol:**
- **Oral morphine preferred** for stabilization
- **Gradual titration essential:** Calculate conversion dose, build up slowly as long-acting clears
- **Medical supervision mandatory:** Use SOWS & OOWS scales
- **Never oversed:** Increases tolerance, causes discomfort
- **DO NOT taper benzos during opioid switch** - stabilize benzos first
- **Verify clearance:** Urinalysis (note: buprenorphine doesn't show on standard ELISA)

**Quantitative vs Qualitative Testing:**
- Qualitative = presence
- Quantitative = concentration (confirms slow metabolizer accuracy)

**Post-Treatment:**
- **Tolerance reset to naïve:** Inform patient repeatedly
- **Overdose risk on relapse:** Use naïve dosing only

---

### **5. BENZODIAZEPINE PROTOCOLS (LIFE-OR-DEATH)**

**NEVER STOP BENZOS BEFORE/DURING TREATMENT**

**Rationale:**
- Ibogaine has **zero effect** on GABA system (Popik 1998)
- **Does NOT reduce** benzo withdrawal symptoms
- **hERG blockage + QT prolongation** INCREASES seizure likelihood
- **Several fatalities** from benzo withdrawal seizures (Alper 2012)

**Protocol:**
- **Continue throughout** treatment to prevent seizures
- **Switch short-acting → long-acting** (diazepam, clonazepam) before arrival
- **Ashton Manual** for equivalent dose calculations
- **Observable withdrawal:** 24-72h (short-acting), up to 3 weeks (long-acting)
- **72-hour observation** if dose accuracy questioned
- **High-dose tolerance:** Poor candidate unless stabilized

**Effects on Ibogaine:**
- **Dulls psychoactive/therapeutic effects** - acceptable trade-off for safety
- Can be **beneficial** for overwhelming effects or prolonged insomnia

**Benzo-Naïve Patients:**
- Use short-acting (alprazolam) if needed
- Small percentage may experience mild waking dream state as side effect

---

### **6. ALCOHOL PROTOCOLS**

**Alcohol Withdrawal = Life-Threatening**

**Timeline:**
- **Acute withdrawal:** 24-120 hours post-last drink
- **Peak seizure risk:** 48-72 hours

**Protocol:**
- **All patients:** Abstain 3 days before, 7 days after treatment
- **At-risk patients:** Complete medical detox 5-7 days before ibogaine
- **If suspected use during intake:** Re-detox under medical supervision, 5-7 days minimum
- **Clear all symptoms:** QT prolongation, arrhythmia, hypertension, delirium tremens, confusion

**Multiple Fatalities:** Alcohol withdrawal seizures + delirium tremens (Alper 2012)

---

### **7. STIMULANT PROTOCOLS**

**Cocaine/Crack:**
- Blocks hERG channel → QT prolongation (Karle 2002)
- Ibogaine potentiates effects (Szumlinski 2000)
- **Cease ≥7 days** before treatment

**Methamphetamine:**
- Prolongs QT without hERG interaction (Haning 2007)
- Affects CYP2D6 metabolism
- Ibogaine potentiates effects (Szumlinski 2000)
- **Cease ≥5 days** before treatment

**Prescription Stimulants:**
- Ritalin, Adderall
- **Cease ≥5 days** before treatment

**Caffeine:**
- Induces hypokalemia (Tajima 2010)
- Inhibits melatonin metabolism (Härtter 2003)
- May contribute to QT prolongation
- **Cease ≥5 days** before treatment

**Post-Cessation:** Chronically-using patients may have hyperexcitable hearts - monitor closely

---

### **8. ANTIDEPRESSANT PROTOCOLS**

**SSRI/SNRI:**
- **Serotonin syndrome risk** (ibogaine also increases serotonin)
- **CYP2D6 interaction** (slows ibogaine metabolism)
- **Ideal:** Complete taper under psychiatric supervision, psychiatric reassessment post-taper
- **Common practice (not ideal):** 2-3 days post-cessation (due to difficult withdrawals)

**MAOI:**
- Affects metabolism of many drugs
- May amplify psychoactive effects (extent unknown)
- **Stop 7-10 days** before treatment

---

### **9. STEROID PROTOCOLS**

**Corticosteroids:**
- Prolong QT interval (Busjahn 2004)
- Used for asthma, respiratory conditions, arthritis, autoimmune disorders
- **Calculate half-life, cease with sufficient elimination time**
- **If emergency use needed during treatment:** Use with extreme caution

**Anabolic Steroids:**
- Oral use → liver malfunction risk (check LFTs)
- Get PSA test + metabolism tests
- Monitor hemoglobin/hematocrit (high count = thrombosis/clot/heart attack risk)
- These patients often have post-treatment anxiety from high BP
- Counsel as substance abuse
- **Testosterone (shots/creams/gels):** Not used day of treatment

---

### **10. ELECTROLYTE MANAGEMENT (CRITICAL)**

**Preferred Ranges (Stricter than Standard Labs):**
- **Potassium:** 4.5-5.5 mEq/L
- **Magnesium:** 1.5-2.5 mEq/L

**Supplementation Protocol:**
- **Only if below preferred range** - test and supplement until normal
- **DO NOT supplement if normal** - excess is as dangerous as deficiency
- **Retest after detoxifying therapies** (colonics, enemas deplete electrolytes)

**Hypokalemia Finding:**
- **100% of documented fatalities** had hypokalemia (König & Hilber 2015)
- Low K + hERG blockage + bradycardia = severely depleted repolarization reserve

---

### **11. MEDICAL SUPERVISION REQUIREMENTS**

**Minimum Staff Credentials:**
- **All providers:** BLS (Basic Life Support) certification
- **Required competencies:** Status epilepticus treatment, airway management

**Required On-Site:**
- **ACLS (Advanced Cardiac Life Support)** trained medical staff
- **Knowledgeable** about ibogaine pharmacodynamics
- **Preferred:** GITA ACLS for Ibogaine Therapy certification

**Emergency Preparedness:**
- **<15 minutes** to fully equipped emergency responders
- **<30 minutes** to 24-hour hospital
- Written emergency plan, clear staff roles

**Supervision Timeline:**
- **Close Supervision (12-24h):** 3-lead monitor, ACLS staff present, monitor respiration
- **Continue until:** T-wave normalized for 1+ hour (typically 12-15h, can be 24h)
- **Regular Supervision (72h):** Vitals q4h minimum, ACLS staff on-hand

### **12. DOSING PROTOCOLS**

**General Considerations:**
- **Begin dosing:** OOWS 3-7 (optimal withdrawal discomfort)
- **Delay if excessive:** OOWS ≥10 (re-stabilize on morphine)
- **Never exceed:** 12mg/kg (higher doses = more cardiac abnormalities)
- **Absolute max:** 24mg/kg in 24-hour period

**Test Dose (Optional):**
- 2-3 mg/kg threshold dose
- ≥2 hours before remaining dose
- Monitor for allergic reaction, metabolic response
- Not necessary with thorough screening

**CYP2D6 Phenotype Adjustments:**
- **Poor Metabolizers:** May need reduced doses or extended monitoring
- **Ultra-Rapid Metabolizers:** May need adjusted regimens
- See Glue 2015 for details

**Hepatic Conditions:**
- Even slight elevation → drastic metabolism changes
- **Test dose mandatory:** Observe 8+ hours before continuing
- Can result in very rapid OR greatly prolonged/intensified effects

**Extreme Weight:**
- mg/kg not literal for very over/underweight patients
- Consider digestive system size
- Careful dose-response monitoring

**Booster Doses:**
- 1-5 mg/kg threshold doses
- **Timing to avoid cardiac complications:**
  - Within 2-3h of initial dose, OR
  - ≥24-36h after initial dose
- Used for: residual withdrawals (verify SOWS/OOWS), psychotherapeutic issues, prolonging effects
- **Rule out other causes:** Dehydration, lack of sleep before boosting

**Life Contract:**
- Agreement patient will return to life if given opportunity to leave body
- Sets psychological framework for experience

### **13. INTERVENTIONS**

**Basic Treatment Course:**
- Anxiety: GABA supplementation, benzodiazepines
- Chronic pain: Non-opioid NSAIDs, spasmolytics, natural products, CES therapy
- Constipation: Hydration, fiber, stool softeners (avoid electrolyte-depleting preps)
- Dehydration: Oral electrolyte fluids, IV saline if needed
- Depression: Psychotherapy, repeated lower ibogaine doses, adjunct therapies
- Insomnia: Natural products, diphenhydramine, Z-drugs, short-term benzos

**Acute/Emergency:**
- **Alcohol Withdrawal:** 10mg diazepam IV/IM, repeat if needed, call EMS if delirium tremens
- **Acute Confusional State:** Avoid antipsychotics (cause prolonged negative reactions), benzodiazepines carefully, constant supervision, transpersonal framework
- **Allergic Reactions:** Diphenhydramine (mild), methylprednisolone (serious), epinephrine auto-injector (anaphylaxis + monitor + call EMS)
- **Arrhythmias:** Follow ACLS protocols, **DO NOT use amiodarone** (prolongs QT), defibrillate if indicated
- **Asthma Attack:** Emergency steroid inhaler, cease ibogaine, monitor O₂/pulse/BP (note: steroids prolong QT)
- **Bradycardia (Symptomatic):** Check vitals, O₂ if <90%, 1L Hartmann's/LR IV, atropine 0.25-0.5mg IV q3-5min (max 3mg), consider EMS
- **Dehydration:** 1L Hartmann's/LR IV, monitor vitals
- **Hypotension:** Treat bradycardia if cause, otherwise push fluids (oral or 1L IV), call EMS if ineffective
- **Opioid Overdose:** Call EMS, assisted breathing, naloxone 0.4-2mg IV/IM/SC q2-3min (note: causes tachycardia, precipitates withdrawal)
- **O₂ Saturation <92%:** Supplement O₂ 2L via nasal cannula, titrate to keep >95%, ACLS or transfer if can't maintain on 1-6L/min
- **QTc >500ms:** Immediate hospital transfer for cardiac monitoring
- **Status Epilepticus:** Call EMS, diazepam 5-10mg IV/IM initially, repeat q10-15min up to 30mg max (watch for respiratory arrest)
- **Vomiting:** Check for medicine regurgitation, monitor for dehydration, diphenhydramine 50mg IM q6h PRN

**Preparatory Fluids (Pre-Dosing):**
- 1L lactated ringer/normal saline + 1 ampule magnesium sulphate
- Creates beneficial fluid overload
- Decreases hypotension
- Protects against torsades de pointes
- Administer: 1h before to 2h after flood dose

**Anti-Nausea (Pre-Dosing):**
- **Preferred:** Diphenhydramine 25-50mg IM
- **Avoid:** Metoclopramide (centrally acting, prolongs QT), palonosetron (drops HR)
- **Alternative:** Ginger extracts/tea for mild cases
- Use pharmaceutical only if significant dehydration risk

**IV Port Access:**
- **Mandatory** until physician confirms cardiac risk passed
- Prevents dehydration complications
- Emergency medication access (arrhythmia, seizure)
- **If no vein access:** Central line (hospital insertion/removal unless sterile field available)

### **14. SET & SETTING**

**Physical Environment:**
- Comfortable bed, easy bathroom access
- Darkening capability (Mindfold masks recommended)
- Quiet space (earplugs, headphones available)
- Ambient lighting (candles, soft lamps)
- No interruptions entire treatment episode
- Patient becomes extremely light/sound sensitive

**Music (Critical Component):**
- **High-fidelity headphones** essential
- **Nonverbal preferred** during peak (or language patient doesn't understand)
- **Examples:** Indian ragas, Sufi chants, Mongolian throat singing, Bwiti/pygmy polyphonic singing
- Natural overtones therapeutically beneficial
- Patient preference paramount

**Therapeutic Approach:**
- **Male/female dyad** recommended (or minimum same-gender therapist)
- **Minimal conversation** during acute phase (4-8h)
- Check-ins only for necessities (bathroom, medical)
- **No active psychotherapy during peak**
- Pre/post sessions appropriate
- Space for internal process essential
- Empathic skill > medical credentials (but medical supervision still required)

**Session Timing:**
- Late afternoon/evening = fewer distractions
- Daytime = easier medical/emergency access
- **Risk reduction should determine timing**

### **15. EXPERIENCE PHASES**

**Onset:** 1-3 hours post-administration

**1. Acute Phase (4-8 hours):**
- Most intense, visual, oneirogenic
- "Waking dream" state
- Watching film on inner screen
- Emotional detachment despite emotional content
- Profound contemplation, self-reflection
- Analogous to conscious REM sleep (Goutarel 1993)
- **Ataxia most pronounced** (difficult to walk)

**2. Evaluative Phase (8-20 hours):**
- Cognitive, emotionally neutral review
- Integration of acute phase material
- Prefer undisturbed, lying still quietly

**3. Residual Stimulation (24-72 hours):**
- Exhaustion common
- Continued sleep difficulty
- Introspection relaxes
- Attention returns to external environment

### **16. NEUROPLASTICITY & ADJUNCT THERAPIES**

**Mechanism:**
- Ibogaine & noribogaine increase GDNF (He 2006, Carnicella 2010)
- GDNF stimulates new dopamine neuron growth
- Produces neuroplasticity state
- Improves ability to learn new information (Popik 1996)
- Window to ingrain new habits/thinking patterns

**Risk:**
- Can also ingrain negative patterns if positive ones not enforced
- **Requires integration support**

**Recommended Adjunct Therapies:**
- **Psychotherapy:** CBT (strong addiction efficacy - Hofmann 2012), Holotropic Breathwork, Gestalt
- **Nutrition:** Neurotransmitter precursors, addressing deficiencies
- **Bodywork:** Connection to body, grounding, pain relief
- **Exercise:** Increases beta-endorphins
- **Meditation:** Brain hemisphere balance, clarity, focus, relaxation
- **Neurofeedback/Brain Stimulation:** FDA-approved devices for anxiety, insomnia, depression, chronic pain

**Evidence for Integration:**
- **Better outcomes with ongoing support** (GITA 2014, Schenberg 2014)
- Bio-psycho-social addiction model (Tatarsky 2007, Mate 2009, Alexander 2010)
- Ibogaine as "elementary step" in larger SUD treatment process

---

### **17. PSYCHOLOGICAL SAFETY**

**Transpersonal Psychology Framework:**
- Experiences are **"holotropic"** (oriented towards wholeness), not psychotic (Grof 1992)
- Draws from millennia of healing/rites of passage traditions
- Provides framework for therapeutic integration

**Rare Psychological Adverse Events:**
- Acute confusional states (hours to days)
- Prolonged psychosis (Houenou 2011, Dyer 2011)
- Post-treatment mania (Marta 2015)

**Management Principles:**
- **Comprehensive psychological assessment** pre-treatment mandatory
- **Sensitivity to oneirogenic state** during/after treatment
- **Set & setting paramount** at all times
- Differentiate spiritual emergency (cathartic potential) from psychosis/medical emergency

**Medication Guidelines:**
- **AVOID antipsychotics** (haloperidol, chlorpromazine) - cause prolonged negative reactions with ibogaine
- **Benzodiazepines acceptable** for overwhelming effects, insomnia, slowing experience
- Don't interrupt psychological process unless necessary

**Acute Confusional State:**
- Time/energy intensive but ultimately beneficial
- **All known cases:** Patients reported benefit when properly supported
- Constant supervision in secure environment until resolution
- Transpersonal framework vs chemical intervention
- No reported permanent psychosis cases

**Grof on Mania:**
- "LSD subjects whose sessions terminate in incomplete rebirth show all signs of mania"
- Resolution: "Turn inward, face difficult emotions, complete (re)birth process" → mania disappears

### **18. DISCHARGE & FOLLOW-UP**

**Discharge Criteria:**
- **Minimum 72 hours** post-administration
- All serious health risks subsided
- Patient in right mind
- **Exception:** Medical emergency requiring hospital admission

**Right to Leave:**
- Patient may leave when in right mind
- Offer discharge waiver acknowledging risks
- Present available options
- **Cannot physically detain** unless threat to self/others

**Follow-up Protocol:**
- **6 weeks minimum** staff availability
- **3 hours/week maximum** (except extenuating circumstances from treatment)
- Encourage additional therapy, peer support, residential services
- Emphasize correlation between ongoing support and long-term success
- Healthy lifestyle choices easier to ingrain post-ibogaine
- Counseling on diet, meditation, exercise

**Record Keeping:**
- Confidential, secure storage
- **5-7 years minimum** retention (US requirement)
- Useful for future retrospective analysis
- **Patient rights:** Review anytime, copy upon request (reasonable fee, cannot deny due to inability to pay)

- Confirm emergency contact information

**Major Life-Threatening Risks to Emphasize:**
- **Stopping benzos/alcohol <72h** → seizures → life-threatening
- **Using stimulants/opiates during treatment** or misrepresenting use → overdose risk
- **Malnutrition/dehydration** → life-threatening complications
- **Post-treatment opioid use** → account for reset tolerance → overdose risk
- **Active infection/abscess/inaccessible veins** → absolute exclusion → need medical care first

### **22. HYDRATION PROTOCOL**

**Pre-Treatment:**
- ~1 fl oz per kg body weight per day
- More for physically active
- **Electrolyte-containing fluids** (coconut water, electrolyte preparation)
- **NOT plain water**
- Light/clear urine = sufficient hydration

**During Treatment (24h before to 72h after):**
- **All hydration with electrolyte fluids**
- Maintains electrolyte levels
- Oral preferred
- IV saline if unable to retain orally (chronic vomiting, cardiac concerns)

**Dehydration Signs:**
- White tongue
- Dark urine
- Decreased skin elasticity
- Elevated vitals

### **23. PRE-TREATMENT DIET & FASTING**

**Avoid:**
- Intensive fasts, cleanses
- Severe protein/diet restriction (juice fasting, master cleanse)
- Anything depleting energy/electrolytes

**Encourage:**
- Healthy whole foods
- Eat well day before treatment

**Day of Treatment:**
- **Empty stomach** to minimize nausea
- **8-12 hour fast** before dosing to avoid vomiting/medicine loss
- **Two bowel movements** required before administration
- Check bowel sounds (positive in all 4 quadrants)

### **24. SECONDARY SCREENING (INTAKE)**

**ECG:**
- Retake upon arrival (confirm application results)
- Same exclusion/risk criteria apply

**Urinalysis:**
- Screen: pregnancy, all substances
- **Include:** Benzodiazepines, buprenorphine, EtG/EtS (alcohol)
- **Note:** Oxycodone, fentanyl, buprenorphine don't show on standard ELISA (request specifically)
- **Note:** Clonazepam, lorazepam don't show as benzos
- **Qualitative vs Quantitative:** Concentration confirms slow metabolizer accuracy
- **GCMS/GCLS analysis:** Generally available within 24h

**Personal Belongings:**
- Turn over all medications, food, drugs (including nasal sprays, diarrhea meds)
- Consider electronics for distraction reduction
- **Baggage search option:** In patient presence, but may create distrust if not careful
- **Alternative:** Extended stabilization observation + relationship building

**Physical Exam:**
- Review clinical history, preparation protocols
- **Re-test blood if >14 days old** or after detoxifying therapy
- Current ECG required before clearance

**Stabilization:**
- **≥24 hours** on short-acting opiates (morphine sulfate preferred)
- Observe psychological condition
- Build therapeutic relationship
- Refer to Ch 3-9 for substance-specific stabilization
