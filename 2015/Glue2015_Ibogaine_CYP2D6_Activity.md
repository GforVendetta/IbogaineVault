# Influence of CYP2D6 Activity on the Pharmacokinetics and Pharmacodynamics of a Single 20 mg Dose of Ibogaine in Healthy Volunteers

**Citation:** Glue, P., Winter, H., Garbe, K., Jakobi, H., Lyudin, A., Lenagh-Glue, Z., & Hung, C.T. (2015). Influence of CYP2D6 Activity on the Pharmacokinetics and Pharmacodynamics of a Single 20 mg Dose of Ibogaine in Healthy Volunteers. *The Journal of Clinical Pharmacology*, 55(6), 680–687. doi:10.1002/jcph.471

## Abstract

Conversion of ibogaine to its active metabolite noribogaine appears to be mediated primarily by CYP2D6. We compared 168 hours pharmacokinetic profiles of both analytes after a single oral 20 mg dose of ibogaine in 21 healthy subjects who had been pretreated for 6 days with placebo or the CYP2D6 inhibitor paroxetine. In placebo-pretreated subjects, ibogaine was rapidly converted to noribogaine. Median peak noribogaine concentrations occurred at 4 hours. Compared with placebo-pretreated subjects, paroxetine-pretreated subjects had rapid (T_max = 1.5 hours) and substantial absorption of ibogaine, with detectable levels out to 72 hours, and an elimination half-life of 10.2 hours. In this group, ibogaine was also rapidly converted to noribogaine with a median T_max of 3 hours. Extent of noribogaine exposure was similar in both groups. CYP2D6 phenotype was robustly correlated with ibogaine AUC₀₋ₜ (r = 0.82) and C_max (r = 0.77). Active moiety (ibogaine plus noribogaine) exposure was ~2-fold higher in paroxetine-pretreated subjects. Single 20 mg ibogaine doses were safe and well tolerated in all subjects. The doubling of exposure to active moiety in subjects with reduced CYP2D6 activity suggests it may be prudent to genotype patients awaiting ibogaine treatment, and to at least halve the intended dose of ibogaine in CYP2D6 poor metabolizers.

## Key Findings

- **CYP2D6 inhibition doubled active moiety exposure:** Paroxetine pretreatment increased active moiety AUC₀₋ₜ from 948 (407) to 1793 (695) nM·h/mL (P = .005), representing an approximately 2-fold increase in total pharmacologically active exposure
- **Ibogaine exposure dramatically increased in CYP2D6-inhibited subjects:** C_max was 26-fold greater (29.5 vs 1.1 ng/mL, P < .0001) and AUC₀₋ₜ was 66-fold greater (238.2 vs 3.6 ng·h/mL, P = .0028) in paroxetine-pretreated subjects
- **Noribogaine exposure equivalent between groups:** AUC₀₋ₜ was similar (placebo: 277.4, paroxetine: 304.1 ng·h/mL, P = .64), confirming alternative metabolic pathways (CYP2C19, CYP3A4) partially compensate — but with dramatically different kinetic profiles
- **Robust CYP2D6 phenotype–PK correlation:** Day 7 DM:DX metabolic ratio correlated strongly with ibogaine AUC₀₋ₜ (r = 0.82) and C_max (r = 0.77), validating phenotyping as a clinical screening tool
- **Paroxetine produced 11-fold shift in metabolic ratio:** Mean DM:DX MR increased from 0.015 to 0.160 (P < .05), though only 2/11 subjects converted to phenotypic PM status (MR > 0.3)
- **Ibogaine half-life extended 4-fold in CYP2D6-inhibited subjects:** t₁/₂ increased from 2.5 to 10.2 hours (P = .009), with detectable levels persisting to 72 hours
- **20 mg ibogaine safe and well tolerated:** No vital sign changes, no hallucinations or perceptual disturbances; 25 AEs total (most attributable to paroxetine, not ibogaine)
- **Clinical recommendation:** CYP2D6 genotyping or phenotyping should precede ibogaine treatment, with at minimum halved doses for poor metabolisers

## Methodology

Randomised, double-blind, parallel-group study conducted at the University of Otago, New Zealand (Ethics: Southern Health and Disability Ethics Committee, NTY/12/040; Registry: ACTRN12613000324718). Twenty-one healthy male volunteers (mean age 23.5 years, range 20–40; mean BMI 24.5 kg/m², range 19.8–30.9; 16 Caucasian, 2 Māori, 2 Asian, 1 other) were randomised to paroxetine (n = 11) or placebo (n = 9) pretreatment.

**Design:** Day 1 — baseline CYP2D6 phenotyping via 30 mg oral dextromethorphan with 5-hour urine collection for DM:DX metabolic ratio. Days 2–15 — double-blind paroxetine (10 mg days 2–3, then 20 mg/day days 4–15) or matching placebo. Day 7 — repeat CYP2D6 phenotyping. Day 8 — single 20 mg oral ibogaine with plasma sampling at 17 timepoints from pre-dose to 168 hours. CYP2D6 PM classification threshold: DM:DX MR > 0.3. No genotyping performed.

**Pharmacodynamic assessments:** Dark-adapted pupillometry (Neuroptics PLR-200, <5 lux) at 9 timepoints for µ-opioid agonist effects. Visual analogue scales (100 mm) for sleepy, energetic, nausea, anxious, and calm at pre-dose plus 6 timepoints to 168 hours.

**Analytical methods:** Ibogaine and noribogaine quantified by LC-MS/MS (internal standard: noribogaine-d4). Assay precision <9% (ibogaine), <13% (noribogaine). Non-compartmental PK analysis. Subject 1 excluded from group analysis (phenotypic PM at baseline, MR = 0.51).

### Subject 1 (Phenotypic PM at Baseline)

Subject 1, excluded from the group analysis due to baseline PM status (DM:DX MR = 0.51), demonstrated the most extreme PK divergence: ibogaine C_max was 6.9-fold higher than noribogaine C_max (47.5 vs 6.9 ng/mL), and ibogaine AUC₀₋ₜ was 2.6-fold higher than noribogaine (580.3 vs 220.8 ng·h/mL). By comparison, the paroxetine group's ibogaine/noribogaine ratios were smaller (C_max ratio 2.3, AUC ratio 0.8), consistent with residual CYP2D6 activity in phenotypically inhibited but genotypically EM subjects.

## Clinical Implications

**CYP2D6 screening is essential before ibogaine administration.** The 2-fold increase in active moiety exposure — and up to 66-fold increase in parent ibogaine — in subjects with reduced CYP2D6 activity makes this the single most important pharmacogenomic variable for ibogaine dosing. At therapeutic doses (10–25 mg/kg), these multiplicative factors could produce dangerously high ibogaine concentrations in unscreened poor metabolisers, particularly given ibogaine's known cardiac effects at higher exposures. This directly supports clinical screening protocols: CYP2D6 genotyping or phenotyping should be considered mandatory prior to any ibogaine administration.

**Dose reduction guidance:** The authors recommend at minimum halving the intended ibogaine dose in CYP2D6 PMs. This conservative recommendation emerges from a 20 mg dose study and may underestimate required adjustments at therapeutic doses, where non-linear kinetics could amplify the CYP2D6 effect. The ascending-dose study by the same group ([[2015/Glue2015_Noribogaine_Ascending_Doses|Glue2015 Ascending Dose]]) provides complementary safety margins for noribogaine, but equivalent ibogaine dose-escalation data in CYP2D6 PMs remains unavailable.

**Drug interaction implications:** Any concurrent medication that inhibits CYP2D6 (SSRIs, particularly paroxetine and fluoxetine; bupropion; quinidine; some antipsychotics) can phenocopy PM status. This extends the contraindication beyond genotypic PMs to include patients on CYP2D6-inhibiting medications who may not have completed adequate washout. The 6-day paroxetine pretreatment in this study is clinically relevant — patients tapering from SSRIs before ibogaine treatment may still have substantial CYP2D6 inhibition from residual drug or active metabolites.

**Noribogaine equivalence reframes the safety concern:** The finding that noribogaine AUC is equivalent regardless of CYP2D6 status, while ibogaine exposure diverges dramatically, suggests the cardiac risk in CYP2D6 PMs is driven primarily by elevated parent ibogaine rather than the active metabolite. This aligns with evidence from [[2012/Koenig2012_Ibogaine_hERG_Cardiac_Arrhythmia_Risk|Koenig2012]] showing ibogaine has greater hERG affinity than noribogaine. See also [[2024/Knuijver2024_Pharmacokinetics_Pharmacodynamics_Ibogaine_OUD_Patients|Knuijver2024]] for the most comprehensive analysis of CYP2D6–cardiac risk interactions.

## Limitations

- **Incomplete CYP2D6 phenoconversion:** Only 2 of 11 paroxetine-treated subjects became phenotypic PMs (MR > 0.3). The study therefore underestimates the PK differences that would be observed in genotypic CYP2D6 PMs, as suggested by the robust correlation data (Figure 4) and the extreme PK profile of Subject 1
- **No CYP3A4/CYP2C19 genotyping:** PM polymorphisms for these alternative ibogaine-demethylating enzymes could have confounded PK data, particularly in paroxetine-treated subjects where CYP2D6 was inhibited and these pathways became the primary clearance route
- **Low single dose (20 mg) limits extrapolation:** At therapeutic doses (10–25 mg/kg, i.e. 700–1750 mg for a 70 kg adult), non-linear kinetics from enzyme saturation could amplify the CYP2D6 effect beyond what this study predicts
- **No ECG data collected:** The study assessed pharmacodynamics via pupillometry and VAS only — no QTc or cardiac monitoring was performed, precluding any assessment of the cardiac safety implications of elevated ibogaine exposure in CYP2D6-inhibited subjects
- **Healthy male volunteers only:** No women, no patients with opioid dependence (the target therapeutic population), and no subjects with comorbidities that might alter ibogaine metabolism or response
- **Pupillometry results inconsistent:** The modest miosis observed (0.4–0.6 mm) did not correlate with plasma concentrations and conflicted with the larger noribogaine study showing no pupillary change, leaving the µ-opioid pharmacodynamic profile unresolved
