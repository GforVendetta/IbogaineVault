# Streamlined screening platforms lead to the discovery of pachysiphine synthase from *Tabernanthe iboga*

**Citation:** Kamileen MO, Nakamura Y, Luck K, Heinicke S, Hong B, Colinas M, Lichman BR, O'Connor SE. Streamlined screening platforms lead to the discovery of pachysiphine synthase from *Tabernanthe iboga*. *bioRxiv* (preprint). 2024 Jul 1. doi:10.1101/2024.06.30.601415

**Key Findings:** Discovery of two novel CYP450 enzymes from *T. iboga* — pachysiphine synthase (TiPS, CYP71D821) and tabersonine-16-hydroxylase (TiT16H, CYP71BE297) — using miniaturized *N. benthamiana* leaf disk assays. Demonstrates T. iboga produces pachysiphine and 16-OH-pachysiphine in young leaves, previously unreported.

---

## Abstract

Plant-specialised metabolism is largely driven by the oxidative tailoring of key chemical scaffolds catalysed by cytochrome P450 (CYP450) enzymes. The monoterpene indole alkaloids tabersonine and pseudo-tabersonine, found in the medicinal plant *Tabernanthe iboga*, are extensively modified by oxidative reactions. Here we developed a streamlined screening strategy to screen the activity of *T. iboga* CYP450s in *Nicotiana benthamiana*. Using multigene constructs encoding the biosynthesis of tabersonine and pseudo-tabersonine scaffolds, we set out to uncover the CYP450s responsible for oxidative transformations of these scaffolds. Our approach identified two *T. iboga* cytochrome P450 enzymes: **pachysiphine synthase (PS)** and **16-hydroxy-tabersonine synthase (T16H)**. These enzymes catalyse an epoxidation and site-specific hydroxylation of tabersonine to produce pachysiphine and 16-OH-tabersonine, respectively.

## Methodology

### Screening Platform Development

The authors developed a miniaturised assay using *N. benthamiana* leaf disks:
1. Transient gene expression via Agrobacterium transformation
2. Leaf disks (10mm diameter) incubated in 300μl buffer with 25μM substrate
3. 16-hour incubation allowing substrate diffusion
4. LC-MS analysis of metabolic products

**Advantages over traditional methods:**
- Minimises substrate quantities required
- Eliminates wash-off issues with syringe infiltration
- Uniform fresh weight enables standardised normalisation

### Gene-Stacked Plasmids

Two multigene constructs were created:
- **pS-Tab:** Produces (–)-tabersonine from stemmadenine acetate
- **pS-psTab:** Produces (+)-pseudo-tabersonine from stemmadenine acetate

These constructs enabled screening of 30 T. iboga CYP71 candidates in pools of 10.

## Key Discoveries

### Pachysiphine Synthase (TiPS, CYP71D821)

- Catalyses **β-epoxidation** of tabersonine to form pachysiphine
- Product is structurally similar to lochnericine (found in *C. roseus*) but with opposite epoxide stereochemistry
- Only 46% amino acid identity with *C. roseus* TEX1/TEX2, suggesting **independent evolution**

### Tabersonine-16-Hydroxylase (TiT16H, CYP71BE297)

- Catalyses **site-specific hydroxylation** at position 16
- Only 56% amino acid identity with *C. roseus* T16H despite same activity
- Suggests **convergent evolution** of this enzymatic function

### Double Oxidation Products

When both enzymes act together on tabersonine:
- Produces **16-OH-pachysiphine** (m/z 369)
- Order of enzyme action does not affect yield
- Both pachysiphine and 16-OH-pachysiphine were subsequently detected in T. iboga young leaves — **previously unreported natural products**

---

## Expression Patterns

From transcriptomic data:
- **TiPS:** Expressed in roots, flowers, and young leaves
- **TiT16H:** Expressed primarily in leaves

This suggests pachysiphine may be synthesised in roots/flowers and transported to young leaves for storage.

---

## Significance for Dimeric Alkaloids

Pachysiphine and 16-OH-pachysiphine serve as **precursors for pharmacologically important dimeric alkaloids**:
- Melodine K
- Connophylline
- Conolodinines A-D
- Scandomelidine

These compounds display various pharmacological activities. The discovery of TiPS and TiT16H provides foundational knowledge for elucidating biosynthetic pathways to these complex dimeric alkaloids.

---

## Alternative Screening Platform: *C. roseus* Flowers

The authors also demonstrated that *Catharanthus roseus* flower petals can serve as an expression platform:
- Flowers contain endogenous stemmadenine acetate pools
- Can access in situ substrates without exogenous supplementation
- Useful for screening enzymes requiring rare/difficult-to-access substrates

---

## Clinical Relevance

While this is a fundamental biochemistry paper without direct clinical applications, understanding the biosynthesis of *T. iboga* alkaloids has implications for:
- **Sustainable production:** Potential for heterologous biosynthesis of iboga alkaloids
- **Analogue development:** Knowledge of enzymatic machinery enables structure-guided analogue design
- **Quality control:** Understanding biosynthetic pathways aids in identifying adulterants and metabolites
