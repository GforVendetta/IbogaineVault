## Summary

### Introduction

The review opens with historical context: Bwiti ceremonial use, Howard Lotsof's 1960s discovery, and the challenges that have prevented ibogaine from becoming a mainstream therapeutic despite demonstrated anti-addictive properties.

### Why Ibogaine Is Problematic as a Drug

The authors identify ibogaine's key liabilities:

1. **Long-lasting hallucinations** — Undesirable for clinical settings
2. **Cerebellar toxicity** — Purkinje cell death at high doses, causing tremors and functional lesions
3. **hERG potassium ion channel inhibition** — Ibogaine is a lipophilic molecule with a basic amine, a common pharmacophore for hERG inhibitors. Several deaths linked to cardiac interference.
4. **Polypharmacology** — Low-affinity ligand for nicotinic acetylcholine, dopamine, serotonin, mu and kappa opioid receptors — unclear mechanism of action

### Three Drug Discovery Programmes

#### 1. 18-Methoxycoronaridine (18-MC) — Kuehne Lab

- Developed during aspidosperma/iboga alkaloid synthesis work
- **Mechanism:** α3β4 nicotinic receptor binding correlates with anti-addictive effects
- **Advantages:** Equivalent efficacy to ibogaine for morphine/cocaine self-administration reduction, NO tremorigenic effects, no cerebellar toxicity at high doses (100mg/kg)
- **Status:** Currently in Phase II clinical trials for opioid use disorder

#### 2. Oxa-noribogaine — Sames Lab (2015)

- Developed using C-H bond functionalisation technology
- Swaps benzofuran for ibogaine's indole
- **Mechanism:** Potent kappa opioid receptor agonist (in vitro and in vivo)
- **Advantages:** Greater efficacy than noribogaine in rat models, NO cardiac pro-arrhythmic potential
- Long-lasting suppression of morphine and fentanyl self-administration after single dose
- Suggests kappa opioid agonism as mechanism for ibogaine's anti-addictive properties

#### 3. Tabernanthalog (TBG) — Olson Lab

- Designed using function-oriented synthesis — pared back complexity to find minimum pharmacophore
- Synthesised in a single step
- **Mechanism:** 5-HT2A serotonin receptor agonism → neural plasticity (dendrite arbor complexity)
- **Advantages:** 
  - Non-hallucinogenic (no head-twitch response in mice) — attributed to 6-methoxy substituent
  - NOT a hERG inhibitor (deletion of lipophilic isoquinuclidine ring)
  - Water soluble
- Provides third explanation for ibogaine's effects: 5-HT2A-mediated neural plasticity

### Synthetic Chemistry Overview

The review catalogues 20+ total syntheses organised by ring construction order:

| Strategy | Order of Ring Formation | Key Examples |
|----------|------------------------|--------------|
| Classical | Isoquinuclidine → Indole → Azepine | Büchi 1965, Fukuyama 1999 |
| Alternative | Azepine → Isoquinuclidine → Indole | Sallay 1967 |
| Modern | Various asymmetric approaches | Trost 1978 (first asymmetric), Luo 2016, Townsend 2023 |

Key structural challenge: tetra-substituted all-cis internal cyclohexane ring with dense stereochemistry.

### Non-Classical Iboga Alkaloids

Over 100 new iboga alkaloids isolated in past 20 years with:
- Varying oxidation states
- Rearranged ring systems
- New carbon skeletons requiring new synthetic strategies

Examples synthesised: tabertinggine, conodusines A/B/C, voatinggine, ervaoffine J/K, tabernanthine, ibogaline.

## Relevance for Clinical Practice

This review explains **why ibogaine analogues matter**:

1. **hERG liability** — The primary driver of ibogaine-related cardiac deaths is a structural feature (lipophilic amine) that can be modified
2. **Multiple mechanisms** — Three different drug programmes suggest three different mechanisms (α3β4 nicotinic, κ-opioid, 5-HT2A plasticity) — the field hasn't converged on which matters most
3. **Trade-offs** — Each analogue sacrifices something (18-MC loses some efficacy, TBG loses the isoquinuclidine entirely)

For clinicians: Understanding this landscape helps contextualise why ibogaine remains in clinical use despite known risks — the analogues aren't yet proven equivalent in humans.
