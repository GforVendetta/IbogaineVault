# Psychedelics Promote Structural and Functional Neural Plasticity

**Citation:** Ly, C., Greb, A.C., Cameron, L.P., Wong, J.M., Barragan, E.V., Wilson, P.C., Burbach, K.F., Soltanzadeh Zarandi, S., Sood, A., Paddy, M.R., Duim, W.C., Dennis, M.Y., McAllister, A.K., Ori-McKenney, K.M., Gray, J.A. & Olson, D.E. (2018). Psychedelics Promote Structural and Functional Neural Plasticity. *Cell Reports*, 23(11), 3170–3182. DOI: 10.1016/j.celrep.2018.05.022

## Abstract

Atrophy of neurons in the prefrontal cortex (PFC) plays a key role in the pathophysiology of depression and related disorders. The ability to promote both structural and functional plasticity in the PFC has been hypothesised to underlie the rapid and sustained antidepressant effects of the dissociative anaesthetic ketamine. Here, the authors report that serotonergic psychedelics — LSD, DMT, DOI, and psilocin — as well as the entactogen MDMA and the iboga alkaloid noribogaine are capable of robustly increasing neuritogenesis and/or spinogenesis both in vitro and in vivo. These cellular effects appear to result from stimulation of the TrkB, mTOR, and 5-HT₂A signalling pathways and could underlie the clinical effectiveness of these compounds. The authors coined the term "psychoplastogen" to describe this class of plasticity-promoting small molecules.

## Key Findings

### Neuritogenesis
All tested psychedelics increased dendritic arbor complexity in cultured rat cortical neurons (DIV6). LSD demonstrated extraordinary potency with an EC₅₀ of 0.409 nM, active across 8 orders of magnitude (picomolar to micromolar). Ketamine EC₅₀ = 132 nM. Effects were conserved across vertebrate (rat cortical neurons) and invertebrate (Drosophila class I da neurons) models, suggesting an evolutionarily conserved mechanism.

**Potency ranking (neuritogenesis):** LSD > DOI > psilocin > DMT > MDMA > noribogaine ≈ ketamine >> ibogaine (inactive)

### Spinogenesis
DMT, LSD, and DOI increased dendritic spine density in vitro (DIV19 cortical cultures). In vivo validation: DMT 10 mg/kg produced spine density increases in rat PFC layer V pyramidal neurons at 24 hr, comparable to ketamine 10 mg/kg. Both compounds shifted spine morphology toward immature types (thin/filopodium) rather than mature (mushroom) spines.

### Synaptogenesis
Psychedelics increased synapse number (not size), with elevated VGLUT1 puncta density. DMT 10 µM increased VGLUT1 density by approximately 50% in vitro.

### Functional Plasticity
DMT increased both frequency and amplitude of spontaneous excitatory postsynaptic currents (sEPSCs) in layer V pyramidal neurons at 24 hr post-treatment, at both hallucinogenic (10 mg/kg) and subhallucinogenic (1 mg/kg) doses.

### Ibogaine vs Noribogaine Dissociation
A critical finding for ibogaine research: ibogaine itself showed no neuritogenic activity at any concentration tested. However, its primary metabolite noribogaine was active, with an EC₅₀ comparable to ketamine. This suggests ibogaine's neuroplastic effects in vivo are mediated entirely through its metabolite, consistent with noribogaine's longer half-life and known neurotrophic activity.

## Methodology

**In vitro:** Rat embryonic cortical neuron cultures (E18 Sprague-Dawley). Neuritogenesis assessed at DIV6 (Sholl analysis), spinogenesis at DIV19. Compounds tested: LSD, DMT, DOI, psilocin, MDMA, noribogaine, ibogaine, ketamine, BDNF (50 ng/mL positive control).

**In vivo:** Male Sprague-Dawley rats. DMT (1 mg/kg, 10 mg/kg) and ketamine (10 mg/kg) administered intraperitoneally. PFC spine density assessed at 24 hr via DiI labelling and confocal imaging. Electrophysiology: whole-cell patch clamp recordings of layer V pyramidal neurons in acute PFC slices.

**Invertebrate model:** Drosophila melanogaster class I da neurons (ddaE) expressing CD4-tdGFP. LSD, DMT, ibogaine, and noribogaine tested to assess evolutionary conservation.

**Pharmacological interrogation:** ANA-12 (TrkB antagonist), rapamycin (mTOR inhibitor), ketanserin (5-HT₂A antagonist) used to dissect signalling requirements.

## Signalling Mechanisms

Effects are mediated through convergent TrkB/mTOR/5-HT₂A signalling:

- **TrkB pathway:** ANA-12 (TrkB antagonist) completely blocked DMT-induced neuritogenesis. BDNF protein levels increased ~2-fold (not statistically significant), while BDNF mRNA was unchanged — suggesting a post-transcriptional mechanism.
- **mTOR pathway:** Rapamycin blocked psychedelic-induced neuritogenesis. mTOR signalling is a known convergence point for plasticity-promoting compounds including ketamine.
- **5-HT₂A receptor:** Ketanserin blocked psychedelic-induced neuritogenesis. The 5-HT₂A receptor appears necessary for the plasticity effects of serotonergic psychedelics, linking the psychedelic experience to the neuroplastic mechanism.

The authors propose that psychedelics activate 5-HT₂A receptors, which signal through TrkB and mTOR to promote rapid structural plasticity — analogous to ketamine's action through NMDA receptor blockade converging on the same downstream pathways.

## Clinical Implications

**For clinical practice:**

1. **Ibogaine/noribogaine dissociation:** Ibogaine's neuroplastic effects depend entirely on metabolism to noribogaine. Patients who are CYP2D6 poor metabolisers may generate less noribogaine, potentially reducing neuroplastic benefit alongside the established pharmacokinetic implications for cardiac safety. This reinforces the importance of CYP2D6 phenotyping in pre-treatment screening.

2. **Mechanistic rationale for sustained effects:** The structural plasticity data (new spines, increased synapse density) provides a biological substrate for the sustained therapeutic effects observed clinically after ibogaine treatment. Unlike acute pharmacological effects that dissipate with drug clearance, structural remodelling of PFC circuits can persist — explaining why a single ibogaine session can produce weeks-to-months of benefit.

3. **Subhallucinogenic doses retain plasticity:** DMT at 1 mg/kg (subhallucinogenic) still produced functional plasticity (increased sEPSC frequency and amplitude). This supports the possibility that neuroplastic benefit may not require the full visionary experience, relevant to discussions about flood-dose vs lower-dose protocols.

4. **Noribogaine's dual role:** Noribogaine contributes both the sustained opioid withdrawal management (via µ/κ-opioid activity and SERT inhibition) and the neuroplastic remodelling that may underlie long-term recovery. The metabolite is doing more therapeutic work than the parent compound across multiple domains.

## Limitations

- Primary data in rodent cortical neurons (in vitro) — direct translation to human PFC requires caution.
- Ibogaine/noribogaine tested only for neuritogenesis, not spinogenesis or synaptogenesis in this study.
- BDNF protein increase was ~2-fold but not statistically significant — the neurotrophic signalling picture may be incomplete.
- In vivo spine data limited to 24 hr timepoint; persistence of structural changes unknown.
- No dose-response for in vivo ibogaine/noribogaine (only in vitro EC₅₀ data).
- Functional plasticity (electrophysiology) tested only for DMT, not ibogaine/noribogaine.
