# Ibogaine Detoxification Transitions Opioid and Cocaine Abusers Between Dependence and Abstinence: Clinical Observations and Treatment Outcomes

**Citation:** Mash, D. C., Duque, L., Page, B., & Allen-Ferdinand, K. (2018). Ibogaine Detoxification Transitions Opioid and Cocaine Abusers Between Dependence and Abstinence: Clinical Observations and Treatment Outcomes. *Frontiers in Pharmacology*, 9, 529. https://doi.org/10.3389/fphar.2018.00529

## Abstract

Ibogaine may be effective for transitioning opioid and cocaine dependent individuals to sobriety. American and European self-help groups provided public testimonials that ibogaine alleviated drug craving and opioid withdrawal symptoms after only a single dose administration. Preclinical studies in animal models of addiction have provided proof-of-concept evidence in support of these claims. However, the purported therapeutic benefits of ibogaine are based on anecdotal reports from a small series of case reports that used retrospective recruitment procedures. We reviewed clinical results from an open label case series (N = 191) of human volunteers seeking to detoxify from opioids or cocaine with medical monitoring during inpatient treatment. Whole blood was assayed to obtain pharmacokinetic measures to determine the metabolism and clearance of ibogaine. Clinical safety data and adverse events (AEs) were studied in male and female subjects. There were no significant adverse events following administration of ibogaine in a dose range that was shown to be effective for blocking opioid withdrawal symptoms in this study. We used multi-dimensional craving questionnaires during inpatient detoxification to test if ibogaine was effective in diminishing heroin and cocaine cravings. Participants also completed standardized questionnaires about their health and mood before and after ibogaine treatment, and at program discharge. One-month follow-up data were reviewed where available to determine if ibogaine's effects on drug craving would persist outside of an inpatient setting. We report here that ibogaine therapy administered in a safe dose range diminishes opioid withdrawal symptoms and reduces drug cravings. Pharmacological treatments for opioid dependence include detoxification, narcotic antagonists and long-term opioid maintenance therapy. Our results support product development of single oral dose administration of ibogaine for the treatment of opioid withdrawal during medically supervised detoxification to transition drug dependent individuals to abstinence.

## Key Findings

This study represents the largest published clinical dataset on ibogaine treatment for substance dependence, drawing from Mash's St. Kitts programme (Healing Visions) which operated as a 12-day inpatient detoxification facility. The cohort of 191 patients — 102 opioid-dependent and 89 cocaine-dependent — received oral ibogaine HCl at 8–12 mg/kg under medical supervision with continuous ECG telemetry for 24 hours post-dose.

Ibogaine produced rapid and statistically robust reductions across all craving dimensions. Opioid-dependent participants showed significant decreases on all four HCQ-29 subscales (emotionality, purposefulness, compulsivity, and expectancy; all p < 0.0001), with effects persisting at one-month follow-up. Cocaine-dependent participants demonstrated comparable reductions on the CCQ-45 and the Minnesota Cocaine Craving Scale, where craving intensity dropped from 5.51 to 1.47 at discharge (F = 56.35, p < 0.0001) and craving frequency from 2.28 to 0.29 (F = 46.42, p < 0.0001).

Depression severity showed marked improvement across three validated instruments. In the opioid group, BDI-II total scores declined from 16.5 pre-treatment to 8.9 at discharge and 4.5 at one-month follow-up (F = 29.79, p < 0.0001). POMS and SCL-90-R depression subscales confirmed this trajectory. The cocaine group showed even more rapid mood improvement, with BDI-II scores falling from 14.3 to 4.2 at discharge (F = 36.86, p < 0.0001).

No serious adverse events occurred. Common side effects were nausea, vomiting, and gait ataxia during the acute drug phase. Orthostatic hypotension occurred in 5% of subjects, predominantly among cocaine-dependent participants — attributed by the consulting cardiologist to volume depletion from cocaine use rather than direct ibogaine cardiotoxicity. This led to routine pre-dosing intravenous fluid administration for all patients.

Pharmacokinetic profiling of 20 patients stratified by CYP2D6 genotype revealed that ibogaine undergoes rapid first-pass metabolism to noribogaine, with ibogaine Tmax of 0.5–4 hours and elimination half-life of 1.6–6 hours. CYP2D6 intermediate metabolisers (wt/4) showed higher ibogaine Cmax values but lower noribogaine levels compared to fast metabolisers (wt/wt), who generated substantially higher noribogaine concentrations (up to 1,606 ng/ml). Physician-rated OOWS scores dropped markedly post-treatment across all genotypes, with most patients scoring 0–2 at 36 hours post-dose against pre-dose baselines of 3–13.

## Methodology

Open-label observational case series conducted at a 12-bed freestanding treatment facility in St. Kitts, West Indies. Participants (N = 191; 144 male, 47 female) were self-referred treatment seekers meeting DSM-IV criteria for opioid or cocaine dependence, confirmed by positive urine screens. Pre-treatment screening included physician examination, clinical laboratory testing, 12-lead ECG, and SCID-I psychiatric assessment. Participants received single oral doses of ibogaine HCl (8–12 mg/kg) with continuous ECG telemetry monitoring for 24 hours. Opioid-dependent patients were bridged to oral morphine sulfate prior to ibogaine administration. Outcome measures included the Objective Opiate Withdrawal Scale (OOWS), Heroin Craving Questionnaire (HCQ-29), Cocaine Craving Questionnaire (CCQ-45), Minnesota Cocaine Craving Scale, Beck Depression Inventory (BDI-II), Profile of Moods (POMS), and SCL-90-R. Assessments were conducted pre-treatment, at programme discharge, and at one-month follow-up where available. CYP2D6 genotyping and ibogaine/noribogaine pharmacokinetics were determined from whole blood samples using validated GC/MS methods. Retrospective chart review was conducted under University of Miami IRB approval (IRB 04-0366). Data analysis used repeated-measures mixed-model ANOVA with compound symmetric covariance (SAS); significance criterion was p ≤ 0.05, two-tailed.

The craving reduction pattern distinguished between the two substance groups in a clinically informative way. Both groups showed craving reductions that maintained or deepened at one-month follow-up, though sample attrition was substantial (37 of 75 for opioid; 32 of 81 for cocaine).

The genotype-stratified PK data reveal a clinically significant pattern: intermediate metabolisers (wt/4) retained higher ibogaine levels with longer half-lives (up to 6.1 hours) but generated lower peak noribogaine concentrations. Fast metabolisers (wt/wt) showed the inverse — rapid ibogaine clearance with correspondingly higher noribogaine exposure. Two ultra-rapid metabolisers (M5 and F2) produced ibogaine levels below detection limits and reported no oneirophrenic (dream-like) effects, suggesting that the visionary experience is mediated primarily by ibogaine itself rather than noribogaine. Despite this pharmacokinetic variability, OOWS scores were reduced across all genotypes, suggesting that even varied metabolic profiles can produce therapeutic withdrawal blockade — though the mechanisms of benefit may differ between genotype groups.

The ibogaine half-life range of 1.6–6 hours across this cohort, combined with noribogaine's substantially longer elimination, supports the clinical observation that ibogaine's acute psychoactive effects resolve within 6–12 hours while its anti-withdrawal and anti-craving effects persist for days to weeks. This pro-drug pharmacology has direct implications for dosing strategy and safety monitoring timelines.

A notable divergence between substance groups emerged in the capacity to resist or control the experience: 40.0% of cocaine-dependent participants reported this ability versus only 16.7% of opioid-dependent participants. This asymmetry may reflect differences in the acute neurochemical milieu — opioid-dependent participants were undergoing active withdrawal during ibogaine administration, whereas cocaine-dependent participants were in a different physiological state. The remarkably low rate of willingness to repeat the experience (16.7%) — despite 91.7% finding it therapeutically useful — is consistent with the broader ibogaine literature and distinguishes ibogaine from substances with abuse liability.

## Clinical Implications

This study provides the most comprehensive quantitative dataset supporting ibogaine's efficacy for both opioid and cocaine detoxification from a single clinical programme. Several findings carry direct clinical relevance:

The dose range of 8–12 mg/kg ibogaine HCl (500–1000 mg absolute) was effective for withdrawal blockade and craving reduction without serious adverse events. This is substantially lower than the 15–25 mg/kg range sometimes used in unregulated settings, suggesting that effective detoxification does not require the higher doses associated with greater cardiac risk (see [Alper2012_Ibogaine_Fatalities](../2012/Alper2012_Ibogaine_Fatalities.md)).

The morphine bridging protocol — switching opioid-dependent patients to oral morphine sulfate at programme entry — provided standardised withdrawal management before ibogaine administration. This anticipates the systematic pre-treatment stabilisation protocols later codified in [GITA2015_Clinical_Guidelines](../Clinical_Guidelines/GITA2015_Clinical_Guidelines.md).


The CYP2D6 genotype-stratified pharmacokinetic data carry significant implications for individualised dosing. The observation that ultra-rapid metabolisers experienced minimal ibogaine exposure and no oneirophrenic effects — while intermediate metabolisers sustained higher parent drug levels for longer — argues for pre-treatment CYP2D6 genotyping as a safety and efficacy measure. This data directly informed later pharmacogenomic recommendations (see [Glue2015_Ibogaine_CYP2D6_Activity](../2015/Glue2015_Ibogaine_CYP2D6_Activity.md)).

The routine pre-dosing IV fluid administration protocol, adopted after observing orthostatic hypotension in cocaine-dependent participants, represents an early example of iterative protocol refinement based on real-world clinical observation — the kind of practice-based learning that later programmes have formalised into standardised operating procedures.

The high rates of psychiatric comorbidity across both cohorts (52.9% depressive disorders in opioid group; 23.6% bipolar disorder in cocaine group) underscore that ibogaine treatment candidates typically present with complex dual-diagnosis profiles. The rapid antidepressant effects observed here, converging across three validated instruments, suggest ibogaine may offer particular benefit for this comorbid population — though controlled studies remain necessary to isolate the drug's antidepressant effect from the confound of acute substance cessation.

## Limitations

The open-label, uncontrolled design precludes causal conclusions about ibogaine's efficacy — placebo effects, the therapeutic milieu of a 12-day inpatient programme, and natural history of withdrawal cannot be separated from drug effects. Follow-up data were available for only a subset of participants (37 of 75 opioid; 32 of 81 cocaine at one month), introducing potential survivor bias. The predominantly Caucasian, male, well-educated sample (mean 14–15 years of education) limits generalisability. CYP2D6 genotyping was performed on only 20 of 191 participants. The study did not report QTc data despite continuous ECG monitoring, a notable omission given the cardiac safety concerns that dominate the field. Self-report measures are susceptible to demand characteristics, particularly in an inpatient setting where patients may feel motivated to report improvement. As the authors acknowledge, the difficulty in obtaining longitudinal follow-up for substance-dependent individuals treated outside the United States remains a fundamental limitation shared across the ibogaine literature.
