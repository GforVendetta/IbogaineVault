## TABERNANTHE IBOGA: An Endangered Species

* 
**Therapeutic Value:** Ibogaine has been shown to be a widely successful tool in therapy and recovery from drug dependence.

* 
**Demand & Crisis:** Increase in worldwide demand has led to a lucrative black market and over-harvesting *Tabernanthe iboga*, the sacred plant of Gabon.

* 
**Decimation:** Poachers have decimated old growth reserves in Gabon, Cameroon, and neighboring countries.

* 
**Status:** Over-harvesting has rendered *T. iboga* an endangered plant species.

---

## TABERNANTHE IBOGA: An Endangered Species

### *Photograph showing a local harvester crouched next to a Tabernanthe iboga bush in the forest.*

**The Impact of Poaching:**
Poachers have been systematically stripping the forest of iboga to meet the growing demand for Ibogaine, and have made the once plentiful plant scarce.

---

## TABERNANTHE IBOGA: An Endangered Species

* 
**Root Bark Ravaging:** Plant stocks have been ravaged for their root bark by poachers for sale to black and grey market producers.

* 
**Impact on Indigenous Culture:** As a result, root bark prices have skyrocketed and indigenous Bwiti practitioners have difficulty obtaining supplies for tribal use and ritual initiation.

---

## TABERNANTHE IBOGA: An Endangered Species

### *Photograph of a black plastic bag filled with harvested, stripped iboga roots.*

**Pillaged Resources:**
Pillaged root bark for the grey market and the black market.

---

## IBOGA: The Way of Ivory

* 
**Legal Action:** The government of Gabon is currently considering criminalizing international trafficking of Iboga in the same manner as the ivory trade.

* 
**Parallel to Ivory:** Ivory trafficking has become one of the most lucrative illegal trades in Africa due to the rising demand in Asia.

---

## A CALL TO ACTION FOR SUSTAINABLE IBOGAINE

* 
**Responsible Sourcing:** Phytostan urges responsible treatment providers to use products obtained through sustainable agriculture.

* 
**Definition:** Sustainable Ibogaine is harvested, manufactured, and distributed in a safe and eco-friendly manner.

---

## IBOGAINE: Black Market

* 
**Deceptive Marketing:** The black market sells a product it calls "Ibogaine" but is actually a crude extract of iboga.

* 
**Low Purity:** This product is often produced in low-tech labs and contains at least 12 other alkaloids in addition to Ibogaine.

* 
**Safety Risks:** Mis-labeled and impure, this product is readily available on the Internet and has a low safety profile that poses a risk to patients.

## IBOGAINE: Grey Market

* 
**Resource Depletion:** Grey market producers consume large amounts of *T. iboga* as start-up material, which is contributing to the decline of resources.

* 
**Widespread Use:** Treatment providers widely use Ibogaine obtained through grey market sources.

* 
**Not for Humans:** While reasonably pure, the product is not intended for human consumption, but rather animal research only.

## IBOGAINE: U.S. Versus Canada

### United States Classification

* 
**Schedule I:** In the United States, Ibogaine is a Schedule I controlled substance, the most stringent drug classification.

* 
**Definition:** Schedule I drugs are not considered legitimate for medical use and have high potential for abuse.

* 
**Examples:** Schedule I drugs include cocaine, heroin, LSD, marijuana, peyote, ecstasy, and Ibogaine.

---

## IBOGAINE IN THE U.S.

* 
**Research Possibilities:** U.S. researchers who possess a Schedule I site license may apply for an import permit from the U.S. Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA).

* 
**Timeline:** It can take up to 9 months to acquire the DEA import permit.
