## Disease Area & Biological Target

* 
**Disease Area:** Neurological and psychiatric disorders

* 
**Biological Target:** Serotonin and vesicular monoamine transporter 2 (VMAT2)

### Summary

Diseases associated with cognitive decline and/or disability affect more than 55 million people in the world. A loss of neuronal function in disease processes such as Alzheimer’s disease (AD), multiple sclerosis, and Parkinson’s disease (PD) has a high and constantly growing global prevalence.

Although the adult human brain is one of the organs with the lowest regenerative ability, neurogenesis occurs throughout life from neural stem cells. Knockout studies revealed certain growth factors and their downstream signaling pathways, transcriptional regulators, and epigenetic proteins as important mediators in neuro-regeneration.

**The Role of Nurr1:**
The orphan nuclear receptor related 1 (Nurr1) is the second member of the NR4A subfamily and is the most extensively studied NR4A member in the context of neurodegenerative diseases. It is mainly expressed in the central nervous system, with particularly high abundance in mesencephalic dopaminergic neurons of the ventral tegmental area and substantia nigra pars compacta, and the paraventricular thalamic nuclei. Furthermore, Nurr1 is expressed in the midbrain from the early prenatal state to adulthood. It is a key regulator in dopaminergic neuron development, maintaining and regulating genes that are essential factors in dopamine neurotransmission, such as vesicular monoamine transporter 2 (VMAT2), tyrosine hydroxylase, dopamine transporter (DAT), and aromatic L-amino acid decarboxylase.

**The Role of VMAT2:**
The human VMAT2, which is expressed in monoaminergic presynaptic neurons, as well as in peripheral tissues, transports cytosolic dopamine (DA), serotonin (5-HT or SER), or norepinephrine (NE) into storage vesicles. Hence, VMAT2 participates in the regulation of cytosolic levels and vesicular stores of biogenic amines. As such, monoamines such as methamphetamine compete with the endogenous neurotransmitters for binding sites and uptake into VMAT2. When VMAT2 binds methamphetamine, vesicular degradation results in DA efflux into the cytosol of presynaptic neurons. This can cause reverse transport by the DAT to increase DA in the extracellular space. Therefore, VMAT2 has become a possible target for therapeutics to treat methamphetamine abuse. Studies have shown that the dysfunction of VMAT2 can evoke cytoplasmic DA accumulation, which leads to dopaminergic neuronal death. Other studies have shown that protein expression levels of VMAT2 were significantly reduced in PD patients and that an increase in VMAT2 level or function might protect against the development of PD.

## Ibogaine and Analogues

### Background and Mechanism

Ibogaine is the main indole alkaloid isolated from the root bark of the African shrub *Tabernanthe iboga*, which has played dual roles in the lives of the population of West Africa. It was sold as an antidepressant in France for decades before its adverse effects precipitated its removal from the market. Low doses of ibogaine are used as stimulants to prevent fatigue on hunting excursions and to dull hunger and thirst, whereas high doses are used for their hallucinogenic properties during initiation rites and religious rituals.

Anecdotal reports and open label case studies of participants with heroin and cocaine addiction have indicated ibogaine’s ability to interrupt the drug dependence via rapid and lasting relief of drug withdrawal symptoms and cravings. It is an atypical psychedelic drug capable of inducing oneirogenic effects (waking dream-like states) and vivid memory recall and replay.

**Toxicity and Receptor Affinity:**
Ibogaine displays moderate to weak affinities for a wide spectrum of receptor and transporter proteins, which has made it neuro- and cardiotoxic. Ibogaine and its principal active metabolite, noribogaine, bind to the transporters for the monoamines SER, DA, and NE. For example, ibogaine ingestion has been linked to several deaths by cardiac arrest. Nonetheless, recent studies suggest that its actions at nicotinic acetylcholine receptor subtypes may underlie its reputed anti-opiate effects.

**Therapeutic Potential:**
Analogs of ibogaine may also hold promise for treating anxiety and depression via the "psychedelic-assisted therapy" approach that employs hallucinogens such as psilocybin and 3,4-methylenedioxy-methamphetamine (MDMA). Ibogaine is the most abundant of approximately 80 structurally similar alkaloids found in the *T. iboga* plant. The adverse effects of ibogaine have led to the synthesis and pharmacologic screening of chemical congeners in hopes of identifying less toxic anti-addictive compounds.

Unlike LSD, mescaline, and psilocybin, the hallucinogenic properties of ibogaine cannot be ascribed to 5-HT2A receptor activation. Its active metabolite noribogaine does, however, display sub-micromolar kappa opioid receptor affinity (0.61 μM) and partial agonism at dynorphin A (an endogenous opioid peptide that preferentially bind to kappa opioid receptors).

### Patent Highlights and Claims

The present Patent Highlight shows that small structural modifications of ibogaine or noribogaine have large and unexpected effects on pharmacological activity at monoamine transporters. VMAT2 has been shown to be a target for the treatment of substance use disorders. In addition, VMAT2 inhibitors may also be used to treat hyperkinetic disorders such as Tardive dyskinesia, Tourette syndrome, and chorea associated with Huntington’s disease. Dual modulation of SERT and VMAT2 is mechanistically important, as some compounds selectively inhibit SERT or VMAT2 or simultaneously both.

**WO 2022/003675 A1 Claims:**
The disclosure relates to a combination of ibogaine and an antidepressant drug for use in treating psychiatric disorders, such as depression.

* 
**Standard Treatments:** Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs) are considered the current standard, including fluoxetine (Prozac), citalopram (Celexa), paroxetine (Paxil, Seroxat), sertraline (Zoloft), and escitalopram (Lexapro). Monoamine oxidase inhibitors (e.g., Nardil) block the enzyme monoamine oxidase and may be used if other medications are ineffective.

**WO 2022/020352 A1 Claims:**
This disclosure provides pharmaceutical compositions and methods for treating psychiatric diseases and disorders, in particular depression.

* 
**Compositions:** Comprise ibogaine or derivatives and at least one antidepressant.

* 
**Synergy:** The combination produces a synergistic effect in reducing symptoms of bipolar disorder, depression, schizophrenia, paranoia, anxiety, panic disorder, mania, PTSD, and OCD. Together they provide highly efficient therapeutic responses compared to when each agent is used alone.

**Animal & Clinical Models:**

* 
**Animal Testing:** Safety and efficacy were tested in animal models (rats or mice) of depression, obesity, PTSD, and anxiety. Animals were treated with antidepressants (NE-DA reuptake inhibitors or SSRIs) alone or in combination with ibogaine (0.01−4 mg/kg i.p.), ibogaine alone, or saline, once daily for 2–60 days.

* 
**Clinical Trial:** A prospective, double-blind, active-controlled, randomized clinical trial of 4 weeks was conducted in outpatients with unipolar depression. Patients were given 1, 5, 10, or 20 mg of Bupropion or Prozac daily, alone or in combination with 0.01−2.0 mg/kg ibogaine. Efficacy is determined by evaluating short-term effects in treating acute unipolar depression.
